# Magic — SR5 Core Rulebook

**Source:** SR5 Core Rulebook
**Book Pages:** 276–327
**PDF Pages:** 278–329

---

## Overview

Magic in Shadowrun 5th Edition is governed by the Special Attribute called Magic, channeled through three skill categories: Sorcery (spellcasting, counterspelling, ritual spellcasting), Conjuring (summoning, binding, banishing), and Enchanting (alchemy, artificing, disenchanting). Awakened characters are either full magicians (who cast spells and conjure spirits) or adepts (who channel mana into physical enhancement via Power Points). The magic system is unified across all traditions; traditions affect only which spirit types are available, how elements map to spell/spirit categories, and which Attributes form the Drain Resistance dice pool.

## Rules

### Magic Attribute

Magic is a Special Attribute with a starting value of 1–6 (or 7 with the Exceptional Attribute quality). It is only present if the Priority table provides it. Every point (or fraction) of Essence lost reduces both the current Magic Attribute and its maximum by 1. If Magic reaches 0, all magical abilities including astral perception and projection are permanently lost ("burned out"). The maximum Magic value is 6 + Initiation grade.

### Inherent Limit: Astral

The Astral limit equals the higher of the character's Mental limit or Social limit. It applies to tests made in astral space.

### Drain

Drain is the cost of using magic. It is always Stun damage unless specific conditions make it Physical:

- If the number of hits (not net hits) on the Spellcasting Test **after applying the limit** exceeds the caster's Magic rating, Drain is Physical.
- For Conjuring, Drain Value = twice the hits (not net hits) on the spirit's defense test; minimum Drain Value 2. If the spirit's Force > caster's Magic, Drain is Physical.
- For Ritual Spellcasting, Drain = twice the net hits on the defense side of the Ritual Spellcasting Test (minimum 2); Physical if the leader's Teamwork hits exceed her Magic rating.
- Drain is resisted with the tradition's Drain Resistance dice pool (e.g., Logic + Willpower for Hermetic Mage; Charisma + Willpower for Shaman).
- Drain damage cannot be healed by any means other than natural rest (no magical healing, no medkits).

### Power Points

Power Points (PP) are the adept resource for purchasing powers. At character creation, adepts receive PP equal to their Magic attribute. Mystic adepts must buy PP with Karma. Each time an adept's Magic attribute increases, they gain 1 free PP (not applicable to mystic adepts). If Magic is reduced, PP must be "un-bought" to compensate for the loss. If Magic reaches 0, all mystic abilities are cut off.

### Traditions

A tradition defines: the five element-to-spell-category mappings, the available spirit types, and the Drain Resistance Test attributes. All traditions use the same mechanical rules for spellcasting and conjuring.

- **Hermetic Mage:** Combat = Fire, Detection = Air, Health = Man, Illusion = Water, Manipulation = Earth. Drain: Logic + Willpower. Views spirits as inferior servants; prefers binding.
- **Shaman:** Combat = Beasts, Detection = Water, Health = Earth, Illusion = Air, Manipulation = Man. Drain: Charisma + Willpower. Views spirits as ancient peers; prefers calling them as needed.

> **Cross-reference:** Additional traditions are defined in Run Faster. The tradition determines which 5 spirit types are available, not the spell list.

### Magical Lodges

A magical lodge is a stationary collection of tradition-appropriate symbols, writings, tools, fetishes, and mystical materials used to learn spells, perform rituals, and craft foci. Cost: Force × 500 nuyen. Requires a number of days equal to the lodge's Force to set up. An active magical lodge acts as a mana barrier (p. 315) and carries the owner's astral signature. Moving a lodge takes 1 day to disassemble; setup requires another full period at the new location. Temporary lodges can be created from reagents (see Reagents). A lodge can be improved by adding materials and spending 1 day times the Force increase desired.

### Perceiving Magic

Noticing magic in the environment is a Simple Perception + Intuition [Mental] Test with a threshold equal to the Skill Rating of the being performing it minus the Force of the magic, or (6 − Force) if no skill is involved; minimum threshold 1 in either case. Characters with any magic-related Active or Knowledge Skill add +2 dice to this test.

## Sorcery

### Spellcasting — Step 1: Choose Spell

Magicians purchase a starting spell list at character creation and add more through advancement. Only learned spells may be cast. Up to Magic attribute spells may be cast simultaneously in one action by splitting Spellcasting + Magic dice pool (minimum 1 die per spell) among them; modifiers are applied after splitting.

### Spellcasting — Step 2: Choose Target

Target must be perceivable to establish the mystical link. Line of Sight (LOS) spells require the caster to see the target, including via reflective surfaces and transparent materials. Cyber/bioware visual enhancements paid for with Essence count as natural. Technological visual aids (cameras, AR feeds) do not establish LOS. Touch (T) spells require physical contact; an unarmed attack roll is needed against an unwilling target. Magicians on the physical plane can only target physical-plane targets; magicians on the astral plane can only target astral targets (except mana spells, which work on any target the caster astrally perceives). Area spells affect a sphere centered on the target/point with radius in meters equal to the spell's Force; only targets the caster can see are affected.

### Spellcasting — Step 3: Choose Spell Force

Declare Force before casting; maximum = twice the caster's Magic rating. If hits (not net hits) after applying the limit exceed Magic, Drain is Physical instead of Stun.

### Spellcasting — Step 4: Cast Spell

Make a Spellcasting + Magic [Force] Test as a Complex Action (includes dice pool modifiers for injury, sustained spells, etc.). Net hits determine effect. If hits after applying the limit exceed the caster's Magic rating, Drain is Physical.

**Reckless Spellcasting:** Cast as a Simple Action (not Complex Action) but adds +3 to the Drain Value. If two spells are cast recklessly in the same Action Phase, both receive +3 Drain Value.

### Spellcasting — Step 5: Determine Effect

Each spell's description lists the effect and any Opposed Tests needed. Effects happen regardless of whether Drain takes the caster out. Some spells allow targets to dodge (p. 182 for ranged combat rules).

### Spellcasting — Step 6: Resist Drain

Drain Value is listed with each spell's formula (based on Force F), minimum 2. Resist using the tradition's Drain Resistance dice pool. Each hit reduces Drain by 1. Remaining Drain is applied as boxes to the Stun (or Physical) Condition Monitor.

### Spellcasting — Step 7: Determine Ongoing Effects

Sustained spells impose a −2 dice pool penalty on all tests while maintained. Multiple sustained spells stack this penalty cumulatively (−2 per spell). Area sustained spells can be moved as a Complex Action if the area remains in LOS. Characters leaving an area spell's zone are no longer affected; new characters entering must resist the original Spellcasting Test result.

### Spellcasting Glitches

A glitch generally adds +2 Drain Value; may invoke wrong elemental effect, hit wrong target, or cause other mischief. A critical glitch can cause the spell to go out of control (no Drain resistance, uncontrolled effect).

### Spell Characteristics

Each spell has the following stats:

- **Type:** P (Physical) or M (Mana). Physical spells create tangible energy/matter and affect both living and non-living physical targets; they are temporary unless sustained. Mana spells can only affect living and magical/astrally active entities; they work on either plane if the caster is on the same plane as the target.
- **Range:** LOS (line of sight), T (touch), or LOS (A) for area.
- **Duration:** I (Instantaneous), S (Sustained), P (Permanent).
- **Damage:** P (Physical) or S (Stun), for spells that deal damage.
- **Drain:** Formula based on Force (F). Minimum Drain Value is always 2.

### Combat Spells

Combat spells deal damage via direct or indirect means.

**Direct:** Net hits on the Spellcasting Test equal the number of damage boxes inflicted. Opposed by Body (Physical spells) or Willpower (Mana spells). Target does not get to resist or dodge.

**Indirect:** Opposed Test: Spellcasting + Magic [Force] vs. target's Reaction + Intuition. Damage Value = Force + net hits; AP = −Force. Resisted with Body + Armor (adjusted for AP). Area indirect spells travel to a point and detonate; treat as grenades (p. 181): Spellcasting + Magic [Force] (3) test with 2D6 meter scatter. Net hits on the Spellcasting test add to Damage Value only if threshold is met; otherwise hits reduce scatter by 1 meter per hit.

**Elemental:** Cause special damage types (see Special Damage Types, p. 170); type listed in spell description.

## Counterspelling

### Spell Defense

Declare spell defense as a Free Action (or Interrupt Action, −5 from Initiative Score if no Free Action remains). Each Combat Turn, the character has a pool of dice equal to their Counterspelling skill for spell defense. These dice can protect a number of people up to the caster's Magic Rating simultaneously (including self). Allocate dice among protected targets; those dice are added to each protected person's Resistance Test against any incoming spell. Pool refreshes at start of each Combat Turn.

### Dispelling

Dispelling counters a sustained or quickened spell. Test: Counterspelling + Magic [Astral] vs. spell's Force + caster's Magic (+ Karma spent quickening, if any). Counterspelling foci may add dice. Each net hit reduces the number of hits the caster had when casting by 1 (potentially reducing the spell's effectiveness to 0 hits = spell ends). Regardless of the outcome, the dispeller takes Drain equal to twice the hits (not net hits) on the opposing test (minimum 2). Drain is Physical if the spell's Force > dispeller's Magic; otherwise Stun. Counterspelling skill does not work against spirits, critter powers, or alchemical preparations.

**Dispelling a Ritual:** Ritual must contain a spell keyword and be ongoing (sustained or duration). Make Opposed Counterspelling + Magic [Astral] against a dice pool equal to the sum of the ritual's Force and the total Magic ratings of all participants. Each net hit reduces the ritual's net hits from the sealing step by 1.

## Ritual Spellcasting

Ritual spellcasting manipulates mana over a longer period. Rituals are learned separately (5 Karma each, same as spells). Spells learned through normal channels can be incorporated into some rituals (e.g., Combat spell in Prodigal Spell, p. 297) at no extra Karma.

### Step 1: Choose Ritual Leader

The leader must know the ritual and seals it in Step 7. Participants announce their participation. Non-same-tradition participants take −2 dice pool penalty on all ritual actions. Solo casting: the leader is also the leader alone.

### Step 2: Choose Ritual

Leader chooses the ritual. If a spell is required, only the leader needs to know it (or the spell category).

### Step 3: Choose Force of the Ritual Spell

Force determines the ritual's power and the Force of any spell woven into it.

### Step 4: Set Up the Foundation

Ritual must be performed in a magical lodge (permanent or temporary from reagents) of the leader's tradition with Force ≥ ritual's Force. Once active, no participant may leave without failing the ritual — except a **spotter** if the ritual calls for one.

### Step 5: Give the Offering

Leader expends reagents equal to Force. Additional reagents may be spent to reduce Drain when sealing (Step 7): every (Force) drams beyond the initial offering reduces the Drain Value by 1, to a minimum of 2.

### Step 6: Perform the Ritual

Duration is specific to each ritual, almost always based on Force.

### Step 7: Seal the Ritual

Leader makes a Ritual Spellcasting + Magic [Force] vs. (Force × 2) Test with a Teamwork Test from participants. The net hits from sealing determine the ritual's effect (per the ritual description). After the effect, each participant takes Drain equal to twice the hits (not net hits) on the defense side of the Ritual Spellcasting Test (minimum 2). Drain is Physical if the leader's Teamwork hits exceed her Magic rating.

### Ritual Failure

A ritual fails if: a participant leaves the foundation after Step 4 but before completion; the leader is incapacitated or killed before Step 7; or the foundation is disrupted/broken after Step 4. On failure, every participant makes a Force × 2 test and takes Drain equal to twice the net hits as Stun damage. Any reagents already spent are lost.

### Ritual Keywords

- **Anchored:** Must be placed on a physical object/symbol; anchor cannot move relative to the Gaiasphere during the ritual or the effect collapses.
- **Material Link:** Requires something that was once part of the target (hair, blood, nail clippings viable for a few hours; tissue samples last a few days; chemical preservation destroys viability; can be frozen). Used up during the ritual offering step.
- **Minion:** Creates a semi-autonomous entity (watcher or homunculus) bound to the leader (max = Charisma).
- **Spell:** Uses a known spell as part of the ritual; leader's mentor spirit modifiers apply.
- **Spotter:** If target is not in LOS of the leader, a spotter (group member or bound spirit) who can astrally perceive the target is required. Spotter must be present at foundation at the start, then travels to the target's location; target need not be astrally active.

## Conjuring

### Summoning

Summoning a spirit is a Complex Action. Only one spirit can be summoned at a time. A summoned spirit returns to its home metaplane when it has fulfilled all services owed or when the sun rises/sets. Max spirit Force = twice the summoner's Magic. Optional powers: 1 per full 3 points of Force (Force 1–2: 0 optional powers; Force 3–5: 1; Force 6–8: 2; etc.).

**Step 1: Choose Spirit Type & Force**
Choose from tradition's available spirit types (5 types per tradition). Force ≤ 2 × caster's Magic.

**Step 2: Attempt Summoning**
Opposed Test: Summoning + Magic [Force] vs. spirit's Force. Reagents may change the limit. If no net hits, spirit does not appear. Net hits = services owed (one per net hit).

**Step 3: Resist Drain**
Drain Value = 2 × hits (not net hits) on spirit's defense test; minimum Drain Value 2. Physical if spirit's Force > caster's Magic.

**Group Summoning:** All members must be able to summon that spirit type; different-tradition members take −2 dice pool penalty. Teamwork rules apply; all members take Drain (twice the hits on the defense test). Only the leader may command the spirit.

### Binding

Binding compels long-term service from an already-summoned spirit. Takes 1 hour per Force of the spirit. Costs Force × 25 drams of reagents. Opposed Test: Binding + Magic [Force] vs. spirit's Force × 2. Each additional net hit beyond the first adds to the services owed. Drain = twice the hits (not net hits) on the spirit's defense test, minimum 2. Physical if spirit's Force > Magic. A magician can have up to Charisma bound spirits.

### Banishing

Banishing severs the bond between a spirit and its summoner. Complex Action. Opposed Test: Banishing + Magic [Astral] vs. spirit's Force (+ summoner's Magic if the spirit is bound). Reagents may change the limit. Each net hit reduces services owed by 1; if services reach 0, the spirit is free and departs. Drain = twice the hits (not net hits) on spirit's defense test, minimum Drain Value 2. Physical if spirit's Force > caster's Magic.

### Spirit Services

Services are consumed one task at a time. A service is a single requested task.

**Unbound Spirit Services:**

- **Combat:** One combat counts as one service.
- **Power Use:** Using a power on a target or targets; sustained power = one service regardless of duration (unless used as part of another task).
- **Physical Task:** Spirit materializes to perform physical actions.
- **Remote Service:** Task that sends spirit beyond Magic × 100 meters; spirit is released after completing it.

**Bound Spirit Services (includes all unbound services plus):**

- **Aid Alchemy/Sorcery/Study:** Spirit adds Force dice to Alchemy, Spellcasting, Ritual Spellcasting, or Learning Tests (if spirit type matches spell's tradition element).
- **Spell Sustaining:** Spirit sustains a spell after it's cast; takes the −2 penalty instead. Lasts only Force Combat Turns per service.
- **Spell Binding:** Spirit sustains spell indefinitely, but spirit's Force is irrevocably reduced by 1 each day (or part thereof). If Force reaches 0, spirit dissipates permanently.

**Spirit Range:** Spirit cannot move farther than Magic × 100 meters from summoner. If forced out of this range, it returns as quickly as possible. Sending beyond this range counts as a Remote Service.

**Spirit-Summoner Link:** Telepathic communication regardless of distance or plane; summoner knows when a summoned spirit is disrupted.

### Spirit Basics

- Spirits are naturally astral forms; they materialize to enter the physical plane.
- When materialized, spirits are **dual-natured** (exist simultaneously on physical and astral planes).
- Physical attributes when materialized are based on spirit type (see Spirit stats tables).
- Movement on physical plane: Walking = Agility × 2; Running = Agility × 4. Use Running skill to sprint (+2 meters per hit).
- Optional powers: 1 per full 3 Force points.
- Spirits do not have their own Edge pools.

## Enchanting

### Alchemy

Alchemy allows creating alchemical preparations — objects that hold a spell for later release. Preparations are less powerful than direct spells but versatile and storable.

**Step 1: Choose Spell** — Choose the alchemical version of a known Sorcery spell (learned separately, same Karma cost).

**Step 2: Choose Spell Force** — Max = twice Magic rating.

**Step 3: Choose Lynchpin** — Small enough to lift and manipulate; must not already have a magical aura (no living beings). High-tech objects may have Object Resistance.

**Step 4: Choose Preparation Trigger**

- **Command:** Caster activates it (must be on physical plane or manifesting, have LOS, takes Simple Action; +2 Drain, only trigger that allows healing spells).
- **Contact:** Next living being to touch it activates it (+1 Drain).
- **Time:** Countdown set during creation; limited by final Potency in hours (+2 Drain).

**Step 5: Create the Preparation** — Spend uninterrupted time equal to Force in minutes. Then make Alchemy + Magic [Force] opposed by the Force of the preparation. Net hits = the preparation's Potency.

**Step 6: Resist Drain** — Drain = Drain Value of the spell plus any trigger modifier. Physical if Potency (hits) > Magic rating.

**Finished Preparation:** Potency = net hits on creation test. If Potency > 0, it functions for Potency × 2 hours at full Potency. After that period, Potency decreases by 1 per hour. When Potency reaches 0 or the lynchpin is broken, the spell is lost.

**Using a Preparation:** When activated, uses Potency in place of Spellcasting dice pool and in place of Magic (also serving as limit). No Drain on the activating character. Targets must be on the physical plane; range is determined by the trigger type. No Edge may be spent on this "spellcasting."

### Artificing

Artificing creates magical foci — permanent, reusable, owner-bonded magical items.

**Step 1: Choose Focus Formula** — Design or purchase a focus formula (Arcana skill). Researching a focus formula: Arcana + Magic [Astral] (Force × Force, 1 day) Extended Test. Translating from another tradition: Arcana + Magic [Astral] (Force, 1 day) Extended Test. Focus formula must be for Force ≤ Magic rating.

**Step 2: Obtain the Telesma** — An inanimate object matching the focus formula's form specifications.

**Step 3: Prepare the Magical Lodge** — Must be same tradition as focus formula and Force ≥ formula Force.

**Step 4: Spend Reagents** — Amount = Karma that would be required to bond the focus (Force drams minimum), expended at start (lost if crafting fails or is aborted).

**Step 5: Craft the Focus** — Spend days equal to formula Force gathering mana into the telesma. At end: Artificing + Magic [formula Force] vs. formula Force + telesma's Object Resistance. Net hits = focus's actual Force. Cannot use Edge. On critical glitch: loses 1 Essence.

**Step 6: Resist Drain** — Drain Value = formula Force + 2 per hit (not net hit) rolled against the magician. Physical if actual Force > Magic. On success, spend Karma equal to actual Force to seal and finish the focus.

**Artifact Assensing:** Opposed Artificing + Magic [Astral] vs. twice the focus's Force. Net hits give information per Assensing Table (p. 313). Once per day per focus.

### Disenchanting

**Deactivating a focus:** Opposed Disenchanting + Magic [Astral] vs. target's Force + owner's Magic. From physical or astral plane with LOS. More successes = focus deactivated.

**Breaking down a focus (touching it):** Opposed Disenchanting + Magic [Astral] vs. target's Force (+ owner's Magic if bonded and focus not yours). Success: focus destroyed; mana recycled — make Alchemy + Magic [Astral] test; each success creates 1 reagent (up to 1/3 of reagents used in Step 4 of Artificing). Takes hours equal to target's Force.

Drain for all Disenchanting tests: 1S per hit (not net hit) rolled against the magician. Physical if target's Force > Magic. Cannot attempt if target's Force > twice the magician's Magic.

### Disjoining

Disjoining removes the magical power from an alchemical preparation (equivalent of Dispelling for alchemy). Requires identifying the object as a preparation via Assensing (p. 142) and touching it. Complex Action. Opposed Disenchanting + Magic [Astral] vs. preparation's Force + alchemist's Magic (+ any Fixation Karma). Each net hit reduces Potency by 1. Caster then takes Drain as if they had created the preparation. Risk: if preparation has a contact trigger and the Disjoining Test fails, the preparation activates.

## The Astral World

### Auras and Astral Forms

Living things cast an aura on the astral plane (vibrant, colorful luminescence). Non-living objects appear as grey shadows. Anything active on the astral plane (spirits, active foci, dual-natured beings) has a tangible astral form.

### Astral Signature

Any magical action leaves an astral signature on affected objects/areas. Signature lasts for hours equal to the magical effect's Force after it ends; foci, lodges, and circles always carry the owner's signature. Read via Assensing + Intuition [Astral] test. A magician can spend a Complex Action to reduce an astral signature's remaining time by 1 hour (no test required); can be done repeatedly. Tampering with a signature is obvious to others Assensing it.

### Astral Perception

Available to magicians and adepts with the Astral Perception power. Activated by Simple Action. Character becomes **dual-natured** (present on both planes simultaneously). Takes −2 dice pool penalty to physical actions while active. Cannot cast spells through Clairvoyance.

### Assensing

Simple Assensing + Intuition [Astral] Test. Hits determine information gained (see Assensing Table). Objects without auras (non-magical, non-living) have no aura to read but may carry impressions from contact with living auras (vague and fleeting).

### Astral Projection

Full magicians only. Body left in a coma-like state. Astral form travels the astral plane. Mind and body remain connected — damage to either is felt by both. Walking rate on astral plane: up to 100 meters per Combat Turn. Running rate: 5 km per Combat Turn. Maximum altitude: ~80 km above Earth's surface. Time limit: Magic × 2 hours; beyond that, astral body ceases to exist and the physical body dies.

**Astral Attributes:**
| Physical Attribute | Astral Attribute |
|---|---|
| Agility | Logic |
| Body | Willpower |
| Reaction | Intuition |
| Strength | Charisma |
| Astra Initiative | Intuition × 2 |
| Initiative Dice | +2D6 (3D6 total) |

**Manifesting:** Purely astral forms can manifest (visible/audible on physical plane) as Complex Action. Can communicate with physical characters. Cannot cast spells on physical targets or be harmed by physical attacks while manifesting. Max manifest time: Magic × 5 minutes total per projection session.

**Staying Astral:** If the session exceeds Magic × 2 hours, the astral body ceases and the physical body dies.

**Astral Detection:** Physical beings sense an astral form passing through them — Perception + Intuition (4) [Mental] test; +2 dice if Awakened. Success = chilly sensation.

### Astral Combat

Astrally perceiving and dual-natured characters use physical Attributes and skills against astral entities with bodies; purely astral projecting characters use mental Attributes (Astral Attributes table). Astral Combat + Willpower for combat.

- **Unarmed astral attack:** Opposed Astral Combat + Willpower [Astral] vs. Intuition + Logic.
- **Weapon focus attack:** Opposed Astral Combat + Willpower [Accuracy] vs. Intuition + Logic.
- **Damage:** Add 1 to DV per net hit. Type = Stun or Physical (attacker's choice). Magician base DV = Charisma; Weapon Focus DV = by weapon (Charisma replaces Strength); Spirit DV = Force; Watcher DV = 1.
- Astral barriers can only be affected by Physical damage.

### Astral Tracking

Nearly all magical things have an astral link to their creator/owner. Following an astral link: Assensing + Intuition (5, 1 hour) [Astral] Extended Test, modified by the Astral Tracking Modifiers table. Mana barriers add their Force to the threshold.

### Mana Barriers

Mana barriers are magical walls that block magic and astral forms. On the physical plane they are invisible but block spells, manifesting entities, spirits, and active foci. Any spell cast through a barrier adds the barrier's Force to the Defense or Resistance dice pool (or turns an unopposed Spellcasting test into an Opposed test against the barrier's Force). On the astral plane, mana barriers are opaque walls stopping astral movement; they are also resistant to astral spells. Dual-natured barriers act on both planes.

Barriers do not affect their creators. Mana barriers have Armor = Force and Structure = Force; they regain full Structure at the start of each Combat Turn while sustained (except Physical Barrier spell which regains Structure). Reduced to Structure 0 = collapses. Passing through subtly: Magic + Charisma [Astral] vs. barrier's Force × 2 Opposed Test; net hits allow passage (can bring friends/sustained spells/astral forms, 1 per net hit). Failing = cannot pass through.

## Initiation

Initiation allows Awakened characters to raise their Magic attribute above 6 and access metamagics. Only characters with a Magic attribute can initiate.

**Cost:** 10 + (Grade × 3) Karma per grade. Initiation is Grade 1, 2, 3, etc. Time: Arcana + Intuition [Astral] (initiate grade, 1 month) Extended Test.

**Benefits:**

- Increased Magic maximum: natural max = 6 + initiate grade.
- Metaplanar Access (first initiation): ability to travel to the metaplanes.
- One metamagic per grade (including first).

**Initiate grade cannot exceed Magic attribute.** If Magic is reduced below the current initiate grade, an initiate grade is lost.

## Reagents and Foci

### Reagents

Reagents are mana-rich materials measured in drams of orichalcum equivalence. Used to:

- Change the limit for Spellcasting (limit = drams spent instead of spell Force).
- Change the limit for Summoning (limit = drams spent instead of spirit Force).
- Change the limit for Banishing (limit = drams spent instead of Astral limit).
- Change the limit for Counterspelling (limit = drams instead of Astral limit).
- Change the limit for Alchemy tests.
- Create temporary magical lodges (Force of lodge drams; lasts 1 hour per Force point, until sunrise/sunset).

### Foci

Foci are permanent astral constructs embedded in physical objects. Must be bonded before use (spend Karma per Focus Table below; 1 hour per Force to bond). Can only bond one person at a time. Max bonded foci = Magic attribute. Max total Force of bonded foci = Magic × 5. Only one focus per test may add its Force to the dice pool. Activating/deactivating: Simple Action / Free Action respectively. Foci have auras visible in astral space.

## Implementation Notes

1. **Essence/Magic coupling** must be calculated at every point of Essence loss. Store both current Magic and maximum Magic separately; the maximum cap is reduced by cumulative Essence loss.
2. **Drain Physical threshold** is checked against raw hits (not net hits) after the limit is applied for spellcasting. Track these separately from net hits used for damage.
3. **Tradition system** should be a lookup table: tradition → {drainAttributes, elementMappings, spiritTypes}. Drain resistance pool is tradition-specific.
4. **Spirit Force ceiling**: spirits cannot be summoned at Force > 2 × Magic. Validate before the Summoning Test.
5. **Services tracking**: unbound spirits go home at sunrise/sunset OR when services = 0. Bound spirits go home only when services = 0. Implement as a countdown.
6. **Preparation Potency decay**: implement as a time-based decrement. Full Potency maintained for Potency × 2 hours; then −1 per hour. Deletion at 0.
7. **Focus bonding limits**: enforce both per-focus and aggregate limits (individual ≤ Magic; total Force ≤ Magic × 5). Only one focus adds Force per test.
8. **Mentor spirit bonuses/penalties** apply at all times regardless of other conditions (no activation required). Disadvantages trigger on specific conditions (berserk on damage, etc.) and require Charisma + Willpower tests.
9. **Assensing Table** requires a sorted lookup on hits (0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5+) returning cumulative information categories.
10. **Ritual failure** must trigger a Force × 2 drain test on all participants regardless of when in Steps 4–7 it fails.
11. **Counterspelling scope**: does not work against spirits, critter powers, or alchemical preparations. Enforce these exclusions.
12. **Physical vs. Mana spell targeting**: validate that Physical spells can target non-living objects while Mana spells cannot.
