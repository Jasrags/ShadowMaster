# Street Gear Part 1: Rules, Weapons, and Armor

**Source:** SR5 Core Rulebook, Section 11
**Book Pages:** 416–438
**PDF Pages:** 418–440

## Overview

Section 11 establishes all rules for acquiring, carrying, concealing, and selling gear in Shadowrun 5th Edition, then catalogs melee weapons, projectile weapons, firearms (all categories), firearm accessories, ammunition types, grenades/rockets/missiles, explosives, and clothing/armor. Every item has a cost, Availability Rating, and optional legality code. The wireless Matrix integration of nearly all gear is a defining mechanic of this edition.

---

## Rules

### Gear Ratings Glossary

Every item has a set of statistics. Key terms:

- **Accuracy:** Caps the number of hits on an attack roll using that weapon.
- **Ammo:** Quantity + reload method. (b) = break action, (c) = detachable box magazine/clip, (d) = drum, (ml) = muzzle-loader, (m) = internal magazine, (cy) = cylinder, (belt) = belt-fed.
- **Armor:** Adds to the wearer's Damage Resistance dice pool (see Armor, p. 168).
- **Armor Penetration (AP):** Positive AP adds to target's Armor value; negative AP reduces it.
- **Availability:** Higher = harder to acquire. Letter suffix: R = Restricted, F = Forbidden. No letter = legal.
- **Blast:** Area-of-effect rating for grenades, missiles, rockets. Damage value reduced per meter of distance from point of origin.
- **Capacity:** Slots for subsystems/accessories. Capacity in brackets = cost of that item in slots.
- **Concealability Modifier:** Dice pool modifier to Perception + Intuition Tests to spot the hidden item. Applies to observer.
- **Cost:** Base price. Rare/illegal items may cost more or less on certain black markets.
- **Damage Value (DV):** Number of boxes of damage + type: P = Physical, S = Stun. Parenthetical (f) = flechette, (e) = electrical.
- **Device Rating:** Overall quality/effectiveness of a device (1–6).
- **Essence Cost:** Cyberware/bioware reduction to Essence.
- **Mode:** Firing mode. SS = single-shot, SA = semi-automatic, BF = burst fire, FA = full auto.
- **Mounts:** Underbarrel, barrel, or top-mount. One accessory per mount. Hold-outs have no mounts. Pistols/machine pistols/SMGs have no underbarrel mount. Rifles and heavy weapons have all three. Projectile weapons take only projectile-specific accessories.
- **Reach:** Melee advantage rating based on length/size. Higher Reach beats lower Reach (see Reach, p. 184).
- **Recoil Compensation (RC):** Reduces recoil modifiers. Numbers in parentheses = full RC when all integral accessories deployed.

### Buying Gear

Gear's Availability Rating determines how easy or hard it is to acquire. Availability is an abstract amalgamation of rarity, legality, supply, demand, and distribution. The letter suffix indicates legality: R = Restricted, F = Forbidden. Gamemasters may modify Availability Rating (number or legality code) for local conditions (war zones, restricted economies).

#### Standard Goods

Items with no Availability rating can be purchased at Kong-WalMart, Stuffer Shack, Microdeck, or ordered online. Pay the listed cost (with GM adjustments for local market). Standard goods purchases are highly traceable—records are kept, shared, and cross-indexed. Even with fake SINs, purchasing habits accumulate profiles over time.

#### Starting Gear

Per character creation (p. 62): starting characters may not purchase items with a rating greater than 6 or Availability greater than 12 during character creation. No permanent purchase record is kept at character creation.

#### Black Market Goods

To purchase an item off the books, make an Availability Test: Opposed Test of Negotiation + Charisma [Social] vs. the item's Availability Rating.

- **Win:** Find the gear at listed price; delivery time from the Delivery Times table divided by net hits.
- **Tie:** Find the gear, but delivery time is twice what the table shows.
- **Fail:** Try again after twice the table's delivery time.
- **Throwing money:** For every additional 25% of item value you're willing to pay, gain 1 additional die on the Negotiation Test (max 400% = 12 extra dice).
- **Glitch:** Inquiries attract unwanted attention (law enforcement sting, Yakuza, rival runners, etc.).
- **Critical Glitch:** No chance of acquiring the item.

**Delivery Times Table:**

| Gear Cost          | Delivery Time |
| ------------------ | ------------- |
| Up to 100¥         | 6 hours       |
| 101¥ to 1,000¥     | 1 day         |
| 1,000¥ to 10,000¥  | 2 days        |
| 10,001 to 100,000¥ | 1 week        |
| More than 100,000¥ | 1 month       |

#### Contacts and Availability

Contacts (fixers, talismongers, deckmeister, etc.) use their Negotiation and Charisma for Availability Tests, with their Connection Rating as a bonus to their Social limit. A contact may ask for a finder's fee if they haven't done much business with you.

### Fencing Gear

Two-step process to convert stolen loot to cash.

**Step 1 — Find a buyer:** Extended Etiquette + Charisma [Social] Test with threshold 10, threshold time equal to the delivery time on the Delivery Times Table. May use Teamwork Test. Bundling more rare items together makes it easier to find a buyer.

**Step 2 — Sell:** Opposed Negotiation + Charisma [Social]. Final price = 25% of item's listed price + 5% per net hit, or –5% per buyer's net hit.

After agreeing on a price, you can accept or find a new buyer and restart.

- **Contacts and fencing:** A contact will fence a hot item for 5% of its value times your Loyalty Rating with that contact. No questions asked.
- **Standard goods** (legal, no Availability) cannot be fenced for more than a couple nuyen.
- **Glitch on Etiquette:** Unwanted attention from authorities or other parties.
- **Glitch/Critical Glitch on Negotiation:** You've insulted or pissed off the buyer; deal may be off—or worse.

### (Il)Legality

Items are classified as **legal**, **restricted (R)**, or **forbidden (F)**.

- **Legal:** Purchase, own, transport, and use without restriction. Using a legal item to commit a crime still carries prosecution for that crime.
- **Restricted (R):** Purchase, own, and transport only under special circumstances. Requires appropriate licenses/permits (firearms permit, concealed carry permit). Licenses obtainable through legal channels with a valid SIN. Fake licenses connected to fake SINs—if one is exposed, the other becomes worthless.
- **Forbidden (F):** Never okay to own or buy as a private citizen. No license or permit makes it permissible.

#### Jurisdiction

Law varies by country and megacorporate extraterritoriality. What's a misdemeanor in one place may be a felony elsewhere. Police in some jurisdictions will prosecute for possessing an item illegal in that jurisdiction even if it's legal where you got it. The book uses basic Seattle/UCAS guidelines as reference; gamemasters should adjust legality and availability for other regions.

#### Noticing Hidden Gear

Perception + Intuition [Mental] Test with dice pool modified by the item's Concealability. Penalized by distractions, distance, and visibility modifiers (p. 175). At least one success = the observer notices the item concealed on someone else.

#### Actively Hiding Gear

If intentionally hiding something someone is specifically looking for, and you've taken a little time (a Complex Action or so): make an Opposed Palming + Agility [Physical] v. Perception + Intuition [Mental] Test.

- Observing character's pool modified by item's Concealability.
- Observer can use Palming in place of Perception.
- If physically patted down: searcher uses Agility and Physical limit instead of Intuition and Mental limit. Any negative Concealability modifier is cut in half; any positive Concealability modifier is doubled.

### Carrying Gear

#### Carrying Capacity

Characters can carry Strength × 10 kilograms without any test. This is your **carrying capacity**. Lifting/carrying more requires a Strength + Body Test. Each hit increases weight you can carry by another 10 kg.

#### Encumbrance

For every 15 kg (or part thereof) by which you exceed carrying capacity: –1 to Physical Limit (minimum 1). Example: Strength 3 character (Carrying Capacity 30) trudging with 50 kg suffers –2 to Physical Limit.

#### Size Costs

Trolls have large bodies and hands; dwarfs have small bodies and hands. Most products are available in dwarf- and troll-friendly sizes. Dwarfs pay +20% on Lifestyle. Trolls pay the "troll tax"—Lifestyle costs are doubled.

#### Using Unadapted Gear

When using an item not customized for your hand size, dwarf and troll characters receive a –2 modifier on dice pools for using human-sized weapons and equipment. An elf trying to use a dwarf-modified weapon also suffers a –2 dice pool modifier. Cumulative: a dwarf forced to use a troll-modified tool suffers –4 dice pool modifier (possibly a hernia). In some cases it may be impossible for a dwarf or troll to use the other's gear.

### Wireless Functionality

Almost every device is wireless-enabled. Non-electronic items without moving parts have built-in computers. Non-wireless devices are likely tagged with **RFID tags** (p. 440).

#### Device Ratings

| Type          | Rating | Examples                                                                                                |
| ------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Simple        | 1      | General appliances, public terminals, entertainment systems                                             |
| Average       | 2      | Standard personal electronics, basic cyberware, vehicles, drones, weapons, residential security devices |
| Smart         | 3      | Security vehicles, alphaware, corporate security devices                                                |
| Advanced      | 4      | High-end devices, betaware, military vehicles, mil-spec security devices                                |
| Cutting Edge  | 5      | Deltaware, credsticks, black-ops vehicles and security devices                                          |
| Bleeding Edge | 6      | Billion-nuyen experimental devices, spacecraft                                                          |

#### Wireless Bonuses

When an item has additional functionality when connected to the Matrix, it is described under the "Wireless" entry in the item's description. This functionality applies only when the device has Matrix access. Noise Rating from a situation greater than the item's Device Rating (not including distance) temporarily removes wireless functionality (see Noise, p. 230). Wireless bonuses only apply when the item's wireless mode is on.

#### Turning It Off

Toggling an individual device's wireless functionality is a Free Action. Toggling all devices to "wireless off" is also a Free Action. You lose wireless bonuses, but items can no longer be wirelessly hacked. Otherwise, rely on your team hacker to provide wireless defense to your PAN and get the best of both worlds.

#### Throwbacks

**Throwbacks** are devices with no wireless capability. They still exist but are increasingly rare in the 2070s. Cannot be accessed by wireless connection; cannot be controlled remotely; gain no wireless bonus.

Converting a device to a throwback: Hardware + Logic [Mental] (8, 10 minutes) Extended Test, or purchase the device as a throwback. Throwbacks are immune to wireless hacking but have a universal access port and can be connected via data cable. A hacker with a datajack can connect to a throwback directly; if they can sneak up and plug in, the device is completely accessible.

#### Incompatibility

When two items are incompatible (e.g., a shotgun and a laser sight, or wired reflexes and synaptic boosters), you cannot use the effects of both items simultaneously. You can own both; you just pick which piece of gear to use at what time.

### Projectile and Throwing Weapons

Muscle-powered, with possible simple mechanical assistance.

#### Bows

Archaic and rare, but hacker-proof. Reloading a bow takes a Simple Action (Ready Weapon, p. 165). Bows have a minimum Strength rating. Attacking with a bow when your Strength is less than the minimum: –3 dice pool modifier per point below the minimum. The bow's Rating also determines range and damage (max Rating 10). Use the lowest value of Strength, bow's rating, or arrow Rating for range and damage.

- **Bow:** Acc 6, Dmg (Rating+2)P, AP –(Rating/4), Avail Rating, Cost Rating × 100¥.
- **Arrow:** Acc —, Avail Rating, Cost Rating × 2¥.
- **Injection arrow:** Avail (Rating+2)R, Cost Rating × 20¥. Delivers one dose of drug/toxin; attack must deal at least one box of damage after the Damage Resistance Test. Injection vector toxin attack.

#### Crossbows

Equipped with automatic reloading devices (no Ready Weapon action unless using museum piece). Internal magazines (m) holding up to 4 bolts. Available in Light, Medium, and Heavy sizes.

- **Light crossbow:** Acc 7, Dmg 5P, AP –1, Avail 2, Cost 300¥.
- **Medium crossbow:** Acc 6, Dmg 7P, AP –2, Avail 4R, Cost 500¥.
- **Heavy crossbow:** Acc 5, Dmg 10P, AP –3, Avail 8R, Cost 1,000¥.
- **Bolt:** Avail 2, Cost 5¥.
- **Injection bolt:** Avail 8R, Cost 50¥. Same rules as injection arrow.

#### Throwing Weapons

- **Throwing knife/shuriken:** Wide variety of aerodynamically balanced weighted throwing weapons. A character can ready (Agility + 2) of these with one Ready Weapon action. Use Throwing Weapons skill. _Wireless:_ All throwing knives/shuriken thrown in a single Combat Turn that are wireless and you have a smartlink system: each knife thrown receives a +1 dice pool bonus per knife thrown (cumulative). Acc Physical, Dmg (STR+1)P, AP –1, Avail 4R, Cost 25¥.

#### Tasers

Legal to carry; good non-lethal incapacitation. Like other electrical weapons: flat AP –5, deal electricity damage. Use Pistols skill. Tasers can take only top-mounted accessories.

- **Defiance EX Shocker:** Heavy-duty taser, fires 4 darts trailing wires up to 20m. Wired connection for more powerful shock. Also features contacts allowing use as stun weapon in melee (Accuracy 3, DV 8S(e), Reach 0). _Wireless:_ Successful hit informs you of target's basic health and Condition Monitors. Acc 4, Dmg 9S(e), AP –5, Mode SS, Ammo 4 (m), Avail —, Cost 250¥.
- **Yamaha Pulsar:** Wireless dart taser; darts contain wireless capacitors (no wires). Slightly less powerful than EX Shocker but faster fire. Lacks EX Shocker's melee contacts. _Wireless:_ Successful hit informs you of target's basic health and Condition Monitors. Acc 5, Dmg 7S(e), AP –5, Mode SA, Ammo 4 (m), Avail —, Cost 180¥.

### Firearms

All firearms are chambered for cased or caseless ammunition. All firearms have wireless capability and a digital ammunition counter. Ammunition is sold separately. All firearms have the following wireless bonuses in addition to model-specific bonuses:

_Wireless (all firearms):_ The weapon displays an ARO telling you ammo levels and ammo type loaded. With a DNI, two additional benefits: (1) ejecting a clip is a Free Action rather than Simple Action; (2) changing fire modes is a Free Action rather than Simple Action.

### Ammunition

Ammunition is defined by type (regular, gel, APDS, etc.), class of firearm (light pistol, heavy pistol, assault rifle, etc.), and whether it's cased or caseless. Unless otherwise indicated, a gun can fire cased or caseless ammo, but not both. Each class of gun can trade ammo with another of its class (all heavy pistols can share ammo).

Damage Modifier and AP Modifier are added to the weapon's usual DV and AP. Example: Ares Predator V (DV 8P, AP –1) loaded with explosive rounds (DV +1, AP –1) has a DV of 9P and AP –2.

**APDS (Armor Piercing Discarding Sabot):** Military-grade armor piercing rounds. Travel at high velocities to punch through personal body armor.

**Assault cannon rounds:** For assault cannons only; the only thing assault cannons can load.

**Explosive rounds:** Shaped-charge explosive; designed to explode and fragment on impact. Misfire whenever you roll a critical glitch—must resist one "attack" with DV equal to the normal damage of the weapon (plus the modifier for explosive rounds). The attack misses its intended target, and the weapon firing the bullets is destroyed.

**Flechette rounds:** Tiny, tightly packed metal slivers. Breaks up and shatters on impact; tumbling hail of shrapnel. Devastating against unprotected targets but not as effective against hardened armor.

**Gel rounds:** Less-lethal; hard jelly-like substance. Semi-rigid slugs flatten on impact, disbursing kinetic energy over a larger-than-normal area. Inflict Stun damage (AP +1). Reduce target's Physical limit by 2 when checking for knockdown (Knockdown, p. 194).

**Hollow points:** Dum dum rounds with indented tips. "Mushroom" or expand on impact, becoming a hail of deadly fragments (similar to flechettes, but older technology). Better against unarmored targets; tend to flatten against armor.

**Injection darts:** For use with dart guns (Parashield pistol and rifle). Each dart carries a single dose of drug or toxin. To successfully deliver the payload: the attack must deal at least one net hit against an unarmored target or three net hits against a target with armor. Injection vector toxin attack.

**Regular ammo (ball / full metal jacket):** Also called ball or full metal jacket rounds. Solid slugs for general purposes (mainly killing things).

**Stick-n-Shock:** Electrical Stun damage equal to the damage of the base weapon –2. Flat AP –5 replaces the weapon's AP instead of stacking with it.

**Tracer rounds:** Ammo burns along the line of flight; easier for the shooter to home in on the target. Can only be used in Full Auto weapons and, being loaded every third round in a clip, is an exception to the restriction of one ammo type per clip. Improve Accuracy of the gun by 1 when firing more than one round in an Action Phase (cumulative with a laser sight but not a smartgun).

**Taser dart:** Designed to be loaded into taser weapons.

### Explosives

Primarily used to damage structures. Use Demolitions skill to prepare explosives, identify key or weak structural points, and focus the blast. Each hit on a Demolitions + Logic [Mental] Test adds 1 to the explosive's effective rating. See Barriers (p. 194) for calculating an explosive's effect on a barrier.

**Damage Value calculation:** Rating (modified by Demolitions Test, if made one) times the square root of the number of kilograms used (rounded down).

**Blast values:**

- Circular explosion: –2 per meter from point of origin.
- Directional explosion (up to 60 degrees in a specific direction): –1 per meter.

When explosives are attached directly to a target, the target's armor is halved; otherwise the explosive has an AP value of –2.

If an explosion destroys a barrier, it creates a cloud of deadly shrapnel that threatens an area far bigger than the actual blast—the shrapnel blast has a DV equal to the explosive's DV minus the Structure rating of the barrier, with a Blast of –1/m.

## Implementation Notes

- **Availability Test vs. Availability Rating:** The Black Market Goods rule is an Opposed Test, not a threshold test. The character's Negotiation + Charisma [Social] opposes the item's Availability Rating as a dice pool, not as a fixed threshold.
- **Ammo stacking:** Damage Modifier and AP Modifier from ammo types are added to weapon base DV and AP. Only one ammo type per clip (with the exception of Tracer rounds, which are every third round).
- **Firearm wireless bonuses:** All firearms get the base ARO bonus (ammo levels + type). DNI adds clip ejection and fire mode changes as Free Actions. These are in addition to model-specific wireless bonuses.
- **Armor Capacity = Armor Rating:** This is critical for tracking which modifications can fit on a given piece of armor.
- **Reach:** Melee combat with Reach advantage/disadvantage is resolved at Melee Combat rules (p. 184), not in this section.
- **Concealing gear:** The Concealability modifier applies to the observer's dice pool, not the hider's. When actively hiding and being physically patted down, negative Concealability modifiers are halved, positive are doubled.
- **Size costs (dwarfs/trolls):** Only apply to Lifestyle costs, not to gear purchase prices directly; gear itself is available in appropriate sizes.
- **Ranger Arms SM-5 fragility:** Track Combat Turn usage in running firefights; Accuracy degrades until recalibrated (1-hour job).
- **Explosive DV formula:** `DV = Rating × floor(sqrt(kg))`. E.g., Rating 10 explosive, 4 kg: DV = 10 × floor(√4) = 10 × 2 = 20.
- **Minigrenades vs. standard grenades:** Use same damage values and cost; differentiated only by size (designed for grenade launchers) and the 5-meter arming distance.
- **Missiles vs. Rockets:** Missiles = rockets + internal guidance. In tables: Avail is +4 compared to the equivalent rocket, Cost is +Sensor Rating × 500¥.
- **Flechette armor interaction:** Flechette rounds have AP +5 (positive = adds to target's Armor, reducing penetration benefit of AP). Devastating vs. unarmored; poor vs. hardened armor.
- **Stick-n-Shock AP:** The –5 AP **replaces** the weapon's AP entirely; it does not stack with the weapon's existing AP value.
