# Helps and Hindrances

**Source:** SR5 Core Rulebook, Section 10, pp. 378–415
**PDF Pages:** 380–417

## Overview

Section 10 covers three major categories of non-player content: Non-Player Characters (NPCs), Critters, and Toxins/Drugs/BTLs. NPCs are divided into grunts (nameless, interchangeable combatants managed as groups), prime runners (named, fully built characters), and contacts (information/service brokers with Connection and Loyalty ratings). Critters encompasses mundane animals, paracritters (Awakened species), and dracoforms, each with full stat blocks and critter powers. Toxins, drugs, and BTLs cover substances used as weapons or recreationally, with full mechanical stats and an addiction system.

## Rules

### Grunts

Grunts are NPCs grouped together because they have virtually identical game statistics. They share one set of attributes and skills, have one Condition Monitor, and use a shared Edge pool (Group Edge). Each group may have specialist grunts with additional skills or equipment; the GM notes these separately.

**Condition Monitor:** Grunts have a single Condition Monitor tracking both Physical and Stun damage. Boxes = 8 + ½ (Body or Willpower, whichever is higher), rounded down. When full, the grunt is out of action. Grunts do not receive overflow damage.

- If final damage type was Stun and total did not exceed Body: grunt is alive.
- If final damage type was Physical and exceeded Body: grunt is dead.

**Initiative Streamlining:** GM may make a single Initiative Test for the entire group. Augmented specialists may roll their own Initiative if desired.

**Mowing Them Down (Optional Rules):**

- A single wound takes a grunt down.
- Normal resistance rules don't apply; all rolls against grunts are unopposed. Most spells go off unhindered; grunts do not roll to dodge ranged attacks.
- If a PC makes any hits on a Sneaking Test, grunts are automatically surprised.
- If grunts see the shadowrunners coming they may attempt an ambush, but the ambush automatically fails.

### Professional Rating

The Professional Rating measures a group's overall experience, discipline, and how well they can handle shadowrunners. It serves as:

1. A dice pool modifier for resisting Social Skill Tests.
2. The determinant for the group's Edge attribute and Group Edge pool.

| Rating             | Description                                                                                    | Examples                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0 (Untrained)      | No unit training, clumsy reactions. Run when someone goes down.                                | Mall cops, random street mobs                              |
| 1–2 (Semi-trained) | Some combat experience, act with purpose but not veteran coolness. Retreat if >25% casualties. | Rookie beat cops, many gang members, corporate security    |
| 3–4 (Trained)      | Well trained, experienced, keep heads together. Withdraw if >50% casualties.                   | Professional bodyguards, experienced cops, mercenary units |
| 5–6 (Elite)        | Top professionals, fight to the last or until mission parameters say to stop.                  | HTR, SWAT, military special forces, fanatics               |

### Group Edge

Grunts do not have individual Edge attributes. Instead, the group has a shared Group Edge pool equal to the Professional Rating. The GM spends Group Edge on behalf of any grunt in the group. Group Edge should only be spent in situations critical to the group's goals. Group Edge refreshes at the GM's discretion, never more frequently than PCs recover their own Edge.

### Lieutenants

A lieutenant is a single grunt who stands above the rest of the team. Rules:

- A team has only **one** lieutenant.
- Lieutenants draw on Group Edge, not individual Edge.
- Lieutenants make their own Initiative Tests; if tied with the team, they go first.
- Lieutenants have a single Condition Monitor like other grunts.
- Lieutenant attributes, when totalled, should be **at least 4 higher** than the standard grunt's total attributes.
- Lieutenant Active skills, when totalled, should **exceed other grunts by at least 4 points**.
- In combat, a lieutenant with the Leadership skill can use it to increase the group's Professional Rating by 1 higher than normal (also adds 1 to their Edge).

### Prime Runners

Prime runners are named NPCs built from the ground up using the Priority System (p. 62), advanced with Karma. They recur through adventures and campaigns.

**Four Levels:**
| Level | Advancement Karma |
|-------|------------------|
| Inferior | 80% of PC's average Karma award |
| Equal | 100% of PC's average Karma award |
| Superior | 125% of PC's average Karma award |
| Superhuman | 200% of PC's average Karma award |

**Building Guidelines:**

- Use the Priority Table (p. 65) for all prime runners.
- For Street or Prime Runner campaign level (p. 64), alternate rules apply.
- Inferior prime runners use the same Priority Table but take one level lower for each category (Priority A resources → receive Priority B resources).
- Inferior prime runners carry the burden of two Priority E choices.
- Prime runners are not bound by starting-character constraints on skills and gear (Superior and Superhuman).
- All dragons are considered Superhuman Prime Runners.

### The Hand of God

When a prime runner who is meant to recur would die in full view of the PCs, the GM may invoke the Hand of God. Burn all of the prime runner's remaining Edge permanently — the prime runner survives, barely, and escapes. He will return later, possibly changed (new scar, reduced attribute by 1, new Negative quality).

> **Ambiguity:** "Burn all remaining Edge" is explicit, but the rule does not specify whether this uses the prime runner's personal Edge or Group Edge if they happen to be leading grunts. Given that prime runners have their own Edge, personal Edge is implied.

### Contacts — By the Numbers

Contacts are Inferior or Equal prime runners built with the Priority System. They are assumed to have basic gear appropriate to their lifestyle and day job.

**Two Ratings:**

**Connection (1–12):** Measures the contact's social influence and network reach. A character cannot start the game with a contact whose Connection Rating exceeds 6.

**Loyalty (1–6):** Measures trust and willingness to assist. Applied as bonus dice when a PC negotiates with the contact. Also used as a modifier or threshold when someone tries to put pressure on the contact about the runners.

### Using Contacts

Four major functions:

1. **Legwork:** The contact makes an Extended Connection + Charisma [Social] Test (interval 1 hour) to research information. Threshold is based on difficulty (Extended Test Thresholds table, p. 48). The contact's Social limit applies. The GM may substitute an appropriate Knowledge skill for Charisma. A PC's Street Cred (p. 372) can affect the Social limit. Paying 100 × (7 – Loyalty) nuyen adds one extra die to the Negotiation Test.

2. **Networking:** To arrange a meeting with someone the runners don't know, the contact makes an Etiquette + Charisma [Social] Test with a threshold equal to the target NPC's Connection Rating, plus any appropriate social modifiers, plus a dice pool bonus equal to the contact's Connection rating. If the test succeeds, the meeting is arranged (4D6 hours). If the meeting goes badly, the contact's rep suffers.

3. **Swag (Buying/Selling):** The contact follows the same Availability and Fencing Tests (p. 418) that PCs do, using their own Charisma and Negotiation skill plus Connection rating as a modifier to their Social limit.

4. **Favors:** Any other direct aid. Governs by the Favor Rating Table. Favors range 1–6. Contacts normally agree to favors with a Favor Rating ≤ their Loyalty Rating. If Favor Rating exceeds Loyalty Rating, the PC must succeed at a Negotiation + Charisma [Social] Opposed Test.

**Contact Availability:** No set test. GM determines availability narratively. If GM doesn't care, roll 2D6; contact is available if the roll equals or exceeds the contact's Connection rating.

### Contact Glitches

When dealing with contacts, glitches can happen on either side. For legwork glitches, the contact may provide wrong information. For swag glitches, the item may be unusable or have bugs. A critical glitch means the contact has given the PCs information that is flat-out untrue, or a third party has learned about the PC's activities.

### Favor for a Friend

Contacts expect payment: approximately Connection Rating × 100 nuyen for information or expertise, up to Connection Rating × 1,000¥ for goods or services, minus up to Loyalty × 10% if the runner has been nice. Alternatively, favors owed are used as plot hooks for adventures.

## Critters

### Attributes and Skills

Critters have a full attribute set. An attribute rating of 0 means no dice from that attribute are added to any test, but the critter can still use linked skills (just gets 0 dice from the attribute). Attributes can be adjusted up to ±3 points from listed values, but never below zero. Skills can likewise be adjusted.

Critters lacking a skill are Unaware (p. 131) in it unless they have the Sapience power, in which case they are Untrained.

Most Awakened critters have a Magic attribute indicating innate magical potential, but this does not grant spellcasting or conjuring unless they also have the Magician or Mystic Adept quality.

### Movement

Movement rates are listed as: walking multiplier × running multiplier / sprint increase in meters per hit. Format: x2/x4/+2. Based on Agility (see Movement, p. 161). Flight uses the Flight skill linked to Agility.

### Powers

Critter powers are special abilities. Each power has:

- **Type:** M (mana — does not affect nonliving targets, cannot be used in astral space or against astral forms) or P (physical — cannot affect astral targets unless critter is dual-natured or astral)
- **Action:** Simple, Complex, or Auto (always on, no action required)
- **Range:** LOS, Touch, or Self
- **Duration:** Always (constant), Instant, Sustained, or Permanent (must be maintained until permanent), or Special

Sustained powers are maintained at no cost or strain; no sustaining modifier applies, no line of sight required after taking hold. Critters may sustain up to Magic powers (or multiple instances of the same power) simultaneously.

### Critter Power Listings

#### Accident

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Instant
- Causes seemingly normal accidents. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Willpower vs. target's Reaction + Intuition. If critter wins, accident occurs. 4+ net hits = critical glitch severity accident. Can target a number equal to its Magic rating simultaneously.

#### Animal Control

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Controls behavior of normal (mundane) animals. Cannot be used on critters with Sapience. Target animal must exhibit a normal behavior. If animal leaves LOS, it continues following last orders. Control: Charisma × 5 small animals OR Charisma large animals.

#### Armor

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Natural Armor rating. Cumulative with worn armor.

#### Astral Form

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Critter exists only in astral plane. Only astral attacks or mana spells/powers can harm it. Physical attacks/spells have no effect. Can manifest on physical plane like an astrally projecting magician (p. 313).

#### Binding

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Instant
- Sticks target to any surface. Target breaks free with Complex Action: Strength + Body vs. critter's Magic + Willpower. Failure = immobilized until next attempt. Range depends on binding method (LOS for webbing, Touch for sticky body, Self for sticking).

#### Compulsion

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Compels target to perform a specific action. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Charisma vs. target's Willpower + Logic. Target immediately carries out compelled action. Once power drops, target's mind is their own; cannot plant future suggestions.

#### Concealment

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Simple | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Mystically hides targets. Subtracts Magic dice from any Perception Tests to locate concealed subject. Can conceal: Magic metahuman-sized targets, or Magic × 5 small targets simultaneously.

#### Confusion

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Makes target indecisive and befuddled. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Willpower vs. target's Willpower + Logic. Net hits become a negative dice pool modifier for all of the target's actions.

#### Corrosive Spit

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Instant
- Standard ranged combat attack using critter's Exotic Ranged Weapon skill + Agility [Physical]. Range increments = Body meters. DV = (Magic × 2)P. AP = –(critter's Magic).

#### Dragonspeech

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Instant
- Telepathic communication within LOS regardless of Language skill. Cannot be picked up by technology. Dragons wishing to communicate through modern technology must use a metahuman as a translator. Targets cannot respond telepathically without their own ability.

#### Dual Natured

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Active in both astral and physical planes simultaneously. Can affect both astral and physical beings. No –2 dice pool modifier for interacting with physical world while astrally perceiving. Cannot shift between planes (always in both).

#### Elemental Attack

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Instant
- Ranged attack using Exotic Ranged Weapon skill + Agility [Physical]. Range increments = Magic meters. DV = (Magic × 2)P + AP –(critter's Magic) + appropriate elemental damage (p. 170).

#### Energy Aura

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Adds Magic to DV of any melee attack the critter makes. Elemental type specified. AP = –(critter's Magic). Successful melee attacks against a critter with Energy Aura also deal damage to the attacker: Damage Resistance Test, DV = critter's Magic × 2, AP –(critter's Magic).

#### Engulf

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Sustained
- Draws target into critter's body/terrain; melee attack. DV = Magic × 2 (element-specific effects apply). Once engulfed, target is trapped and cannot move. Every Action Phase, critter automatically inflicts same damage; target resists each time with Body + Armor, AP –(critter's Magic). Complex Action to escape: Opposed Test, target's Strength + Body vs. critter's Magic + Body.
  - **Air Engulf:** Stun damage, resisted as inhalation toxin. Armor does not protect.
  - **Earth Engulf:** Target resists Physical damage.
  - **Fire Engulf:** Target resists Fire damage (p. 171).
  - **Water Engulf:** Stun damage. Victims who pass out continue taking Stun (overflow to Physical as normal).

#### Enhanced Senses

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Improved sensory capabilities beyond normal human range (low-light, thermographic, hearing, smell, sonar, etc.). Specific senses listed in critter description. If enhanced sense lacks specific effect, grants +2 dice pool modifier for tests using that sense and +1 to appropriate limit.

#### Essence Drain

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Permanent
- Permanently drains Essence from a sapient physical being. Target must be willing, subdued, or mentally controlled. Emotional connection required (passion, anger, terror — terror must be specifically of the attacker). Extended Test: Charisma + Magic (10 – target's Essence, 1 minute). Interruption = Essence not drained. Lost Essence affects Magic/Resonance (p. 52). Target at Essence 0 dies. Critter can only increase Essence to twice its natural maximum. Excess Essence drained beyond this cap is lost; astral form can only hold so much. Critter can pump stolen Essence into other attributes (Simple Action): each Essence point temporarily boosts one Physical/Mental attribute or Magic by +1. Max boost per attribute: +4. Boost lasts 12 hours. Willing victims may find draining addictive — must make Psychological Addiction Test (p. 413) with threshold 2.

#### Fear

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Special
- Fills target with overwhelming terror. Opposed Test: critter's Willpower + Magic vs. target's Willpower + Logic. Terror lasts 1 Combat Turn per net hit. Once fear ends, target must succeed at Willpower + Logic (critter's net hits) test to face the critter again.

#### Guard

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Protects against normal environmental accidents and hazards (including Accident power) and can prevent glitches. Can be used on a number of characters equal to critter's Magic. Each accident averted or glitch prevented counts as a service if used by a spirit.

#### Hardened Armor

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Rating in Armor, same as Armor power with differences: If modified DV ≤ Hardened Armor rating (modified by AP), the attack does **no damage** — no Damage Resistance Test required. If modified DV > Hardened Armor rating, perform Damage Resistance Test normally, and half the Hardened Armor rating (rounded up) counts as automatic extra hits.

#### Hardened Mystic Armor

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Same as Hardened Armor but provides protection against attacks on the astral plane.

#### Immunity

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Enhanced resistance to a specific attack type. Effectively Hardened Armor equal to twice the critter's Essence for that damage type.
  - **Immunity to Age:** Critter does not age.
  - **Immunity to Normal Weapons:** All non-magical attacks (not weapon foci, adept/critter powers). If critter also has an Allergy, the Immunity does not apply against non-magical attacks made using the allergen.

#### Infection

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Permanent
- Vector for HMHVV transmission. When critter uses Essence Drain to reduce a victim's Essence to 0, Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Charisma vs. victim's Body + Willpower. If critter wins, victim is infected and enters a coma-like state. 24 hours later, victim rises as a new Infected critter (as determined by original metatype) with 1 Essence and an insatiable hunger. Player characters transformed by Infection become NPCs.

#### Influence

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Instant
- Implants a suggestion. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Charisma vs. target's Willpower + Logic. Target acts on suggestion as if it were their own idea. Target can make a Willpower Test (threshold = net hits) to recognize the wrongness of the suggestion. Cannot plant future-triggered suggestions; once used, the target's mind is their own.

#### Innate Spell

- **Type:** As spell | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** As spell | **Duration:** As spell
- Critter instinctively casts a specific spell. Requires Spellcasting skill. Innate Spells are from the list of spells cast by magicians. Magicians can counter with Counterspelling normally. Produces Drain as normal; –2 penalty for sustaining. Critters and Spirits resist Drain with either Intuition or Charisma (GM's discretion).

#### Materialization

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Sustained
- Astral critters project into the physical world and form a temporary "body." Gains Immunity to Normal Weapons while materialized. Materializing and dematerializing each require a Complex Action.

#### Mimicry

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Simple | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Sustained
- Exactly imitates sounds including speech and other creature calls. Memory-based imitation requires a Memory Test (p. 152). Detecting a false sound requires a Perception Test; threshold = number of hits the critter scored with Charisma + Magic.

#### Mist Form

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Sustained
- Critter transforms into a cloud of mist. Movement: 5 meters per Combat Turn. Cannot use other powers while in mist form. Can pass through any cracks not airtight. While in mist form: Immunity to Normal Weapons. If exposed to an allergen, immediately forced back to normal form. Systems defending against gases/bacteria also stop mist form.

#### Movement

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Modifies target's movement rate. Only works on things predisposed to locomotion. Critter can multiply or divide the target's movement rate by up to its Magic. Only one instance per target at a time. Once target leaves the critter's terrain/domain, power ends. On vehicles: Magic + Willpower test (threshold = ½ vehicle's Body, minimum 2); if met, multiply hits by vehicle's Acceleration Rating and add to/subtract from vehicle's Speed next Combat Turn.

#### Mystic Armor

- **Type:** M | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Functions as Armor but only provides protection from attacks on the astral plane.

#### Natural Weapon

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Instant
- Claws, teeth, tail, etc. Description specifies DV and AP. Natural melee weapons may be used against astral targets within reach by a dual-natured critter (uses Unarmed Combat skill, physical DV). Natural ranged weapons do not work on astral plane.

#### Noxious Breath

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Instant
- Ranged attack using Exotic Ranged Weapon skill + Agility [Physical]. Toxin attack with inhalation vector (Speed: Immediate, Power: critter's Magic, Effect: Stun damage, nausea; see p. 409). Armor useless; respiratory protection helps. Extends in a cone of (Body) meters; can catch up to 2 targets within 1 meter of each other.

#### Paralyzing Howl

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Special
- Affects everyone within (Magic × 15) meters. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Charisma vs. target's Intuition + Willpower. Rating of sound-dampening devices/cyberware/spells is added to target's Opposed Test. If critter wins: target's Reaction and Agility each reduced by 1 per net hit; reduction in Reaction affects Initiative and Initiative Score. Effect lasts (Magic + net hits) Combat Turns. If either Agility or Reaction drops to 0, target is paralyzed for (Magic + net hits) minutes and can take no actions except breathe. After paralysis ends, Reaction and Agility each return at 1 point per minute.

#### Paralyzing Touch

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Special
- Touch-range version of Paralyzing Howl. If target is surprised/unaware, automatic success treated as melee attack that causes no damage. Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Willpower vs. target's Intuition + Willpower. Otherwise functions as Paralyzing Howl.

#### Petrification

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Opposed Test: critter's Magic + Willpower vs. target's Intuition + Willpower. If critter wins, target's Agility and Reaction each reduced by 1 per net hit (also affects Initiative). If either drops to 0, target transformed into stone statue. Statue has Barrier Rating = critter's Magic × 2, is unaware of surroundings. Once power ends, target resumes normal state, Reaction and Agility return at 1 point per minute.

#### Psychokinesis

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Moves objects with the mind (similar to Magic Fingers, p. 294). Strength and Agility = number of hits scored on Magic + Willpower test.

#### Regeneration

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Rapidly heals damage. At the end of each Combat Turn, if critter has any damage on either Condition Monitor, it makes a Magic + Body Test. Adds Body to hits scored. Heals that many boxes, first from Physical overflow, then Physical damage monitor, then Stun damage monitor. If Physical overflow still exceeds Body after test, critter is not yet dead but still gets a Regeneration Test. Cannot heal: brain/spinal cord damage (called shots to head), magical damage from weapon foci, combat spells, most critter/adept powers, Drain, or damage from allergens (while in contact with allergen). Incompatible with augmentations — critters with Regeneration cannot receive augmentations; surgical incisions close too quickly.

#### Sapience

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- Critter is self-aware, capable of independent choices, at or above Homo sapiens level. Treats skills without Sapience as Untrained (not Unaware). Can learn new skills. Can Awaken and possess a Magic attribute; can learn any magical task. Not known to Emerge as technomancers (but scientists believe it is only a matter of time).

#### Search

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** Special | **Duration:** Special
- Finds a specific target. Extended Test: Magic + Intuition (5, 10 minutes). Various modifiers apply (see Search Modifiers Table). Critter must have seen the target before. Spirits can search in astral space.

#### Venom

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Instant
- Critter secretes a toxin (p. 408). Typical attributes: Vector: Injection, Speed: 1 Combat Turn, Penetration: 0, Power: Magic, Effect: Physical damage. Some critters have venoms with different attributes — noted individually.

#### Weather Control

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Complex | **Range:** LOS | **Duration:** Sustained
- Manipulates local weather conditions within realistic environmental possibility. Builds over time; peaks when critter completes a Magic + Willpower (10, 30 minutes) Extended Test. Critter summons and pushes weather in desired direction but cannot aim lightning bolts, etc.

### Critter Weaknesses

#### Allergy

Treat as the Allergy negative quality (p. 78).

#### Dietary Requirement

Critter must include something specific in its diet (toxic waste, petroleum, gold, metahuman flesh). Needs at least one meal per day appropriate to its size and metabolism. Without the specific requirement, the critter will eventually grow sick and die.

#### Essence Loss

Some critters (notably the Infected) have no actual Essence of their own. They slowly lose any Essence they've stolen at the rate of 1 point every lunar month. Losing Essence can affect Magic (p. 278). At Essence 0, the critter will die within Body + Willpower days unless it feeds. Any power that is not automatic (requires an action to use) is Essence-intensive for Infected — each such use accelerates Essence loss by one week.

#### Induced Dormancy

Some condition (lack of air, orichalcum) forces critter into a coma-like suspended animation. Length of exposure varies per critter. The critter will awaken quickly (usually within a minute) once the condition is removed.

#### Reduced Senses

One or more of the critter's five basic senses is limited in effectiveness. Typically reduced senses function at half the normal range or effectiveness; may be reduced further to complete loss.

#### Uneducated

Treat as the Uneducated negative quality (p. 87).

#### Vulnerability

Critter has an Achilles' heel. Increase DV of all attacks with the substance by 3. Weapons made of the substance bypass any Immunities. Damage from the substance cannot be healed by Regeneration or healing magic — only by natural healing. Some Vulnerabilities are conditions, not substances (e.g., basilisk is vulnerable to its own Petrification power — a –3 dice pool modifier applies to its Resistance Test).

### Critter Combat

Critters roll attribute + attack skill. They use defaulting rules if they lack a skill. Most critters without combat skills attempt to flee when confronted. Critters that feel they have an advantage (or are given a command) will initiate combat; if advantage wanes, they retreat at the first opportunity. Cornered predators attack in an all-out attack until either the critter or its foe is a corpse.

#### Barghest

Metaspecies of English mastiff. ~85 cm at shoulder, ~170 kg. Pure white or pure black fur. Luminescent saliva due to bacteria; eyes shine red. Hunt alone, in pairs, or packs of 12+. Used as guard animals by some corps.
**Natural Habitat:** Urban areas in North America and northern Europe; also Australia, Hong Kong, Nairobi.

## Toxins, Drugs, and BTLs

### Toxin Attributes

Every toxin has the following attributes:

- **Vector:** How the toxin is delivered (Contact, Ingestion, Inhalation, Injection)
- **Speed:** How quickly the Effect occurs. Effects always applied at the end of a Combat Turn. "Immediate" = end of same Combat Turn. "1 Combat Turn" = end of next Combat Turn.
- **Penetration:** Works like Armor Penetration; affects the rating of protective systems defending against it.
- **Power:** The DV of the attack for damage-dealing toxins. Non-damage toxins: Power determines whether effects take place if the Toxin Resistance Test reduces Power to 0.
- **Effect:** What happens to a victim exposed to the toxin.

**Effect Types:**

- **Disorientation:** –2 dice pool modifier to all actions for 10 minutes.
- **Nausea:** Catch-all (pain, panic, vomiting, double vision). If Power after Toxin Resistance Test exceeds target's Willpower → incapacitation (unable to take any actions) with vomiting/dizziness for 3 Combat Turns. Nausea also doubles all wound modifiers for a nauseated character.
- **Paralysis:** Victim unable to move (neuromuscular signals blocked). If Power after TRT exceeds target's Reaction → paralyzed for 1 hour. Even if not paralyzed, –2 dice pool modifier for next hour.
- **Truth Serum:** Reduces target's Willpower by 3 (minimum 1) for the remainder of the toxin's effect (see Gamma-Scopolamine).

### Using Toxins

When a toxin comes into play, note its Speed to determine when it takes effect. At the end of the appropriate Combat Turn, the victim makes a **Toxin Resistance Test**: Body + Willpower + rating of any protective gear/systems. Each hit reduces the toxin's Power by 1. If Power reaches 0, the toxin takes no effect. Otherwise, apply the effect based on remaining Power level.

### Concentration

If exposed to multiple doses simultaneously, increase Power by +1 per additional dose. Duration may also increase (GM's discretion). If left in contact with a toxin for an extended period, perform another Toxin Resistance Test each time the Speed interval elapses. For each subsequent TRT after the first, increase Power by +1 cumulatively.

### Antidotes

Antidotes must be taken before effects kick in to prevent damage; taken after kickin they may reduce other effects but will not diminish damage already caused. Some toxins (particularly neurotoxins) have no effective antidote. If a victim suffered Physical damage overflow from a toxin, application of the appropriate antidote automatically stabilizes them.

### Better Than Life (BTL)

BTL programs remove the safety protocols from simsense recordings, producing high-intensity psychologically addictive output that directly stimulates pleasure centers.

**All BTL programs share:**

- **Speed:** Immediate
- **Duration:** Varies, typically 10 × 1D6 minutes
- **Addiction Type:** Psychological

**BTL Chips:** Physical chips requiring a simsense deck or modified module with hot-sim accommodation. Formats: Dreamdeck chips (require old-fashioned simsense deck/modified module) or direct-input chips (slotted into skilljack or datajack). Both formats auto-erase after single use. The one-shot self-erasing feature can be bypassed with a Hardware + Logic (10, 1 hour) Extended Test.

**BTL Downloads:** One-time, self-erasing programs available via commlink with appropriately modified hot-sim module.

#### BTL Types

- **Dreamchips:** Standard simsense recordings modified for BTL output. Studio-produced sims or doctored personal recordings.
- **Moodchips:** Focus on emotive track and intense emotional sensations. After the chip's program runs its course, user crashes and feels the opposite emotion for 1–2 hours. Most common BTL on the street. RAS override frequently disabled.
- **Personafix chips (p-fixes):** Combination of simsense and skillsoft technology modifying the user's base personality. While under the chip's influence, user becomes a different person based on historical figures or pop culture icons. RAS override frequently disabled.
- **Tripchips:** Like moodchips but primarily produce sensory output (interesting and unusual sensations). Latest craze: deliberately induced synaesthesia with jacked-up sensory levels and RAS override disabled.

## Substance Abuse and Addiction

### Addiction Tests

Each substance has an Addiction Rating and Addiction Threshold (Addiction Table, p. 414). Every time a character uses an addictive substance (11 – Addiction Rating) **weeks in a row**, they must make an Addiction Test. The clock keeps ticking even if a week is skipped; going one week without the substance reduces the Addiction Threshold by 1 (returns to normal when used again). When the threshold hits 0, the character is off the hook until they use again.

**Test resolution:**

- Psychological addiction: Logic + Willpower
- Physiological addiction: Body + Willpower
- Both types: Make two tests (one psychological, one physiological)
- Threshold: Given in the Addiction Table

**Consequences of failing:**

- First failure: gain the Addiction quality (without bonus Karma). If already addicted, severity increases by one step: Mild → Moderate → Severe → Burnout.
- At Burnout: If the Addiction Test fails again, Body or Willpower (whichever is higher) is permanently reduced by 1, along with its maximum Rating. If tied, reduce Body for physiological or Willpower for psychological (if both, flip a coin). If either attribute drops to 0, character falls into a coma; fill both Condition Monitors and begin taking 1 box of overflow damage (Exceeding the Condition Monitor, p. 170).

### Addiction Levels

- **Mild:** Social use, craving "every now and then." Don't notice the problem. As manageable as addictions get.
- **Moderate:** Developed tolerance; use more frequently; others begin to notice; mood swings, drop in reliability, beginning of financial problems.
- **Severe:** Out of control, constantly strung out, every income goes to the habit. Steal, borrow from loan sharks, etc.
- **Burnout:** Long-term addict displaying physiological and psychological side effects from continual substance abuse. Life expectancy measured in weeks or days. Physiological: abscesses, infections, incontinence, other unpleasant side effects. Psychological: blackouts, flashbacks, drastic mood swings, schizophrenia, paranoia.

### Getting a Fix

Once addicted, users need a dosage on a regular basis appropriate to their Addiction quality (p. 77). To resist the craving, make a Withdrawal Test using rules for Addiction Test and applying modifiers appropriate to the addiction level.

### Withdrawal and Staying Clean

When going too long without the addictive substance, the character enters withdrawal (effects listed under the Addiction quality, p. 77). To attempt to kick the habit: stay off the substance for a number of weeks equal to the Addiction Rating, then make an Addiction Test for the substance. If successful, buy off the Addiction quality with the appropriate Karma. If failed or can't afford the Karma, the character is still in withdrawal and the process starts again.

### Overdosing

Taking a substance while already on it, or on one that shares an effect (e.g., cram and novacoke both affect Reaction), causes Stun damage with DV equal to the **sum of the Addiction Ratings** of the overlapping drugs, resisted with Body + Willpower.

## Implementation Notes

1. **Grunt Simplification:** The game explicitly wants GMs to minimize bookkeeping for grunts. The single Condition Monitor is intentional — implement it as a single `conditionMonitor` field, not separate physical/stun monitors. Track damage type only for determining alive/dead at combat end.

2. **Group Edge vs. Individual Edge:** Grunts have no `edge` attribute. The group itself has an `groupEdge` value equal to `professionalRating`. Lieutenants also draw from Group Edge, not personal Edge.

3. **Professional Rating as Social Modifier:** The Professional Rating adds directly to the group's dice pool when resisting Social Skill Tests. It also sets the Group Edge pool size.

4. **Contact Connection Cap at Creation:** The character creation validator must reject contacts with `connectionRating > 6` at game start. This cap does not apply post-creation.

5. **Critter Power Types:** Mana (M) powers cannot affect nonliving targets or astral forms of non-Awakened beings, and physical (P) powers cannot affect purely astral targets. Dual-natured critters are the exception. This requires a `powerType` field ("M" | "P") and targeting validation.

6. **Hardened Armor Implementation:** This is a two-stage check: first compare modified DV against Hardened Armor rating; if DV ≤ rating, return zero damage (no test). Only if DV > rating does a normal Damage Resistance Test occur, with `floor(hardened_armor_rating / 2)` auto-hits added to the pool.

7. **Toxin Speed Values:** Speed can be "Immediate" (same turn), "1 Combat Turn" (next turn), "3 Combat Turns" (3 turns later), "10 minutes," etc. The system should store speed as a typed value that resolves to a Combat Turn offset or clock time.

8. **Concentration Rules:** Simultaneous exposure increases Power by +1 per additional dose. Extended exposure requires re-testing at each Speed interval with cumulative +1 Power for each repeat test after the first.

9. **Drug Duration Formula:** Many drug durations are expressed as `(X – Body) hours, minimum 1 hour`. The system must apply the minimum floor when Body is high.

10. **Addiction Test Cadence:** Track `weeksOfContinuousUse` per substance. Addiction Test triggers when `weeksOfContinuousUse % (11 - addictionRating) === 0`. Each week without the substance decrements `addictionThreshold` by 1 until it returns to table value when used again.

11. **Overdose:** Only triggers when substances share an Effect attribute (e.g., both affect Reaction). The system should index drugs by their Effect attributes to detect overlaps.

12. **Vampire ESS:** The "2D6" in the stat block represents a variable Essence, not a rolled attribute for tests. Implement as a range (2–12) that decreases due to Essence Loss mechanics.

13. **Dragon Scaling:** All three dracoform types share the same base critter powers. Individual dragons may also have powers from the "Individual Powers" list. The Magician quality is mandatory for all dracoforms — they always know most spells.

14. **Essence Drain Emotional Connection:** The emotional connection requirement is not a test — it is a prerequisite condition. If the condition is not met, the power cannot be used at all. Implement as a validation check before resolving the Extended Test.
