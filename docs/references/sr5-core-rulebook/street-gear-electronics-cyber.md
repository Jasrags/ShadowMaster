## Overview

This section covers all electronic gear (commlinks, cyberdecks, RFID tags, communications countermeasures, software, skillsofts, ID/credit, tools, optical/audio devices, sensors, security gear, survival equipment, biotech), followed by the full augmentation catalog: cyberware/bioware grades, cybersurgery rules, headware, eyeware, earware, bodyware, cyberlimbs with enhancements and accessories, cyber implant weapons, standard bioware, cultured bioware, and magical equipment. ## Rules

### Commlinks

Commlinks are universal communication devices with AR Matrix capability, built-in GPS, RFID scanners, chip players, retractable earbuds, and voice-access dialing. Every commlink has a **Device Rating** that serves as its Matrix attributes (see Devices, p. 219).

**Sim Module:** Upgrade that translates computer data into neural simsense signals for direct AR/VR experience. Requires a DNI (trodes, datajack, or implanted commlink). Sim modules can be modified for **hot-sim**, which opens the full (and dangerous) range of VR experiences. Hot-sim adds +4F to Availability and +250¥ to cost.

Commlinks with a Device Rating of less than 5 are not installed inside the skull for security reasons.

### Cyberdecks

Cyberdecks (decks) are the primary hacking device. Each deck has a **Device Rating**, an **Attribute Array** (four values assigned to Attack, Sleaze, Data Processing, Firewall in any order), and a **Programs** slot count. All cyberdecks include illegal hot-sim modules by default. See **Cyberdecks**, p. 227, for full Matrix rules.

### Electronics Accessories

Unless otherwise noted, all accessories are compatible with commlinks, cyberdecks, and (with gamemaster permission) other electronic devices.

- **AR Gloves:** Manual Matrix interaction in AR mode; provide tactile force-feedback. Can provide exact weight, temperature, and hardness of objects. Device Rating 3, Avail —, 150¥.
- **Biometric Reader:** Reads fingerprints, retinas, voice patterns, tongue prints, etc. Can lock electronics to specific biometrics. Device Rating 3, Avail 4, 200¥.
- **Electronic Paper:** Foldable/rollable digital display; wireless read/write capable. Device Rating 1, Avail —, 5¥.
- **Printer:** Full-color hardcopy printer with paper supply. Device Rating 3, Avail —, 25¥.
- **Satellite Link:** Uplinks to low-Earth orbit satellites; enables Matrix access where no local wireless network exists. Limits Noise due to distance to –5. Includes portable satellite dish. Device Rating 4, Avail 6, 500¥.
- **Simrig:** Records experience data (sensory and emotive) for simsense chips. Requires working sim module with DNI to record. Device Rating 3, Avail 12, 1,000¥.
- **Subvocal Microphone:** Stage-whisper communication via throat adhesive; –4 dice pool modifier on Perception Tests to overhear when subvocalizing. Device Rating 3, Avail 4, 50¥.
- **Trid Projector:** Projects trideo hologram into a 5-meter cube adjacent to the device. Device Rating 3, Avail —, 200¥.
- **Trodes:** Headband/net electrode-and-ultrasound device providing direct neural interface without a datajack. Adds 2 Capacity slots when added to headgear. Device Rating 3, Avail —, 70¥.

### RFID Tags

RFID tags are tiny devices (Device Rating per type) that can hold one or more files. Physical location findable via Matrix (Trace Icon, p. 243). Tag data erasable with a tag eraser (p. 441) or reprogrammable via Edit File action (p. 239). Tag owners can be changed to "nobody." Sold in packs of 10.

- **Standard Tags:** Device Rating 1, general purpose. —/1¥ per 10.
- **Datachip:** Device Rating 1; no wireless capability; requires physical universal data connector to read/write. —/5¥ per 10.
- **Security Tags:** Device Rating 3; used by megacorps for employee/asset tracking; cannot be erased with a tag eraser (EMP hardened). If implanted under skin, removal requires Extended Medicine + Logic [Mental] (10, 1 minute) Test. Avail 3, 5¥ per 10.
- **Sensor Tags:** Device Rating 2; can be equipped with a single sensor of up to Rating 2 (Sensors, p. 445); records everything for up to 24 hours; programmable to shut off or overwrite older data. Avail 5, 40¥ per 10.
- **Stealth Tags:** Device Rating 3; always run silent (p. 235); Sleaze equal to Device Rating; additional –2 Concealability modifier; used as megacorp backup for security tags; can be implanted. Avail 7R, 10¥ per 10.

### Communications and Countermeasures

- **Bug Scanner (Rating 1–6):** Locates and locks wireless devices within 20 meters; measures signal strength and pinpoints location. To operate: roll Electronic Warfare + Logic [Rating]. A running-silent device uses its Logic + Sleaze to defend. Get any net hits at all, you find it. Wireless: substitute scanner's Rating for Electronic Warfare skill. Avail (Rating)R, Cost Rating x 100¥.
- **Data Tap:** Attaches to a data cable; provides direct connection to all devices on either end of the cable (Direct Connections, p. 232). Can be wirelessly commanded to self-destruct as a Free Action, immediately severing the connection (does not harm the cable). Avail 6R, 300¥.
- **Headjammer:** Neutralizes implanted commlinks when attached to the head. Works like any other jammer but effects limited to the target and their augmentations. Removing without proper key: Hardware + Logic [Mental] (8, 1 Complex Action) Extended Test, or Locksmith + Agility [Physical] (8, 1 Complex Action) Extended Test. Self-removal without proper key: Escape Artist + Agility [Physical] (4) Test, Complex Action. Avail (Rating)R, Cost Rating x 150¥.
- **Jammer, Area (Rating 1–6):** Generates noise equal to Device Rating in a spherical area; rating reduced by 1 for every 5 meters from center (similar to grenade blast rules). Affects only devices and personas within the jamming area, not walls and other obstacles. Wireless: set to not interfere with designated devices/personas. Avail (Rating x 3)F, Cost Rating x 200¥.
- **Jammer, Directional (Rating 1–6):** Affects conical area with 30-degree spread; rating reduced by 1 for every 20 meters from center. Avail (Rating x 2)F, Cost Rating x 200¥.
- **Micro-transceiver:** Short-range voice communicator within 1 kilometer; consists of ear bud and adhesive subvocal microphone (p. 439); hardto-spot designs. Wireless: range becomes worldwide. Avail 2, 100¥.
- **Tag Eraser:** Handheld strong electromagnetic field device; destroys RFID tags and unshielded electronics within 5 millimeters; deals 10 boxes of Matrix damage (resisted normally) to electronic devices in range. One charge, rechargeable at power point in 10 seconds. Wireless: recharges fully in one hour by induction. Avail 6R, 450¥.
- **White Noise Generator (Rating 1–6):** Creates field of random noise masking sounds within Rating meters; prevents direct audio surveillance. All Perception Tests to overhear a conversation within (Rating) meters receive a negative dice pool modifier equal to generator's Rating. Multiple generators: only count highest rating. Redundant in already-noisy environments; does not curtail video surveillance or jam wireless. Wireless: effective radius is tripled. Avail Rating, Cost Rating x 50¥.

### Skillsofts

A skillsoft is a recorded skill — digital knowledge and muscle memory. Used in conjunction with proper augmentations (a skilljack for knowsofts/linguasofts, a skillwire system for activesofts). Skillsofts let you know and do things you've never otherwise learned. Skill tests made with a skillsoft may not be boosted with Edge.

- **Activesofts (Rating 1–6):** Replace physical active skills (every Active skill not based on Magic or Resonance). Requires skillwire system (p. 455) to translate into usable muscle memory. Limit to number of skills usable at once based on skillwire's rating. Avail 8, Cost Rating x 5,000¥.
- **Knowsofts (Rating 1–6):** Replicate Knowledge skills; actively overwrite user's knowledge with their data. Must be accessed with a skilljack; number usable at once limited by skilljack. Avail 4, Cost Rating x 2,000¥.
- **Linguasofts (Rating 1–6):** Replicate language skills; automatically translate signals from speech cortex. Must be accessed with a skilljack; chipped speech can be awkward. Avail 2, Cost Rating x 1,000¥.

### Optical and Imaging Devices

All optical/imaging devices have wireless capability by default (can use universal data cable). Each device has a **Capacity** allowing vision enhancements to be installed. Electronic devices can take vision enhancements; optical devices (which use glass/light-bending optics) cannot take vision enhancements.

**Optical Devices (cannot take vision enhancements):**

- **Endoscope:** Fiber-optic cable at least 1 meter long; first 20 cm on each end made of myomeric rope (p. 449) and an optical lens; look around corners, under door slits, into narrow spaces; any length available. Spellcasting targeted through optics suffers –3 dice pool modifier. Avail 8, 250¥.
- **Mage Sight Goggles:** Heavy goggles connected to a myomeric rope (p. 449) wrapped around a fiber-optic cable ending in an optical lens; available in 10, 20, or 30 meter lengths. Avail 12R, 3,000¥.
- **Periscope:** L-shaped tube with two mirrors; look, shoot, or cast spells around corners. Avail 3, 50¥.

**Electronic Imaging Devices (can take vision enhancements):**

- **Binoculars (Capacity 1–3):** Handheld; built-in vision magnification. Optical (can't take vision enhancements) or electronic (can take vision enhancements). Avail —, Cost Capacity x 50¥ (electronic) or 50¥ (optical).
- **Camera (Capacity 1–6):** Captures still photos, video, and trideo including sound. Micro version available at Capacity 1. Avail —, Cost Capacity x 100¥.
- **Contacts (Capacity 1–3):** Worn directly on eyes; nearly undetectable; must be wireless (no room for universal data connector). Avail 6, Cost Capacity x 200¥.
- **Glasses (Capacity 1–4):** Lenses in lightweight frames worn on bridge of nose; hard to distinguish from prescription glasses. Avail —, Cost Capacity x 100¥.
- **Goggles (Capacity 1–6):** Bulky; strapped to head making them difficult to dislodge; widest array of vision enhancements. Avail —, Cost Capacity x 50¥.
- **Imaging Scopes (Capacity 3):** Top-mounted on weapons (Firearm Accessories, p. 431). Avail 2, 300¥.
- **Monocle (Capacity 1–4):** Worn on headband/helmet with flip-down arm, or on a chain. Avail —, Cost Rating x 120¥.
- **Micro-Camera (Capacity 1):** Compact camera. Avail —, 100¥.

### Vision Enhancements

Each enhancement has an associated Capacity cost (shown in brackets). Available for installation in visual sensors and imaging devices from contacts to cameras.

- **Low-light vision [1]:** See normally in light as low as starlight; doesn't help in total darkness. Avail +4, Cost +500¥.
- **Flare compensation [1]:** Protects from blinding flashes and simple glare; mitigates vision modifiers for glare and reduces penalty from flashing lights (e.g., flash-pak). Avail +1, Cost +250¥.
- **Image link [1]:** Display visual information (text, pictures, movies, current time, etc.) in field of vision; share tactical/situational info in real time with team; required to truly "see" AR and participate in modern world. Avail —, Cost +25¥.
- **Smartlink [1]:** Works with smartgun system; provides range to targets, ammunition level and type, heat buildup, mechanical stress, etc. A smartlink in a natural eye or in cybereyes is more effective than a smartlink in an external device (see **Smartgun System**, p. 433). Avail +4R, Cost +2,000¥.
- **Thermographic vision [1]:** See in infrared spectrum to detect heat patterns; useful for spotting living beings in areas of total darkness, checking if a motor or machine has been running. Avail +6, Cost +500¥.
- **Vision enhancement (Rating 1–3) [Rating]:** Sharpens vision at all ranges; adds Rating as positive modifier to visual Perception Tests limit. Wireless: adds vision enhancement's rating as a dice pool modifier to visual Perception Tests. Avail +Rating x 2, Cost +(Rating x 500¥).
- **Vision magnification [1]:** Digitally magnifies vision up to 50 times; allows distant targets to be seen clearly. For ranged combat rules see p. 177. Avail +2, Cost +250¥.

Sensors available in seven ratings (2–8) and two types: single and array. Sensors need to be placed in a housing or case, built into another device, or placed into a vehicle/drone's existing capacity. When using a sensor array for Perception Tests, use Electronic Warfare skill in place of Perception skill, and may use the sensor's Rating as limit.

- Atmosphere sensor: —
- Camera: —

- Laser microphone: 100 meters

**Sensor Function Descriptions:**

- **Atmosphere sensor:** Weather/atmospheric analysis; keeps you from getting caught in rain with up-to-the-second analysis of the air.
- **Cyberware scanner:** Millimeter-wave scanner primarily to detect cyber-implants; can identify other contraband. Max range 15 meters.
- **Geiger counter:** Picks up radioactivity surrounding it.
- **Laser range finder:** Emits laser beam reflected off target; calculates exact distance. Max range 1,000 meters.
- **MAD scanner:** Magnetic Anomaly Detection; detects weapons and concentrations of metal. Max range 5 meters.
- **Motion sensor:** Uses ultrasound and low-power infrared to detect motion and drastic ambient temperature changes. Max range 25 meters.
- **Olfactory scanner:** Picks up and analyzes molecules in the air; works like olfactory booster cyberware.
- **Ultrasound:** Emitter and receiver; creates topographic ultrasound map; perfect to "see" textures, calculate distances, detect objects invisible to naked eye (cloaked people); can't penetrate glass; can be set to passive mode (doesn't emit but picks up external ultrasound sources). Max range 50 meters.

**Shock hand:** Shock glove built into palm; perfect for inflicting a nasty shock to anything it comes into contact with; deals Electricity damage (p. 170); ten charges in each hand; plugged in they recharge at one charge per ten seconds.

### Bioware

Bioware is subtler, more holistic, and less invasive than cyberware, at the cost of being substantially pricier. Augments the body's own functions and integrates transplanted organs that function as natural features. Fine balance of homeostasis between the body's organic systems must be maintained. Bioware is more expensive monetarily, costs less Essence, and is much harder to spot. Bioware has **no wireless capability at all**.

**Standard Bioware:**

- **Adrenaline Pump (Rating 1–3):** Enhanced gland implanted in lower abdomen connected to both supradrenal glands. When dormant: reservoir for adrenaline. When activated, small muscular sac contracts sending a surge of concentrated adrenaline into the blood stream. Physical and emotional stress can force the pump to activate if you fail a Composure Test (p. 152); otherwise trigger as a Free Action. While active: ignore injury modifiers; don't fall unconscious even if Stun Condition Monitor is filled; Rating added to Strength, Agility, Reaction, and Willpower attributes; pump works for Rating x 1D6 Combat Turns (can't be switched off early). When time's up, you crash and take Stun damage equal to the number of Combat Turns the pump was active (use natural Body + Willpower to resist the damage). After effects end, attributes return to normal and you stop ignoring injury modifiers or unconsciousness from Stun damage. After use, pump requires 1 hour to regenerate; during that time, it cannot be activated. ESS Rating x 0.75, Avail (Rating x 6)F, Cost Rating x 55,000¥.
- **Bone Density Augmentation (Rating 1–4):** Molecular structure of bones altered to increase density and tensile strength; ligaments strengthened; increased bone density increases your weight a bit; Body attribute increased by the bone density Rating for purpose of damage resistance tests; also deal Physical damage in unarmed combat based on Rating (see Bone Density Augmentation table). Incompatible with other augmentations to the bones, including bone lacing cyberware. ESS Rating x 0.3, Avail (Rating x 4), Cost Rating x 5,000¥. Unarmed combat damage by rating: Rating 1: (STR)P; Rating 2: (STR+1)P; Rating 3: (STR+2)P; Rating 4: (STR+3)P.
- **Cat's Eye:** Transgenic vat-grown implants; literally cat's eyes with vat-grown DNA profile and occipital connecting structures; amplifies light and enhances night vision; provides low-light vision; slit and reflective just like a cat's. Obviously not compatible with cyberware eye enhancements or replacements. ESS 0.1, Avail 4, 4,000¥.
- **Enhanced Articulation:** Joint-surface coating, re-lubrication, tendon and ligament augmentation for more fluid muscle and joint action; +1 dice pool modifier to Escape Artist skill and +1 increase to Physical limit (cumulative with other limit modifiers). ESS 0.3, Avail 12, 24,000¥.
- **Muscle Augmentation (Rating 1–4):** Biological weaving treatment that enhances existing muscle tissue rather than replacing it; muscle cables woven into existing muscle fibers; adds Rating to Strength. Incompatible with augmentations that increase Strength, including muscle replacements cyberware. ESS Rating x 0.2, Avail (Rating x 5)R, Cost Rating x 31,000¥.
- **Muscle Toner (Rating 1–4):** Increases muscle fiber elasticity in existing muscle tension, resulting in increased muscle tension and flexibility; adds Rating to Agility. Cultured bioware must be tailor-made for the body in which it will eventually find a home. More expensive and takes longer to acquire than off-the-shelf stuff.

## Implementation Notes

### Essence and Capacity

- Essence cost deducted from character's Essence (starting 6.0). When Essence drops to 0 or below, the character dies.
- Cyberlimb enhancements cost Capacity from the cyberlimb, not Essence directly.
- Items with Capacity costs in brackets [n] can be installed in a cyberlimb, spending that Capacity instead of Essence.
- Partial cyberlimbs (hand/foot, lower arm/leg) only apply their enhanced attributes to tests directly involving those limbs.

### Grade Multipliers

Apply grade multipliers at time of purchase. Alphaware/betaware/deltaware require a contact (Street Index or specific vendor), not just nuyen. Only Standard, Alphaware, and Used are available at character creation.

### Wireless Bonuses

Almost all cyberware has wireless capability except bioware (which has **none**) and data locks (not wireless enabled). Wireless bonuses are only gained when the wireless function is active. Items with wireless bonuses can still gain their full listed functionality if wireless is disabled; they simply lose the wireless bonus.

### Commlink vs. Cyberdeck

A commlink is used for everyday Matrix activities; a cyberdeck is required for hacking (running silent, using attack/sleaze programs, etc.). A commlink installed in the head (headware commlink) comes with a sim module at no additional cost.

### Cyberlimb Attribute Rules

- When attacking with a cyberarm, use the cyberarm's STR/AGI attributes.
- When using multiple limbs for a single task, average all limbs involved.
- When careful coordination of several limbs is required, use the weakest limb.
- Partial limbs count as half a limb for the purpose of extra Physical Condition Monitor boxes.

### Sensor Rules

- When using a Sensor Array for Perception Tests, use Electronic Warfare instead of Perception; use sensor's Rating as the limit.
- Sensors must be placed in a housing; the housing's type determines the maximum sensor rating.

### RFID Tags

- Standard and Datachip tags: Device Rating 1; standard RFID tags are erasable; security tags are NOT erasable with a tag eraser (EMP hardened).
- Sensor tags record everything for up to 24 hours; programmable to shut off or overwrite older data.
- Stealth tags run silent and have Sleaze equal to their Device Rating.

### Slap Patches vs. Injection

- Slap patches are applied to willing patients or require a successful melee attack against unwilling patients (Called Shots rules apply).
- Tranq patches are resisted with Body only (not Willpower).

### Bioware Special Requirements

- Symbiotes require special food costing Rating x 200¥ per month (covered by High Lifestyle or better).
- Suprathyroid Gland requires eating twice as much as normal and increases lifestyle costs by 25%.

> **Ambiguity:** The bone density augmentation table shows unarmed combat damage values but the bodyware table lists essence cost as "Rating x 0.3" and avail as "(Rating x 4)" — both reference the same Rating but the availability modifier is not marked with R or F, suggesting it may be unrestricted (no restriction letter). Verify with other sourcebooks if the restriction letter is intentionally absent.

> **Ambiguity:** The Reaction Enhancers wireless bonus states the total bonus Reaction from reaction enhancers and wired reflexes combined "can be above +4 if both systems have wireless active." The standard +4 maximum applies when either or both systems do not have wireless active. This interaction should be tracked in character state.
