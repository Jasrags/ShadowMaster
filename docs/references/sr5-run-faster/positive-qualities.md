# Positive Qualities

**Source:** Run Faster, pp. 144-150
**PDF Pages:** 146-152

---

## Overview

Run Faster expands the positive quality catalog from SR5 core. These qualities can be taken at character creation (p. 62, SR5), bought during play with Karma (p. 104, SR5), or assigned by the gamemaster. All still require GM approval. The standard 25 Karma limit on positive qualities at creation applies unless using an alternate creation method.

---

## Rules

### Adrenaline Surge (12 Karma)

Allows the character to act first in the first Initiative Pass of a new combat, even without the highest Initiative Score. If multiple characters have Adrenaline Surge or use Edge to Seize the Initiative in the same pass, they act in order of their Initiative Scores. Does not allow a Surprised character to act first in an ambush, but allows a non-surprised character to strike first.

### Animal Empathy (3 Karma)

+2 dice pool modifier for all tests involving the influence or control of an animal (including riding), including Awakened species.

### Black Market Pipeline (10 Karma)

At character creation, choose one existing contact and one merchandise category (vehicles, weapons, electronics, armor, etc.). That contact always buys/sells in that category at better-than-normal rates. Purchases: 10% price reduction and +2 dice pool modifier for Availability Tests. Sales: character gets (7% x Loyalty) of original value instead of the standard 5%. Contact must be appropriate to the category.

### Born Rich (5 Karma)

Character came from an affluent background. During character creation, may exceed the normal 10 Karma limit for trading Karma to nuyen, instead trading up to 40 Karma total at the same 2,000 nuyen per Karma rate, for up to 80,000 nuyen of additional funds. Available only during character creation.

### City Slicker (7 Karma)

+1 dice pool modifier for all Outdoors skill group tests in an urban environment. Downside: -1 dice pool modifier to Perception and Survival Tests in any non-urban environment.

> **Cross-reference:** Opposite of Outdoorsman (p. 148, Run Faster). Mutually exclusive in practice since they provide opposing bonuses.

### College Education (4 Karma)

During character creation, buy Academic Knowledge skills at half price (rounded up). After character creation, Academic Knowledge skill ranks of 3 or higher receive a 1 Karma cost reduction per rank.

### Common Sense (3 Karma)

GM acts as an inner voice of reason, warning the character before foolish actions. The GM may issue a number of warnings per session equal to or less than the character's Edge rating.

### Daredevil (6 Karma)

Whenever the character performs an exceptionally daring action (GM's discretion), they regain two points of Edge instead of one.

### Digital Doppelganger (7 Karma)

The character's digital identity has been cross-linked with someone else's identity. Anyone researching the character has difficulty finding accurate information and faces a +2 threshold for Matrix Searches tracking the character on this ID. Requires SINner quality; must be linked to a particular SIN.

### Disgraced (2 Karma)

Character is a disgraced former authority figure. +2 dice pool modifier to intimidate criminals (gangers, organized crime, etc., as determined by GM). However, upright citizens treat the character with disdain, treating them as Prejudiced in any Etiquette Test.

### Erased (8 Karma)

Character's SIN was actively and thoroughly erased. Legwork can only be done via word-of-mouth contacts; Matrix searches turn up nothing. Prevents Public Awareness from ever going above 1. Downsides: cannot maintain a Lifestyle higher than Middle; cannot use a fake SIN or attached licenses for more than three months. If the character has a High Lifestyle for a month, they must use Low or lower for at least a month afterward, or risk the systems failing and building Public Awareness.

### Fame (4 to 16 Karma)

Fame grants benefits in certain social circles and additional income (if character also has Day Job), but increases recognizability and Public Awareness. Characters must have the SINner quality or a Rating 3 fake SIN. Characters can use a disguise/mask; identifying the star becomes a Disguise + Intuition [Mental] Opposed Test vs Perception + Intuition [Mental].

- **Local Fame (4 Karma):** Select a single sprawl. +1 Social Limit, +1 dice pool modifier on Social Skill Tests to positively influence those who know of the fame. Identified by Intuition + Logic (2) Test in home sprawl, (1) outside. Public Awareness +2. Day Job: income x2.
- **National Fame (8 Karma):** Select a nation and sprawl of origin within it. Requires 4+ ranks in native language of the nation. In selected sprawl: +1 Social Limit, +2 Social Skill modifier. In chosen nation: +2 Social Limit, +1 Social Skill modifier. Identified by Intuition + Logic (2) Test or any rank in appropriate Knowledge Skill. Public Awareness +3. Day Job: income x4, +10% workload.
- **Megacorporate Fame (12 Karma):** Choose a home megacorporation, home sprawl, and home nation. In home megacorp: +2 Social Limit, +2 Social Skill modifier. In home nation/sprawl: +1 Social Limit, +1 Social Skill modifier. Citizens of home megacorp/nation identify with Intuition + Logic (1) Test; others with (2) Test. Public Awareness +5. Day Job: income x6, +10% workload.
- **Global Fame (16 Karma):** +3 Social Limit, +3 Social Skill modifier to positively influence anyone who knows. Identified by Intuition + Logic (2) Test or any rank in appropriate Knowledge Skill. Public Awareness +8. Day Job: income x10, +25% workload.

> **Ambiguity:** The text says Fame "offers benefits within certain social circles and additional income if the character also chooses the Day Job quality" but does not explicitly state Fame requires Day Job -- it just synergizes with it.

### Friends in High Places (8 Karma)

Additional pool of Charisma x 4 in Karma for purchasing contacts at character creation. Restrictions: none of these contacts can have a Connection Rating less than 8; any leftover Karma is lost; these contacts cannot be called at every turn.

> **Cross-reference:** See Who You Know chapter (p. 172) for contact abuse rules.

### Hawk Eye (3 Karma)

+1 dice pool modifier to Perception Tests. Shift all Range Environmental modifiers up one category (e.g., Medium becomes Short). Part of the character's natural eyes; incompatible with electronic vision enhancements and cyber-/bioware augmentations or replacements.

### Inspired (4 Karma)

Character gains +2 Street Cred (p. 372, SR5) among fellow artists. +1 dice pool modifier for all Artisan skills OR all Performance skills (choose one category at purchase; cannot be changed). Quality can only be selected once.

### Jack of All Trades, Master of None (2 Karma)

Karma costs for learning skills are reduced by 1 (minimum 1) up to Rating 5. Beyond Rating 5, it costs 2 additional Karma per point of skill. Does not apply during character creation.

### Lightning Reflexes (20 Karma)

+1 to Initiative rating and a bonus initiative die. Not cumulative with any other Initiative enhancement (technological, chemical, or magical). +1 dice pool modifier for all Defense Tests. Requires unaugmented character.

> **Ambiguity:** "Not cumulative with any other Initiative enhancement" -- unclear whether this means the quality is lost if augmentation is later acquired, or simply doesn't stack. Given the high Karma cost and "unaugmented" requirement, implementation should prevent purchase if character has any Initiative-enhancing augmentation.

### Linguist (4 Karma)

Halve the learning time for languages. +1 dice pool modifier to all Language skill tests. At character creation, purchase Language skill points at 2-for-1 rate. After character creation, language skill ranks of 3 or higher receive a 1 Karma cost reduction per rank.

### Made Man (5 Karma)

Select a crime syndicate as a free Group Contact (see Group or Organization Contact Options, p. 176). Loyalty starts at 3 instead of 1. Can use the syndicate as a fence (30% of item value for stolen goods) or source for stolen/restricted goods (10% price reduction and +1 Availability modifier). Obligation: participate in syndicate work (~20 hours/week in standard campaign, one session per four weeks in Missions).

> **Cross-reference:** See Group or Organization Contact Options (p. 176).

### Night Vision (2 Karma)

Grants low-light vision equivalent. Downside: extremely light-sensitive eyes. Character suffers severely from glare (Blinding Glare on a clear day, Moderate Glare on overcast, etc.) per Environmental Compensation Table (p. 175, SR5). Can mitigate with sunglasses. Incompatible with cyber-/bioware augmentation or replacement (if augmented, quality is lost with no Karma refund).

> **Cross-reference:** See Environmental Compensation Table (p. 175, SR5).

### Outdoorsman (3 Karma)

+2 dice pool modifier for all Outdoors skill group tests in a rural or wild environment. Downside: -1 dice pool modifier to Perception and Survival Tests in an urban environment.

> **Cross-reference:** Opposite of City Slicker (p. 145, Run Faster).

### Overclocker (5 Karma)

Add 1 point of Rating to one of the character's cyberdeck's ASDF attributes. This point can be reallocated as desired any time the cyberdeck is reconfigured.

### Perceptive (5 or 10 Karma)

Available at two levels. For 5 Karma: +1 dice pool modifier on all Perception Tests (including Astral and Matrix Perception). For 10 Karma: modifier increases to +2.

### Perfect Time (5 Karma)

Character always knows the exact time. Can only be obstructed by prolonged isolation, unconsciousness, distortion of temporal perception through drugs/chips, or extended time in the Matrix. Provides: +1 dice pool modifier for Performance Tests involving timing and rhythm; one additional Free Action every Action Phase.

### Poor Link (8 Karma)

-2 dice pool modifier for the Ritual Spellcasting Test during Step 7 (Seal the Ritual) for all ritual sorcery directed at the character, including friendly rituals. +2 dice pool modifier for any Resistance Tests the character makes against rituals, including friendly rituals.

> **Ambiguity:** The quality penalizes ALL rituals directed at the character, including beneficial ones. Implementation should apply the modifiers uniformly regardless of intent.

### Privileged Family Name (7 Karma)

Character's real name carries significant social clout in home sprawl (selected at creation). Functions as a get-out-of-jail-free card for minor misdemeanors. Local minor NPCs suffer a -2 dice pool modifier on Social tests against the character. Requires SINner (National or Corporate) quality. Drawback: the family name makes the character very easy to identify.

### Restricted Gear (10 Karma per item)

At character creation: purchase one piece of gear with Availability up to 24 (bypassing the normal creation Availability limit). After character creation: purchase one piece with Availability up to 18, but at +35% cost. Can be taken up to 3 times total; only one purchase can occur at character creation.

### School of Hard Knocks (4 Karma)

During character creation, buy Street Knowledge skills at a rate of 2-for-1. After character creation, Street Knowledge skill ranks of 3 or higher receive a 1 Karma cost reduction per rank.

### Sense of Direction (3 Karma)

Character always knows true north and can retrace their path. Can estimate distances traveled accurately within a few meters while alert and aware. +1 dice pool modifier on Navigation skill tests.

### Sensei (5 Karma)

Character has a teacher for a chosen skill or skill group. Requires a contact with Connection Rating 3+. For a single skill: Sensei has skill at Rating 13, Instruction Test dice pool of 10, limit of 7. For a skill group: Sensei has skill at 12, Instruction Test dice pool of 12, limit of 8. Sensei charges nothing but should generally be available (GM's discretion).

### Solid/Legendary Rep (2 or 4 Karma)

Reputation bonus within a specific group (1,000-5,000 members). Solid (2 Karma): +1 Reputation with that group. Legendary (4 Karma): +2 Reputation. Can only be taken once.

### Speed Reading (2 Karma)

Read a full page of text (~800 words) in about 5 seconds, or an 800-page textbook in about an hour. Information is not memorized (unless character has Photographic Memory, p. 76, SR5). To locate specific information while reading: Logic + Intuition Extended Test with threshold and interval set by GM based on text length, complexity, and obscurity.

> **Cross-reference:** See Photographic Memory (p. 76, SR5) for memorization interaction.

### Spike Resistance (10 Karma per rating, max 3)

+1 dice pool modifier per level to resist damage from harmful biofeedback (black IC, dump shock, black hammer, etc.).

### Spirit Whisperer (8 Karma)

Spirits add 1 die when resisting Summoning Tests from this character. However, if the character succeeds, the spirit summoned is always 1 Force stronger than declared. For all purposes, the character is considered to have summoned the spirit at the declared Force. Summoned spirits behave curiously toward the character (plot hook for GM).

### Steely Eyed Wheelman (2 Karma)

When making Vehicle Tests, Terrain Modifiers are reduced by 1 to a minimum of 0.

### Technical School Education (4 Karma)

During character creation, buy Professional Knowledge skills at a rate of 2-for-1. After character creation, Professional Knowledge skill ranks of 3 or higher receive a 1 Karma cost reduction per rank.

### Tough as Nails (5 Karma per level, max 4)

Each level provides one additional box to either the Physical or Stun Condition monitor, up to a maximum of 3 additional boxes on any one Condition monitor.

> **Ambiguity:** The quality can be taken up to 4 times at 5 Karma each, but no single Condition monitor can gain more than 3 boxes. This means at least 1 box must go to the other monitor if all 4 levels are taken.

## Implementation Notes

- **Education qualities** (College Education, School of Hard Knocks, Technical School Education) follow an identical pattern: 2-for-1 skill points at creation, 1 Karma reduction per rank for ranks 3+ post-creation. Implement as a single parameterized handler with knowledge skill category as the variable.
- **Fame** is a single quality with tiered levels, not four separate qualities. Store as one quality with a `level` property. Each level has distinct Social Limit bonuses, Social Skill modifiers, Public Awareness increases, and Day Job income multipliers.
- **City Slicker / Outdoorsman** are functional mirrors. Both modify the Outdoors skill group in one environment and penalize Perception/Survival in the other. Consider implementing as a single "environment affinity" quality with a parameter.
- **Born Rich** only affects the Karma-to-nuyen conversion cap during character creation. No ongoing mechanical effect.
- **Restricted Gear** needs a counter (max 3) and a flag for whether the character-creation purchase has been used.
- **Tough as Nails** needs tracking per-monitor (Physical vs Stun) with a cap of 3 per monitor and 4 total.
- **Perfect Time** grants an additional Free Action per Action Phase -- this interacts with the action economy and should be surfaced in the combat tracker.
- **Overclocker** adds a virtual rating point to a cyberdeck ASDF attribute. This needs to integrate with the deck configuration UI and be reallocatable.
- **Spirit Whisperer** modifies summoning outcomes (summoned spirit is Force+1). This affects drain calculation since drain is based on declared Force, but the spirit acts at Force+1.
