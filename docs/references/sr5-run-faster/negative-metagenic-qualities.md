# Negative Metagenic Qualities

**Source:** Run Faster, p.119-123
**PDF Pages:** 121-125

---

## Overview

Negative Metagenic Qualities are the downsides of SURGE, representing harmful or socially stigmatizing mutations. They provide bonus Karma during changeling character creation, which must be balanced against Positive Metagenic Qualities. Many of these qualities reference the Freaks sidebar (p. 123), which imposes cumulative social penalties.

---

## Freaks Sidebar Rule (p. 123)

Every quality that references the Freaks sidebar imposes: -1 dice pool modifier to all Social-based tests per quality taken. For every 3 such qualities, Social Limit decreases by 1. This is the core social penalty mechanic for visibly mutated changelings.

---

## Rules

### Adiposis (10 Karma)

Clinically obese (>30% body fat) due to metabolic/genetic dysfunction. Movement rates reduced to: Walking (Agility x 1), Running (Agility x 2), Sprinting (+0.5m/hit). -1 dice pool modifier to all physical activities including combat. Out of breath faster: double the DV for Fatigue Damage (p. 172, SR5) from strenuous activities. Halve duration between Resistance Tests.

### Astral Hazing (5 Karma non-Awakened, 15 Karma Awakened)

Character generates tainted astral space. Becomes an aspected domain affecting all magic in vicinity. Creates a Rating 3 background count (p. 30-32, Street Grimoire) that extends Essence x 100 meters from the character. Background count also impairs Awakened character's own actions. If stationary, background count expands by 1 meter per 4 hours (GM discretion, max +4 expansion).

> **Ambiguity:** The text says the background count "impairs her own actions if the character is Awakened" but does not specify the exact penalty beyond the standard background count rules in Street Grimoire.

> **Cross-reference:** Background count rules in Street Grimoire pp. 30-32 define the mechanical penalties per rating level.

### Berserker (6 Karma)

Prone to primal rage from hormonal/psychological imbalance. Character has no control over triggering. GM may trigger rage when character is emotionally agitated; character may attempt Composure (3) Test (p. 152, SR5) to suppress. While enraged: +1 to all Physical attributes, -1 to all Mental attributes (min 1). Duration: 1D6 Combat Turns. Attacks anyone nearby including friends. If character has adrenaline pump (p. 459, SR5), the berserker rage always triggers the implant.

### Bioluminescence (5 Karma)

Hair/skin produces luciferin creating green-to-blue glow. Too weak to see in normal light. In partial light or full darkness, bright enough to spot the character, reducing visibility modifiers by one level. Sufficient to enable low-light vision characters to reduce visibility modifiers by one level as well.

### Cephalopod Skull (6 Karma)

Kraken-like head: nacre-like substance, soft epidermis, tiny tentacles covering shoulders/upper thorax. Skull and upper torso are soft: -3 dice pool modifier when resisting damage in this region. Inflicts social interaction modifiers (Freaks sidebar, p. 123).

### Cold-Blooded (5 Karma)

Blood runs cold (snake-like). Temperature-dependent penalties:

- Below 30 degrees Celsius: -1 to all Physical attribute tests
- Below 20 degrees Celsius: -3 to all Physical attribute tests
- Below 10 degrees Celsius: -5 to all Physical attribute tests
- At 0 degrees Celsius: character ceases to function, enters comatose state until warmed

Body temperature close to air temperature: -2 dice pool modifier against detection by thermographic vision or thermal sensitivity.

### Critter Spook (5 Karma)

Presence/smell threatens animals and paranormal critters within 5 meters. Animals react with fear and hostility: -2 dice pool modifier to control, soothe, or pacify them. In combat, critters will go out of their way to attack this character first.

### Cyclopean Eye (6 Karma)

Single huge eye above nose (instead of two). Lacks binocular vision (no stereopsis/parallax). -1 dice pool modifier to all Combat Tests and all technical/physical skill tests requiring precision (GM discretion). Cannot compensate with a cybereye (lacks neural connections for binocular processing). Characters suffer Freaks social modifiers (p. 123).

### Deformity (5 or 15 Karma)

Abnormal and asymmetrical facial/body features. Two severity levels:

- **Picasso (5 Karma):** Severe facial deformity (e.g., eyes on one side, nose centered on forehead). -2 dice pool modifier to Perception (if sensory) or -2 dice pool modifier to Physical Active Tests (if motor, called Quasimodo).
- **Quasimodo (15 Karma):** Significant physical deformity. Same as Picasso but characters who do not hide abnormalities suffer double Freaks modifiers. Cannot be surgically corrected (complications halt procedure). Can be bought off with Karma.

### Feathers (3 Karma)

Body hair replaced by feathers (fine/downy or fluffy/vibrant). +1 dice pool modifier to all Matrix Search Tests and tests to identify/locate the character. No protective value. Incompatible with Unusual Hair and Scales. Characters suffer Freaks social modifiers (p. 123).

### Impaired (Attribute) (8 Karma)

One Mental or Physical attribute is Impaired. Permanently reduce one metatype attribute maximum by 2 (e.g., human max 6 becomes 4). Also decreases augmented maximum by the same amount. Once per attribute. If taken during play, may reduce current rating if new max is lower.

> **Ambiguity:** The text says "permanently reduce one of the character's metatype attribute maximums by 2" -- this affects both the natural maximum and the augmented maximum. Implementation must validate that current attribute rating does not exceed the new maximum.

### Insectoid Features (6 Karma)

Insectoid features (compound eyes, mandibles, insect wings -- cosmetic only, no flight). Resemblance to insect spirits causes Hostile social modifier (p. 140, SR5). In combat, people may attack this character first. +1 dice pool modifier to all Matrix Search Tests and tests to identify/locate. Characters seriously affect social interactions (Freaks sidebar, p. 123).

### Mood Hair (4 Karma)

Hair changes color based on emotional state (clearly visible, cannot be hidden). +2 dice pool modifier to Judge Intentions Tests (p. 152, SR5) against the character if observer is aware of this quality. Incompatible with any hair modification and the Feathers and Scales qualities.

### Neoteny (6 Karma)

Halted physical development; character retains child/adolescent appearance (up to ~13 years old). Physical Condition Monitor reduced to 6 + (Body/2, rounded up). Custom armor/equipment at +10% cost. GM may modify Social Tests based on NPC interactions. Characters suffer Freaks social modifiers (p. 123).

### Nocturnal (4 Karma)

Abnormal circadian rhythm; sleeps during daytime, active at night (crepuscular schedule also possible). All Mental attributes reduced by 1 during daylight hours due to weariness.

### Progeria (9 Karma)

Accelerated aging syndrome. Character has only a few more years to live (GM determines exact number) unless infected by HMHVV or undergoes life-prolonging treatment. Physical development halted at early stage (small stature, childlike features, wrinkled skin -- may be mistaken for child, similar to Neoteny). Atherosclerosis and cardiovascular problems: -2 dice pool modifier to all Physical Tests.

### Scales (5 Karma)

Scaly skin (lizard/snake-like). May cover entire body or sections. Periodically shed. No protective value. +2 dice pool modifier to all Matrix Search Tests and tests to identify/locate. Incompatible with bioware skin augmentations and other skin-related qualities. Characters suffer Freaks social modifiers (p. 123).

### Scent Glands (4 Karma)

Foul odor (rotten eggs/skunk spray). Stinky at best, highly offensive under stress. Difficult to remove from clothing. +1 dice pool modifier to locate character by scent (+2 when stressed). -1 dice pool modifier to all Social Tests (-2 when stressed) whenever odor is not masked.

### Slow Healer (3 Karma)

Recuperates sluggishly from wounds, fatigue, pathogen, and toxin effects. -2 dice pool modifier to all Healing Tests.

### Striking Skin Pigmentation (4 Karma)

Skin takes on unusual color (patches or entirely -- orange, blue, golden, leopard spots, etc.). Obvious and visible. +2 dice pool modifier to all Matrix Search Tests and tests to identify/locate. Incompatible with bioware skin augmentations.

### Stubby Arms (10 Karma)

Skeletal dysplasia causing short arms. -1 Reach penalty for all Melee and Unarmed combat tests. -1 dice pool modifier to all non-Combat Tests requiring limberness/dexterity with arms or hands (GM discretion).

### Symbiosis (5 Karma)

Character bonds to a location after sustained residence (>1 season, GM determines exact period). Body + Willpower (3) Test each month to bond; if failed, character feels incomplete (-1 dice pool modifier on the test if unwilling to bond). Once bonded: +1 dice pool modifier to Outdoor Skill Group and all Social Tests with local residents. +1 to Healing Tests in neighborhood. Intuitively senses local conditions (environmental problems cause persistent Mild Allergy). Bond broken only by moving away for a full month, then Body + Willpower (3) Test in new location.

> **Ambiguity:** The bonding range is listed as "Essence x 100 meters" for Awakened characters but the exact range for non-Awakened is not clearly stated beyond "permanent residence."

### Third Eye (3 Karma)

Third eye in middle of forehead. No vision modifiers (can perceive depth with one eye closed). Detectable with Perception (2) Test even when closed. Incompatible with Cyclopean Eye (p. 120). Characters incur Freaks social modifiers (p. 123).

### Unusual Hair (3 Karma)

Hair changes to unusual color, texture, or grows in unusual patterns/areas (lion mane, downy fur, monkey-like hanuman fur). +1 dice pool modifier to all Matrix Search Tests and tests to identify/locate. Incompatible with other natural or artificial hair modifications.

### Vestigial Tail (6 Karma)

Normal or stumpy non-functional tail (scaly, hairy, or hairless). Can be wagged slightly. -1 dice pool modifier to all Physical Tests involving balance or movement (jumping, climbing, running, etc.). Incompatible with Functional Tail (p. 115), cybernetic, or bioware tails. Characters suffer Freaks social modifiers.

## Implementation Notes

- **Freaks sidebar** is a cross-cutting concern: implement as a computed property that counts all qualities referencing Freaks and applies cumulative -1 Social dice pool modifier per quality and -1 Social Limit per 3 qualities.
- **Cold-Blooded** requires environmental temperature tracking or GM input to determine which penalty tier applies.
- **Berserker** is a combat-phase triggered effect requiring GM intervention (Composure test) -- model as a condition with duration tracking (1D6 Combat Turns).
- **Impaired (Attribute)** must validate that the new reduced maximum does not fall below the current attribute rating; if it does, the current rating must also be reduced.
- **Astral Hazing** cost depends on whether character is Awakened (15 Karma) or not (5 Karma). The Awakened version is much more expensive because it also impairs the character's own magical actions.
- **Deformity (Quasimodo)** doubles Freaks modifiers -- implement by multiplying the per-quality Social penalty by 2 for this quality specifically.
- **Adiposis** modifies movement rates to fixed formulas (not modifiers to existing rates): Walking = AGI x 1, Running = AGI x 2, Sprint = +0.5m/hit.
- **Nocturnal** and **Progeria** apply flat penalties that should be modeled as persistent conditions.
- Negative metagenic qualities that reference Freaks: Cephalopod Skull, Cyclopean Eye, Feathers, Insectoid Features, Neoteny, Scales, Third Eye, Vestigial Tail, and Deformity (Quasimodo at double rate).
