# Creating Something Different

**Source:** Run Faster, p.100-110
**PDF Pages:** 102-112

---

## Overview

This section covers character creation rules for metavariants (subraces of the five standard metatypes), metasapients (non-metahuman sapient beings), shapeshifters (Awakened animals that shift into metahuman form), and changelings (metahumans affected by SURGE). Character creation follows standard priority procedures with modifications: additional Karma costs, different attribute ranges, unique racial traits, and (for changelings) metagenic quality selection.

---

## Rules

### Metavariant Character Creation

Character creation follows the same procedures as standard metatypes with one change: some options require starting Karma to be spent for the additional abilities these characters possess. The Karma cost for playing a certain option does not count against the character's Positive Quality limit, though some options require taking Negative Qualities to afford the Karma buy-in. Metavariant attribute ranges replace the base metatype ranges.

### Metasapient and Shapeshifter Magic

Metasapients and shapeshifters who select Priority A, B, C, or D for Magic have their natural Magic of 1 replaced with their new Magic attribute. Character options that have a natural Magic attribute cannot also have a Resonance attribute. Special attribute points can increase the natural Magic attribute of a metasapient or shapeshifter, and Magic can also be increased through Karma purchase as normal advancement. If Magic is reduced to 0 through Essence loss, the character cannot use any abilities tied to Magic per SR5 p.278.

### Creating a Shapeshifter

The player creates the animal first using the Shapeshifter Attribute Table. To determine metahuman attributes, note how many attribute points were put into each attribute; those same points are applied to the base metahuman attributes. At character creation, the shapeshifter chooses a metahuman form and pays the Karma cost from the Shapeshifter Metahuman Form Cost table (Human is default, costs 0). This Karma cost is in addition to any Karma cost for playing certain shapeshifter species from the Extended Priority Charts.

### Shapeshifter Dual Attributes

Once character creation is complete, animal and metahuman attributes are treated as separate scores. Increasing one form's attribute does not change the other form's attribute. Racial traits are a special case: most transfer from natural form to metahuman form, with the exception of Natural Weapon (Bite) and Goring Horns (unless the character is a troll or other variant with naturally large horns). Natural Weapon (Claws) takes the form of extremely hard and sharp fingernails and toenails in metahuman form.

### Shapeshifter Edge

All shapeshifters have an Edge attribute range of 1 to 4.

### Shapeshifter Negative Qualities

Negative Qualities such as Uneducated can be bought off with Karma, but a good story reason is needed (e.g., canine shifters who live among people don't have Uneducated because they learn alongside humans).

### Creating a Changeling

SURGE can happen to anyone of any metatype. Creating a changeling requires choosing a SURGE class:

- **Class I** (10 Karma): Limited to metahuman and minor animal qualities. Player rolls all qualities randomly. Roll random Positive Qualities (max total 30 Karma), then roll random Negative Qualities to balance.
- **Class II** (15 Karma): Broader traits including those outside metahumanity. Player selects Positive Qualities to desired Karma level, then rolls Negative Qualities randomly to balance.
- **Class III** (30 Karma): Most extreme changes, often themed (catperson, lizardperson, etc.). Player selects the full range of Positive and Negative Qualities. Both Positive and Negative should relate to the overall theme.

### Changeling Quality Balancing

When selecting metagenic qualities, the combined Karma values must either balance exactly or err in favor of a single point of Karma on the Positive Quality side. The player must pay for any imbalance with Karma from their free Karma pool.

> **Ambiguity:** The text says qualities must "balance exactly or err in favor of a single point of Karma on the Positive Quality side" -- it is unclear whether this means Positives can exceed Negatives by exactly 1 Karma, or Negatives can exceed Positives by exactly 1 Karma. The example on p.103 shows Positives (24) exceeding Negatives (20) by 4 Karma, which contradicts the "single point" rule. The example resolution suggests the player must pay the difference from free Karma or adjust qualities.

### SURGE Class Thematic Rules

- **Class I:** No need to connect Positive and Negative Qualities to the same metatype or animal classification. Totally random, limited to metahuman and minor animal qualities.
- **Class II:** More diverse, includes traits outside metahumanity. No need to connect qualities, but thematic connection tends to be more likely.
- **Class III:** Positive and Negative Qualities should all relate to an overall theme (e.g., all reptilian, all avian).

### Random Metagenic Quality Selection

To randomly determine metagenic qualities: (1) Roll 1D6 on Random Positive Karma Value table to get a Karma range. (2) Consult the corresponding Positive Metagenic Qualities table for that range and roll the specified dice to determine the quality. (3) Roll 1D6 on Random Negative Karma Value table. (4) Consult the corresponding Negative Metagenic Qualities table and roll.

### Pre-Change Demographics

Humans are most likely to SURGE (59%), followed by orks (16%), trolls (10%), dwarfs (9%), and elves (6%).

## Implementation Notes

### Metavariant Implementation

- Metavariants are children of base metatypes. Each has its own attribute min/max ranges and racial traits that replace (not supplement) the parent metatype's values.
- Attribute format in table is "min/max" (e.g., "1/4" means min 1, max 4).
- Racial traits listed per metavariant should be stored as an array of trait references.

### Shapeshifter Dual-Form System

- A shapeshifter character needs TWO complete attribute sets: animal form and metahuman form.
- The metahuman form's base attributes come from the chosen metahuman form's metatype/metavariant base, plus the same number of attribute points allocated in animal form creation.
- The `abilitiesNotPresent` field in the Shapeshifter Metahuman Form Cost table indicates racial traits that do NOT transfer to metahuman form.
- Treat `Shift (Metahuman Form)` as a universal shapeshifter racial trait.
- Movement values in the shapeshifter table use format: (walking/running/+swimming) in meters per turn.

### Extended Priority Charts

- These charts extend the SR5 core priority table. Each entry shows the metatype/variant name, special attribute points in parentheses, and the additional Karma cost.
- The Karma cost here is separate from the Shapeshifter Metahuman Form Cost -- both must be paid.
- Shapeshifter entries group multiple breeds at the same cost tier (e.g., "Bovine; Vulpine" share a row).

### Changeling System

- Changeling is an overlay on any metatype, not a separate metatype. The character's base metatype is unchanged.
- SURGE class determines: (1) Karma cost, (2) Karma limit for metagenic qualities, (3) selection method (random vs. chosen).
- The metagenic quality random tables use different dice per tier (3D6, 4D6, 2D6) -- implementation must use the correct die type per table.
- Some qualities offer player's choice between options (e.g., "Gills (Aqua or Air, player's choice)") -- these should present a sub-selection.
- Quality balancing logic: sum of Positive quality Karma values must approximately equal sum of Negative quality Karma values, with at most 1 Karma difference favoring Positives. Any excess Positive cost is paid from free Karma.

### Data Model Considerations

- Metavariants need a `parentMetatype` field linking to the base metatype.
- Shapeshifters need `animalForm` and `metahumanForm` attribute blocks.
- Changelings need a `surgeClass` (1, 2, or 3) and a `metagenticQualities` array.
- The extended priority charts should extend the existing priority system, not replace it.
