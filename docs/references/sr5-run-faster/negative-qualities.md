# Negative Qualities

**Source:** Run Faster, pp. 151-159
**PDF Pages:** 153-161

---

## Overview

Negative qualities provide bonus Karma at character creation and can also be earned during gameplay. Buying off a negative quality requires GM approval and costs the quality's Karma value in earned Karma (p. 106, SR5). Some qualities require completing in-game goals before they can be bought off.

---

## Rules

### Albinism (4 or 8 Karma)

Partial or complete lack of pigmentation in eyes, skin, and hair. All ethnicities, metatypes, and metavariants can manifest albinism.

- **Weak Glare penalty** to all actions in regular indoor lighting and during bright but overcast days.
- **Moderate Glare penalty** when working on a sunny day.
- All other Glare modifiers increased by one level versus albino characters. Normal compensation methods (sunglasses, etc.) work.
- Sunburn resistance tests have halved duration between tests (p. 148, Run & Gun).
- Characters who gain cybereyes only gain 4 Karma at character creation. If cybereyes are gained during play, must buy down to 4 Karma level immediately; cannot spend Karma on anything else until quality is bought down.

> **Cross-reference:** Sunburn rules, Run & Gun p. 148.

### Amnesia (4 or 8 Karma)

Memory loss from injury, magic, drugs, or unknown causes. Two levels:

- **Surface Loss (4 Karma):** Classic amnesia -- character retains skills and abilities but has no memory of who they are or their past. Knowledge skills are absent; to access a Knowledge skill, the character must buy it (2 Karma for an additional rank if already on their list, normal cost if not). GM determines backstory based on player's chosen knowledge skills and reveals information as it becomes relevant.
- **Neural Deletion (8 Karma):** Extreme memory loss. Character starts with very limited knowledge. Physical Attributes, skills, qualities, and even Edge start as mysteries revealed during play. Requires GM collaboration; character creation is largely in GM's hands.

> **Ambiguity:** Neural Deletion says "Mental Attributes, skills, qualities, and even Edge should start as a mystery" but doesn't specify exactly which stats are hidden from the player vs. the character. Implementation should flag this as a GM-adjudicated quality.

### Asthma (8 Karma)

Chronic asthma causing wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing. Additional and more rapid effects from all forms of Fatigue. When considering a Fatigue damage effect, the character faces the damage twice as often and suffers additional effects as soon as they start taking damage per the Asthma Effects Table.

**Asthma Effects Table (all effects are cumulative):**
| Damage Boxes | Effects |
|---|---|
| 1 box | Wheezing; -1 dice pool modifier to all Physical Actions; Social Limit decreased by 1 |
| 2 boxes | Shortness of breath; -1 dice pool modifier to all Actions; Social Limit decreased by 1 additional point |
| 4 boxes | Chest tightness; further Fatigue damage resisted with only Willpower |
| 8 boxes | Wracking cough; -1 dice pool modifier to all Actions; Social Limit decreased by 1 additional point |

> **Cross-reference:** Fatigue Damage, SR5 p. 172.

### Bi-Polar (7 Karma)

Mental state fluctuates between depression, stability, and mania. GM rolls 1d6 at session start and whenever there is a shift in activity (at least once per day):

- **Manic (1-2):** +1 dice pool modifier to Agility/Reaction tests; -2 dice pool modifier to Logic/Intuition tests.
- **Depressed (3-4):** -2 dice pool modifier to Agility/Reaction/Logic/Intuition tests.
- **Stable (5-6):** No modifiers.
- Medication available at 500 nuyen/month (requires prescription and SIN). Missing meds for 12+ hours triggers a GM die roll. Street cost: 100 nuyen/dose.

### Big Regret (5 Karma)

Character has a dark secret from their past. Social Limit decreased by 3 against anyone who knows about their past. Could result in blackmail. Cannot buy off quality until secret becomes public, at which point character gains 1 point of Notoriety and must buy off quality ASAP.

### Blind (15 Karma; 5 Karma for characters with access to Astral Sight)

Character is completely blind. Automatically fail all vision-based Perception Tests.

- -4 dice pool modifier to general Perception tests
- -3 dice pool modifier to Surprise Tests
- Blind Fire in Ranged Combat, Total Darkness in Melee Combat
- Character cannot use cybereyes (blindness is a nerve issue)
- Awakened characters who are blind can use astral perception but apply -2 dice pool modifier to all actions on the physical plane while perceiving the astral (including indirect combat spells)

> **Ambiguity:** The 5 Karma cost for Awakened characters with Astral Sight makes this significantly cheaper but the text says GMs should "apply the customary -2 dice pool modifier." Unclear if this -2 replaces or stacks with the -4 general Perception penalty.

### Borrowed Time (20 Karma)

Character has a fatal condition (disease, poison, cortex bomb, etc.) with no set time limit. At the beginning of each game session, GM secretly rolls three dice -- a deadly combination of three dice means the character dies at a dramatically appropriate moment that session. Quality cannot be bought off. If the player wants the character to survive, the character must burn all current Edge (not before); quality then goes away.

> **Ambiguity:** "A deadly combination of three dice" is not precisely defined. The text says "three of a kind" (three dice in secret; three of a kind means times up). Implementation should treat as: GM rolls 3d6, if all three match, character's time is up.

### Computer Illiterate (7 Karma)

Character has no experience with AR, computers, commlinks, or electronic devices. -4 dice pool modifier to all tests involving a computer, electronic device, or Matrix-connected system. During stressful situations, GM may require a Success Test (with the -4 modifier) for tasks others take for granted (making commcalls, sending messages, doing a Matrix search).

### Creature of Comfort (10, 17, or 25 Karma)

Character is accustomed to a specific Lifestyle level:

- **10 Karma:** Middle Lifestyle
- **17 Karma:** High Lifestyle
- **25 Karma:** Luxury Lifestyle
- For every day spent "slumming" in a lower Lifestyle category, the character suffers -1 penalty to all Social and Healing tests per Lifestyle category below their chosen level.

### Day Job (5 to 15 Karma)

Character has a regular job with time commitments that conflict with shadowrunning. Requires a valid SIN (SINner quality or at least Rating 4 fake SIN). If they have SINner quality, income is considered already taxed. Losing their job costs 2 Street Cred and a month's salary in lost purchases.

**Day Job Table:**
| Karma | Salary/Month | Hours/Week |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 1,000 nuyen | 10 |
| 10 | 2,500 nuyen | 20 |
| 15 | 5,000 nuyen | 40 |

- GM tracks consecutive days spent shadowrunning. If character can't fulfill day job responsibilities: first offense = warning, second = fired.
- Combined with Fame quality, could provide enough income to support the character.

### Deaf (15 Karma)

Character is completely deaf and can perceive no sound at all. Automatically fail all audio-based Perception Tests. -2 dice pool modifier to general Perception tests. -3 dice pool modifier to Surprise Tests. Other modifiers at GM's discretion.

### Did You Just Call Me Dumb? (3 Karma)

Character camouflages insults into well-spoken words. Any Glitch rolled by this character on any Social test always counts as a Critical Glitch.

### Dimmer Bulb (5 Karma per level, max 3 levels)

Character is not mentally sharp. For every level: -1 dice pool modifier on all tests involving Logic or Intuition.

### Driven (2 Karma)

Character has an obsession driving them forward. When faced with a clue or opportunity to advance their quest, must make Willpower + Logic (4) Test to not immediately drop everything to pursue it. While actively working a lead: +1 to Willpower.

### Emotional Attachment (5 Karma)

Character is irrationally attached to a specific piece of gear. Will always use this item and will risk life (and teammates' lives) to retrieve it if lost. If irretrievably lost/damaged: must immediately spend Karma to buy off quality OR suffer -1 penalty to all tests that would have used that gear for 6 months. After 6 months, quality transfers to a new item.

### Ex-Con (15 Karma)

Character is fresh out of prison and on parole. Three major drawbacks:

- **Criminal record:** Local police and prison corporation have full file (augmentations, associates, biometrics, etc.) connected to a new Criminal SIN. Cannot have Restricted or Forbidden augmentations (prison removes them).
- **Parole:** Must check in with parole officer (PO) twice a week via Matrix, once a week in person. PO can make house calls anytime.
- **Known ex-con:** Street-level contacts associate freely. Corp contacts require Loyalty 4+. Law enforcement contacts require Loyalty 5-6.
- Cannot be bought off; must be replaced with other negative qualities of equal or greater value if parole ends.

### Flashbacks (7 or 15 Karma)

Character suffers vivid memory-based sensory hallucinations triggered by a specific stimulus (any sensory input including astral/Matrix). When triggered: Composure (5) Test or become incapacitated for (5 - hits) Combat Turns. During flashback, character cannot perform useful actions.

- **7 Karma:** Stimulus comes up once every other shadowrun.
- **15 Karma:** Stimulus appears at least once every game session.

### Hobo with a Shotgun (10 Karma)

Character will not stay in accommodations above Squatter lifestyle. If forced to stay somewhere above Squatter: -2 to all Mental attributes, confused, erratic, and agitated until they can spend a day in Squatter or Street lifestyle to rezone.

### Hung Out to Dry (8 Karma)

Character's contacts have suddenly stopped responding. Unknown to the character, they are a social pariah. GM knows the reason; character must figure it out. Contacts may clam up if other team members ask about it. Must buy off quality or replace with another negative quality of equal value once situation is resolved.

### Illiterate (5 Karma)

Character cannot read. Social Limit reduced by 1. -2 dice pool modifier when working with computer/commlink systems and electronics (unfamiliarity with iconography). During character creation, cannot choose Knowledge skills requiring reading. During gameplay, all Knowledge skills requiring reading cost double Karma until quality is bought off.

### In Debt (1 to 15 Karma)

Character owes money to an unsavory third party. For every point of Karma spent on In Debt, character gets 5,000 nuyen to spend during character creation (up to 75,000 nuyen max).

- Owes borrowed amount + 50%, increasing by 10% every month until paid off.
- Missing a monthly payment: 1 box Physical damage per 20,000 nuyen owed (can't be resisted or healed until payment is made).
- Can be bought off if character has both enough Karma and cash. If not enough Karma, can trade for another negative quality of equal Karma.

### Incomplete Deprogramming (10 Karma)

Character was mentally programmed as a deep-cover agent; deprogramming wasn't completed. Has occasional, unexpected shifts to cover identity.

- Stressful situations (interrogation, combat, injury): Composure (4) Test or cover identity takes control for 1D6 minutes (or GM-determined duration).
- Multiple stressful situations can trigger additional Composure (4) Tests and personality shifts in the same encounter.
- During cover identity states: character forgets runner identity and skills, reverting to mild-mannered NPC. GM may create a secondary character sheet for these episodes.

### Infirm (5 to 25 Karma)

Diminished physical fitness. For every 5 Karma, all Physical Attribute maximums drop by 1. Can be taken up to 5 times. No Attribute maximum can drop below 1. Character can never have a Physical Attribute modified beyond this maximum by any source (magic, machine, or otherwise).

> **Cross-reference:** Stacks with Aged quality from Bullets & Bandages (p. 12). Characters must be careful not to drop any Attribute maximum lower than 1.

### Liar (7 Karma)

Character is a compulsive liar. -1 dice pool modifier to all Social skill tests. Whenever addressing someone, GM rolls 1D6: on a result of 1, the target assumes the character is lying regardless of truth. Any NPC who loses trust this way maintains mistrust; on a second offense, things go wrong on a roll of 1 or 2. On a third offense: +1 Notoriety, -1 Loyalty for contacts (Loyalty 0 = contact lost).

### Night Blindness (6 Karma)

Character's natural vision does not adjust to poor lighting. All Light/Glare Environmental modifiers are one category worse, except Full Light/No Glare (which remains unmodified). Incompatible with any quality affecting the character's eyes. Must be immediately bought off if corrective cyber- or bioware is installed. If character lacks Karma, corrective gear cannot be obtained.

### Oblivious (6 or 10 Karma)

Character fails to notice surroundings.

- **6 Karma:** -2 dice pool modifier to all Perception Tests (standard, Astral, and Matrix).
- **10 Karma:** All Perception Test thresholds are increased by 1.

### Pacifist (10 or 15 Karma)

Moral inflexibility preventing violence.

- **10 Karma:** Avoids violence not in self-defense. Will not participate in wetwork. Non-lethal attacks still considered "violent." May only use violence in self-defense after an opponent has clearly attacked the character, teammates, or bystanders.
- **15 Karma:** Will not commit any violence regardless of provocation. Any act of violence causes -1 dice pool modifier to all Mental attribute tests until a Charisma + Willpower (20, 1 day) Extended Test succeeds. Failure within 7 days: lose 1 point of Willpower or Charisma (permanent, whichever is higher). If character kills (or thinks they did): -2 modifier, Extended Test interval becomes 1 week, Mental Limit decreased by 1.

### Paranoia (7 Karma)

Character truly believes everyone is out to get them. -2 dice pool modifier on all Social Tests involving contacts with Loyalty less than 4 or any generally unfamiliar person. Refuses to give out address or location information. Must change living spaces every few months.

### Paraplegic (10 Karma)

Character is paralyzed from the waist down. Can perform physical tasks not requiring legs; locomotion via wheelchair or wheelchair drone. Walking speed = Agility x 3, running speed = Agility x 4 (regardless of metatype -- fast/agile wheelchairs). Trouble with stairs and curbs.

- Paralysis can be permanent or temporary (building funds for surgery to buy off quality).
- Lifestyle cost increased by 10% (cumulative with racial modifiers). Vehicles need modification: +5% price or Rigger Interface (p. 461, SR5) for DNI control.
- Quality does not affect astral or Matrix abilities.

### Phobia (5 to 15 Karma)

Character has a visceral fear triggered by exposure to a specific stimulus. Karma cost based on frequency + severity.

**Phobia Table (Frequency):**
| Condition | Karma |
|---|---|
| Uncommon (relatively rare, e.g., smell of roses, specific insects) | 2 |
| Common (commonly encountered, e.g., sunlight, trolls, insects, magic, outdoors, crowds) | 5 |

**Phobia Table (Severity):**
| Severity | Karma | Effects |
|---|---|---|
| Mild | 3 | -1 dice pool modifier to all actions |
| Moderate | 5 | -3 dice pool modifier to all actions; Composure (2) Test or must get away from source |
| Severe | 10 | -6 dice pool modifier; Composure (5) Test or must flee for (5 - hits) Combat Turns |

> **Ambiguity:** Total Karma = Frequency Karma + Severity Karma. So a Common + Moderate phobia = 5 + 5 = 10 Karma. The header says "5 to 15 Karma" which matches the range of Uncommon+Mild (2+3=5) to Common+Severe (5+10=15).

### Pie Iesu Domine. Dona Eis Requiem. (2 Karma)

Character is a flagellant. Gains High Pain Tolerance 1 but always starts the day with 1 box of Physical damage. This box can be healed, but the character will seek to cause themselves another box of damage as soon as possible. Wounds may cause social issues.

### Poor Self Control (4 to 12 Karma)

Various mental ticks reflecting inability to control actions. Karma cost varies by subtype based on the Composure threshold. Can be taken multiple times (once per form).

- **Braggart (5 Karma):** Must always try to top others' claims of success, to the point of lying. Without a Composure (3) Test, won't back down from the brag. May be ignored, considered infantile, or challenged.
- **Thrill-Seeker (4 Karma):** Always takes the most dangerous/risky option unless Composure (2) Test succeeds. Payoff: +1 Initiative Score for 5 Combat Turns when pursuing the thrill.
- **Compulsive (4 to 12 Karma):** Must exert sense of order on everything. Composure (2) Test to resist. Base Karma = 2 x threshold for Composure Test (1-4); then add 2 for personal environment, 3 for single public aspect, or 4 for broad public environment.
- **Vindictive (5 Karma):** Must always consider escalation/retaliation. Composure (2) Test to resist urge for instant payback. Even when resisted, must add offender to hit list.
- **Combat Monster (10 Karma):** Loses self-control once a fight begins. Fights until all opponents are disabled, even if outnumbered and losing, unless Composure (3) Test succeeds to break off.

### Records on File (1 Karma per Rating, max Rating 10)

One of the Big 10 megacorps has up-to-date records on the character (SIN, biometrics, personal/medical data). The megacorp's agents get +2 dice pool modifier to identify the character through their records. Representatives of the selected megacorp also get +2 dice pool modifier to track/locate the character in areas with C or better security rating. Cannot have Records on File and Erased at the same time.

### Reduced (Sense) (2 to 20 Karma)

One of the character's five senses (or astral sight for Awakened) is dulled. -2 dice pool modifier to tests involving the chosen sense. Can be taken multiple times (different sense each time); effects cumulative for tests involving multiple senses (e.g., general Perception Tests).

- Smell or Taste: 2 Karma each
- Hearing, Sight, or Astral Sight: 5 Karma each
- Touch: 10 Karma each (affects most Physical skill rolls)
- If sense is repaired/corrected permanently, quality must be bought off.
- Cannot have Reduced (Sight) and Blind at the same time.
- Cannot have Reduced (Hearing) and Deaf at the same time.

### Sensory Overload Syndrome (15 Karma)

SOS -- epileptic-like episodes triggered by high ARO saturation or sensory enhancement systems. Willpower + Edge (4) Test or enter epileptic seizure for (5 - hits) minutes. Can possibly be prevented by deactivating enhancement systems, commlink, or running in hidden mode (but hidden mode creates identification issues).

> **Cross-reference:** Related to AIPS (Augmented Intelligence Processing Syndrome).

### Signature (10 Karma)

Character feels compelled to leave a calling card (item, symbol, technique) at every job. Anyone who knows the character and their signature can easily identify their participation. Tests to identify/track the character get a dice pool modifier equal to the character's Street Cred and Public Awareness combined.

### Vendetta (7 Karma)

Character is in a blood feud with an individual or group. Extreme difficulty resisting confrontation with the foe. When encountering the target: Composure (3) Test or must incite a violent confrontation. If the character neutralizes their nemesis: buy off quality or someone new picks up the vendetta.

### Wanted (10 Karma)

Character has a bounty or contract on their head worth at least 25,000 nuyen. Must frequently deal with bounty hunters or people trying to collect. If bounty is cleared (turned in, name cleared), quality must be bought off with Karma.

## Implementation Notes

- **Karma cost structures vary significantly:** Some qualities are fixed cost (Driven: 2), some are tiered (Creature of Comfort: 10/17/25), some are per-level (Dimmer Bulb: 5/level, max 3), some are calculated (Phobia: frequency + severity), and some are per-rating (Records on File: 1/rating). The data model needs a flexible cost representation.
- **Composure Tests are the primary resistance mechanic** for behavioral qualities (Driven, Flashbacks, Pacifist, Poor Self Control, Vendetta, Incomplete Deprogramming). Track Composure threshold per quality for automated prompts.
- **GM-adjudicated qualities** (Amnesia: Neural Deletion, Borrowed Time, Hung Out to Dry, Big Regret) have mechanics that are largely narrative. Flag these as requiring GM involvement rather than attempting full automation.
- **Bi-Polar state tracking** requires a session-level state machine with three states (manic/depressed/stable) and dice roll triggers. The medication mechanic adds a secondary tracking requirement.
- **Asthma interacts with Fatigue damage** -- implementation needs to hook into the Fatigue damage system to double frequency and add cumulative penalties from the Asthma Effects Table.
- **In Debt** creates a character creation Karma-to-nuyen conversion (5,000 nuyen per Karma point) with ongoing debt tracking (principal + 50% + 10%/month compounding). This is a financial subsystem.
- **Phobia** Karma is a composite: frequency (2 or 5) + severity (3, 5, or 10). Implementation should present this as two dropdowns rather than a single value.
- **Poor Self Control** sub-types are independent and can each be taken once. Compulsive has a further variable cost based on scope (personal environment +2, single public aspect +3, broad public +4).
- **Incompatibility pairs:** Blind/Reduced (Sight), Deaf/Reduced (Hearing), Records on File/Erased, Night Blindness/any eye-affecting quality. Implement as validation rules.
- **Pacifist 15 Karma** has a complex cascading penalty system: violence triggers a -1 modifier, killing triggers -2, with Extended Tests of different intervals and permanent attribute loss on failure. This is one of the most mechanically complex negative qualities.
