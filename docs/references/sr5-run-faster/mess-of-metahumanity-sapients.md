# The Mess of Metahumanity -- Sapients of the Sixth World

**Source:** Run Faster, p.88--107
**PDF Pages:** 90--109

---

## Overview

This section catalogs all playable metavariants (subspecies of the five core metatypes), metasapients (non-metahuman sapient species), and shapeshifters (sapient paranormal animals that can assume metahuman form). Each entry provides attribute ranges, racial traits, and character creation costs. The section also covers changelings (SURGE-affected individuals) and their metagenic quality system, plus extended priority charts for all non-standard options.

---

## Rules

### Creating a Metavariant Character (p.102)

- Character creation follows standard procedures with one change: some options require part of the starting Karma to be spent on additional abilities.
- The Karma cost for playing a certain option counts toward the Negative Quality Karma limit allowed.
- The Karma cost does NOT count against the Positive Quality limit.
- Some options require taking Negative Qualities to afford the Karma buy-in.

### Creating a Metasapient or Shapeshifter (p.102)

- Metasapients and shapeshifters who select Priority A, B, C, or D for Magic have their natural Magic of 1 replaced with the new Magic attribute from their priority selection.
- Character options with a natural Magic attribute cannot also have a Resonance attribute.
- Special Attribute Points can be used to increase the natural Magic attribute of a metasapient or shapeshifter.
- Magic can be increased through Karma purchase as part of normal advancement.
- If Magic is reduced to 0 through Essence loss, the character loses all magic-tied abilities (see SR5 p.278).

### Creating a Shapeshifter (p.102--103)

- The player creates the animal form first using the Shapeshifter Attribute Table.
- To determine metahuman attributes: note how many attribute points were put into each attribute, then apply those same points to the base metahuman attributes.
- The shapeshifter chooses a metahuman form at character creation and pays the Karma cost from the Shapeshifter Metahuman Form Cost table (Human = 0 Karma).
- Once creation is complete, animal and metahuman attributes are treated as separate scores; increasing one does NOT change the other.
- Racial traits are a special case: most transfer from natural form to metahuman form, with the exception of Natural Weapon (Bite) and Goring Horns (unless the character is a troll or variant with naturally large horns).
- Natural Weapon (Claws) takes the form of hard, sharp fingernails and toenails in metahuman form.
- Negative Qualities like Uneducated can be bought off with Karma if justified by backstory.
- All shapeshifters have an Edge attribute range of 1 to 4.

### Creating a Changeling (p.103)

- SURGE can happen to anyone. Creating a changeling requires choosing a SURGE class:
  - **Class I:** 10 Karma -- minor traits usually associated with metatypes (pointed ears, tusks, thermographic vision, dermal deposits). Player selects all qualities randomly.
  - **Class II:** 15 Karma -- broader alternative phenotypic expressions (mammal, amphibian, reptile, bird, fish traits). Player selects Positive Qualities freely, then rolls Negative Qualities randomly.
  - **Class III:** 30 Karma -- most radical changes, may seem like an entirely different species. Player selects full range of Positive and Negative Qualities freely.
- The chosen class indicates the Karma limit of chosen metagenic qualities and determines SURGE variability.
- Combined Positive and Negative Metagenic Quality Karma values must either balance exactly or err by a single point of Karma on the Positive side; the player pays any imbalance from free Karma.
- Pre-change demographics for SURGE: humans 59%, orks 16%, trolls 10%, dwarfs 9%, elves 6%.

### SURGE Pre-change Demographics

- Humans: 59%
- Orks: 16%
- Trolls: 10%
- Dwarfs: 9%
- Elves: 6%

> **Cross-reference:** Metagenic Positive and Negative Qualities are detailed in the same chapter, p.111--123.

## Metavariant Summaries

### Dwarfs (Homo sapiens pumilionis)

#### Gnome

- Smaller than standard dwarfs (~85 cm, ~30 kg). Lacks body hair except head hair. Dense musculature on small frame.
- Resistance to arcane castings (source unverified).

#### Hanuman

- Named for Hindu god. Elongated limbs, feet, hands; profuse lower body hair; prehensile tail; agile frame. Excellent climbers.
- Decreased mental aptitudes in modern testing (cultural bias possible).

#### Koborokuru

- Japanese variant. Almond-shaped eyes, less body/facial hair, lower muscle density/bulk, lower muscle tone and metabolic rate.
- Small increase in arcane potential; high tendency toward mystic adept split potential.

#### Menehune

- Hawaiian/Transoceanian variant. Thicker/fuller body hair, lighter wiry beard, broader/flatter nose, nictitating membrane (perfect underwater vision, salt water eye protection), thinner extended foot structure with webbed toes.
- Decreased muscle density vs. base pumilionis; thin insulating fat layer. No inherent toxin resistance.
- Higher EQ; significant increase in adept population.

### Orks (Homo sapiens robustus)

#### Hobgoblin

- Shares low-light vision trait of robustus. Shorter, less muscular, smaller frame. Green skin hue, decreased body hair. Enlarged lower canines, broad jaw, sharp teeth, narrow tapered jaw. Eyes completely black (no distinction between pupil, cornea, sclera). Minimal body hair.
- Quicker temper (hormonal). Enhanced EQ scoring.

#### Ogre

- Shorter stature, smoother skin, protruding jaw, wider mouth. Height similar to standard H. sapiens. Muscular robustness closer to H. sapiens progenitors.
- Skin is thick and rough. Can digest almost anything they swallow.
- Lower mental acuity/intellect vs. base robustus. Excel in tests of will.

#### Oni

- Japanese variant. Enhanced vision and muscular bulk of robustus, plus variations in skin tone, eye structure, ear shape, and horns similar to ingentis.
- Skin tones: bright red, blue, or orange only. Protuberant eyes. Greatly extended aural helix (similar to nobilis). Horns rise vertically.
- Similar intellect to base metatype but greater EQ and force of personality.

#### Satyr

- Mediterranean origin. Low-light vision, varies in facial shape, leg structure, body mass, horns.
- Caprine (goat-like) leg and foot structure with cloven hooves. Exceptional running speed and enhanced vertical/horizontal jumping.
- Above average EQ, score well on adaptive thinking tests.

### Trolls (Homo sapiens ingentis)

#### Cyclops

- Single eye, larger than standard ingentis eyes, centered on forehead. Greater muscular bulk, minimal hair growth, variable horn growth, lack dermal deposits.
- Brain lacks structures for binocular vision; limited depth perception for nearby objects, distance evaluations difficult.
- Scores more poorly than base ingentis on standardized testing. Lower EQ (anti-social tendency).

#### Fomorian

- Celtic origin (British Isles). Standard thermographic vision, increased muscular bulk, pronounced horns, enlarged lower canines. Shorter stature (~2.35 meters average), lack of dermal deposits. Retains dermal deposits of base ingentis (makes them appear less "monstrous").
- Higher EQ than primary subspecies. Greater percentage of Awakened individuals. More resistant to arcane effects including spells.

#### Giant

- Scandinavian origin. Same increased muscular bulk, enlarged lower canines, thermographic vision. Greater height (average 3.0 meters, tallest recorded 3.65 meters), increased epidermal density and hair growth, fewer dermal deposits. Thickened skin (rhinoceros/bark-like density).
- Unique: high frequency of genetic reversion to sapiens species (1/4 of female giant offspring are sapiens expression; only occurs in females).
- Mental acuity tends to be underestimated; average slightly lower than other metatypes but often brighter than expected.

#### Minotaur

- Greek/Mediterranean origin. Enhanced muscle mass, thermographic vision. Wide flattened nasal structure, wide-set eyes, symmetrical horns resembling a bull's face. Horns longer, set at forward angle, anchored into thick bone plates (nearly 4x denser than standard ingentis horns). ~10 cm shorter than standard trolls. Profuse body hair, distinct lack of dermal deposits.
- Score within same ranges as base ingentis. Higher adaptive thinking and EQ (narrower range). Reputation for stubbornness and strong will.

### Elves (Homo sapiens nobilis)

#### Dryad

- Resembles standard nobilis with slight decrease in stature. More slender and fair-haired.
- Most intelligent of all nobilis metavariants. Often perceived as graceful and physically attractive.
- No standardized test data available (all anecdotal).

> **Ambiguity:** Dryads have refused standardized testing. The claim that non-Awakened dryads may possess glamour that magically influences others is unverified.

#### Nocturna (Night Ones)

- Short, dark fur covering entire body and face (similar to black panther genus). Fur usually black but may range deep blue to violet. Increased body hair growth, increased auditory sensitivity, hypersensitivity to bright light promoting nocturnal bio-rhythm.
- Same intelligence ranges as primary nobilis. Slight negative EQ shift; many antisocial/introverted scores.
- High occurrence of Awakened members.

#### Wakyambi

- Southern African origin. Taller and thinner than primary nobilis. Dark skin, tightly curled dark hair, less pronounced aural helix extension. Substantial height increase (can reach heights similar to ingentis giant metavariant).
- Most possess natural low-light vision (not thermographic).
- Indicators suggest higher rate of Awakened (not statistically confirmed).

#### Xapiri Thepe

- South American origin. Same primary physical characteristics of nobilis with slight decrease in average height. Epidermal layer integrates chloroplast-containing cells enabling photosynthesis. Patches grow with age; oldest subjects completely green-skinned.
- Test results similar to primary nobilis with notable decreases in intellect and increases in adaptive thinking (possible cultural testing bias).

### Humans (Homo sapiens sapiens)

#### Nartaki

- Indian origin. Variable skin color (blue, red, gold) and an additional set of upper limbs (4 or 6+ arms total). Fully functional extra appendages.
- No variation in test scores from base sapiens.
- Appeared during SURGE cycle of 2061. A mana pulse during Year of the Comet/Holi celebrations instantly boosted population by ~500 individuals. All new nartaki were female.

## Metasapients

### Centaur (Equus sagittarius) (p.98)

- Equine lower body with hominid torso. Height: 2.7--3.2 meters. Weight: 400--1000 kg.
- Three digits plus opposable thumb per hand; skull shape varies within species.
- Two variations: common centaur (equine head, shortened) and "lesser centaur" (human head, rarely reach adulthood, often cast out).
- **Population:** 50,000 common; 1,500 lesser.
- **Population Centers:** Eurasian Steppe, North American refuge (Dunkelzahn), parts of Greece and Czech Republic.
- **Status:** Recognized as sapient by Native American Nations, Greece, Czech Republic, Amazonia, Yakut, Mongolia. Evo is only megacorp offering citizenship.

### Naga (Custos serpens) (p.98--99)

- Intelligent, ten-meter-long Awakened snake species. Cold-blooded physiology (climate restricted).
- Can speak metahuman languages with only a slight speech impediment (hard consonants).
- **Population:** 190,000.
- **Population Centers:** Amazonia, Naga Kingdom of Angkor Wat, Indian Union.
- **Status:** Eligible for SINs in Amazonia, Bangla Commonwealth, Burma, Indian Union, Laos, Malaysia, Manchuria, Salish-Shidhe Council, Sichuan, Singapore, Sri Lanka, Vietnam. All Big Ten employ significant naga populations (typically magical security).

### Pixie (Insufficient Data for Classification) (p.99)

- Miniature elf-like humanoids with wings. Under 0.5 meter tall. Wingspan ~1 meter.
- Wings resemble iridescent dragonfly wings (variations include bat, bird of prey, butterfly, grasshopper patterns). Flight uses innate mana manipulation, not aerodynamics.
- Possess innate magical concealment ability. Immediately disappear at moment of death (complicates taxonomy).
- **Population:** Unknown.
- **Population Centers:** Broceliande Forest (France), United Kingdom, Tir na nOg, Tir Tairngire, Ukraine, Duchy of Pomoyra, Black Forest Troll Republic.
- **Status:** France is only nation issuing permanent SINs. Tir Tairngire, UK, Tir na nOg, UCAS issue criminal SINs only. NeoNET and Evo are only megacorps with pixie corporate citizens.

### Sasquatch (Pesvastus pilosis) (p.99--100)

- Three meters tall, muscular build similar to trolls. Covered in fur (black, brown, pale yellow, auburn; silver tips with age).
- Omnivorous. Lack ability to speak; audio mimicry ability used for hunting.
- Successfully entered metahuman society in entertainment, politics, diplomacy.
- **Population (2075):** 40,000.
- **Population Centers:** Native American Nations, Nepal, Tibet, UCAS, Yakut.
- **Status:** UN recognized as sapient species in 2042. Eligible for SINs in most nations and megacorporations.

## Shapeshifters (p.100--101)

Shapeshifters are sapient paranormal animals with the innate magical ability to assume metahuman form. Their natural forms are nearly identical to mundane counterparts but usually more physically impressive. Distinctive traits in metahuman form can sometimes identify a shifter.

- Lifespan greater than base animal; no greater pattern determined across species.
- Can breed with base animals and same-species shifters; genetics tests reveal very little difference.
- No shapeshifters originate from Awakened animal species.
- **Population:** 200,000 (divided among various species).
- **Population Centers:** Amazonia, Azania, Aztlan, Salish-Shidhe, Yakut.
- **Status:** NAN states, Amazonia, Azania, Aztlan recognize sapience and issue SINs. Many countries offer bounties for their destruction. Awakened Yakut (former Russian republic) is the only nation with a strong political force of shapeshifters.

### Shapeshifter Breeds

- **Bovine:** Cows, bison, cape buffalo, brahmin. Broad noses, flaring nostrils, broad shoulders in human form.
- **Canine:** Medium and large breed dogs only (no poodle/chihuahua). Enlarged canines, extensive body hair, resemblance to Canis familiaris.
- **Equine:** Drawn-out chin, long features. Hairline extends between shoulder blades in narrow strip (mane-like).
- **Falconine:** Various birds of prey (vulture, eagle, falcon, hawk). Dark eyes, sharp features, claustrophobia tendency.
- **Leonine:** Rare (dwindling lion population). Most from southern African nation of Azania. Tawny hair, dark African complexion.
- **Lupine:** Northern Hemisphere ("wherever wolves roam"). Prominent eyebrows, hairy hands/palms/knuckles.
- **Pantherine:** Mid-size great cats (jaguar, leopard, puma, ocelot). Retain catlike iris and reflective layer in eyes; traces of natural coat pattern in hair and skin.
- **Tigrine:** Central and eastern Asia. Retain catlike eyes and natural hair color with hint of stripes.
- **Ursine:** Bear species, Northern Hemisphere. Excessive body hair, robust build, fat over solid muscle.
- **Vulpine:** Fox breed, primarily Japan/China (rare in Europe/North America). Unique: retention of fox tail in metahuman form (often concealed under long coats).

## Changelings (p.101)

- First appeared in 2061 with UGE, Goblinization, and SURGE (Sudden Unexplained Recessive Genetic Expression).
- Changes caused by metagenic traits damaged over millennia activating when mana spikes.
- Changeling traits are autosomal (not sex-linked) and can pass to offspring. Offspring may express traits of either parent, both, or neither.
- Three classes based on extent of change: Class I (minor), Class II (moderate), Class III (extreme, may cause mental health issues).

## Implementation Notes

### Metavariant Character Creation

- Metavariants use the **same priority system** as core metatypes but reference the Extended Priority Charts (p.106--107) instead of the SR5 core book charts.
- The `additionalKarmaCost` field in the priority charts represents Karma the player must spend from their starting pool. This is separate from and in addition to any quality costs.
- Some metavariants have 0 additional Karma cost at certain priorities (e.g., Nartaki at Priority A/B/C/D/E), making them mechanically identical to base metatype at those priorities except for attribute ranges and racial traits.

### Attribute Table Format

- Attribute values are expressed as "min/max" pairs. For implementation, parse these as `{ min: number, max: number }`.
- The racial traits string is a comma-separated list that should be parsed into an array of trait identifiers.
- Metasapient and shapeshifter tables include a MAG column (always 1 for these entries), indicating innate magical ability.
- Shapeshifter table includes an INI column showing initiative dice bonus (e.g., "+1D6", "+2D6").

### Shapeshifter Dual-Form System

- A shapeshifter character has TWO complete attribute sets: one for animal form (from the shapeshifter table) and one for metahuman form (derived from the base metatype chosen).
- The metahuman form attributes are calculated by: (1) determining how many points above minimum were allocated to each animal attribute, (2) applying those same point allocations to the chosen metahuman form's base attributes.
- The `abilitiesNotPresent` field in the metahuman form cost table lists racial traits from the animal form that do NOT carry over to metahuman form.

### Changeling / SURGE System

- Changelings are an overlay on top of any metatype (including metavariants).
- The SURGE class determines the Karma budget AND the selection method for metagenic qualities.
- Positive and Negative metagenic quality Karma values must balance within 1 Karma (favoring Positive side). Any excess is paid from free Karma.

> **Ambiguity:** The Gnome's "Arcane Arrester (2)" racial trait and the Fomorian's "Arcane Arrester (1)" racial trait use the same mechanic but at different levels. The metahuman form cost table notes Gnome form loses "Arcane Arrester (2)" and Fomorian form loses "Arcane Arrester (1)" -- shapeshifters choosing these forms do not gain the arrester ability.

> **Cross-reference:** Metagenic qualities (positive and negative) are detailed on p.111--123 of Run Faster. The random metagenic quality tables (p.109--111) use dice roll mechanics to randomly assign qualities for Class I and Class II changelings.

> **Cross-reference:** The core metatype attribute tables and standard priority charts are in SR5 core rulebook. These extended charts supplement but do not replace the core charts.
