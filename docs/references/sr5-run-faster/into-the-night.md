# Into the Night — Infected

**Source:** Run Faster, p.124-143
**PDF Pages:** 126-145

---

## Overview

This section provides rules for playing HMHVV-Infected characters as PCs in Shadowrun 5th Edition. It covers character creation for Infected types, the three strains of HMHVV with full disease mechanics, Essence Drain addiction rules, the interaction between Infection and Magic/Resonance, positive and negative Infected qualities for 20 Infected types, the Carrier negative quality, and two new critter powers (Adaptive Coloration, Corrosive Secretions). The standard SR5 rule that Infected PCs become NPCs is overridden by these optional rules.

---

## Rules

### Creating Infected Characters

- Created like any other character; the Infected quality is purchased during Step Four (Qualities).
- The 25 Karma cap on Positive Qualities does **not** apply to Infected qualities, but the 25 Karma cap on Negative Qualities **does** remain (meaning mutaqua cannot be selected unless the character is Awakened, since its cost exceeds the negative quality limit without the Awakened discount).
- Each Infected quality specifies which metatype(s) can take it.

### Creating an Infected Character Step by Step

1. Create character as normal (remember Essence/Magic changes and cyberware/technomancer restrictions).
2. At Step Four, buy the appropriate Infected quality (ensure correct metatype).
3. Purchase enough Negative Qualities to cover total Karma needed.
4. When you acquire the Infected quality, apply the following:
   - Note changes in minimum and maximum attributes (see Infected Metatype Attribute Table).
   - Add two points to selected Physical Attributes (+1 to two or +2 to one).
   - Add two points to selected Mental Attributes (+1 to two or +2 to one).
   - The attributes you select must come from the Improved Physical Attributes and Improved Mental Attributes lists included with each quality.
   - Make appropriate Essence/Magic changes according to HMHVV strain.
   - Apply Gained Powers and Gained Weaknesses.
5. Continue with Step Five of normal character creation.

### Becoming Infected During Play

- If a character becomes Infected during play and has unspent Karma, spend it immediately to pay the quality cost.
- If the character lacks enough Karma, they must spend all Karma earned on the quality until it is fully paid off.

### Bioware and Augmentations

- Any bioware an Infected character possesses drops a point of Rating; if it is Rating 1 or has no Rating, it is lost.
- Cyberware is less severely affected, but non-deltaware implants may be rejected.
- For each non-deltaware implant: make a **Body + Essence (rounded down) (3) Test**. On failure, the body rejects the implant, causing 1 box of Physical damage per day per rejected implant.
- Rejected implants must be removed, or the character can take immuno-suppressant drugs (100 nuyen/month, -2 dice pool penalty to Disease Resistance Tests).
- Infected with Regeneration can only accept **deltaware** augmentations (Regeneration rejects others; see p.400, SR5).

> **Cross-reference:** Augmentation restrictions for Infected will be expanded in future augmentation-focused rulebooks.

### Walking in Shadows (Allergy to Sunlight)

- Allergy (Sunlight) represents heightened sensitivity to UV-A radiation.
- Ordinary clouds do very little to diminish UV-A; volcanic ash or heavy pollution might reduce allergy penalties.
- High pollution density (requiring breathing masks) can reduce allergy intensity by one level (e.g., Severe becomes Moderate).
- UV-A bounces off concrete, asphalt, and windows -- standing in building shade or using an umbrella does **not** provide protection.
- Moving about during daytime requires a full hood and mask, and this only **reduces** severity, not eliminates it.
- UV-blocking windows in vehicles help; UV-treated clothes help for covered body parts only.
- Artificial UV lighting (security systems) causes discomfort and can prevent use of Infected powers.
- **Sunlight damage taken cannot be healed with Regeneration**, even after exposure ends. Normal and magical healing still work.

### Mist Form Optional Rule (You Can't Take It With You)

- Standard rules: Mist Form transforms the critter, basic clothing, bonded foci (active or not), and any augmentations (as they are part of the body). All other gear is left behind.
- **Optional rule:** Infected with Mist Form can carry up to **Essence in kilograms** of additional gear.
  - To transform a piece of gear: **Essence + Willpower Test** vs. the gear's **Object Resistance** (p.295, SR5).
  - If the Infected wins, the gear transforms; transforming back does not require a test.
  - Each failed attempt accelerates **Essence Loss** (p.401, SR5), which can lead to additional feeding and Essence Drain addiction.

### Essence Drain Addiction

- Essence Drain (p.396, SR5) is addictive for both the drainer and the victim.
- Uses the standard addiction rules with the Addiction Table values (see companion JSON).
- For the **victim**: addiction leads to seeking out further draining, becoming a thrall.
- For the **drainer**: addiction leads to more draining, more killing, and increased law enforcement attention.

### Infection, Magic, Resonance, and Essence

#### Strain II and III: Starting Magic/Essence

- All Strain II and Strain III Infected begin with **Essence 5** and **Magic 1**, unless they were magically active before Infection.
- If magically active before Infection: they begin with **Magic = original Magic - 1**; the Essence lost to disease takes the same toll on Magic as at any other time.
- They can increase Magic with Karma like any attribute, to a maximum of **5 + initiate grade**.

#### Strain I: Magic and Essence Interaction

- Essence Drain power and Essence Loss weakness cause Essence to fluctuate during a campaign; as Essence changes, Magic changes too.
- Magic loss does not occur every time the character loses or spends Essence -- maximum Magic equals current **Essence + Initiate grade**.
- If current Magic Rating exceeds current maximum Magic rating, current Magic is adjusted down. The removed points are inaccessible but not lost.
- Each point of Essence regained allows "lost" Magic to be regained via:
  1. Spend (new Rating) Karma to reconnect to a regained Magic point.
  2. Take a geas (p.142, Street Grimoire) for each point of Magic "lost."
  3. Instead of paying Karma, drain more Essence beyond normal maximum (each excess Essence point reconnects one lost Magic point, but each such drain requires an **Addiction Test**; see Essence Drain Addiction, p.134).

#### Temporarily Exceeding Magic

- Using Essence Drain (p.396, SR5), Infected characters can temporarily exceed their maximum Magic Rating by spending Essence points to boost Magic.
- If the Infected loses Essence to anything other than Essence Loss or Essence Drain, that lost Essence is subtracted from **maximum** Essence (e.g., a vampire losing a point of Essence to burnout loses maximum Essence permanently).

#### Dual Natured

- All Infected are dual natured and may freely learn Assensing and Astral Combat.
- Unless magically active, they cannot learn or use skills linked to the Magic attribute, astrally project, bond foci, or initiate.
- Exception: the Magical Guard power allows the Infected character to learn the Counterspelling skill.

#### Magic Resistance

- No Infected character can take Magic Resistance.
- If a character with Magic Resistance becomes Infected, they lose that quality; they may deduct its Karma cost from the Infected quality cost.

#### Infected Aura

- An Infected character's aura always reveals its Infected nature, though this can be masked by metamagic as normal.

### Infection and Resonance

- There are no Infected technomancers; HMHVV is magical and completely incompatible with Resonance.
- When a technomancer is Infected, they lose all connection to the Resonance Realms and all technomancer abilities.
- Any Active Skills linked to Resonance become Knowledge Skills.

### Movement Rate Bonus

- The "+1 to movement rates" listed in many Infected qualities applies to the movement multiplier.
- Example: A banshee (elf) with Agility 3 goes from walking 6 (AGI x 2) / running 12 (AGI x 4) to walking 9 (AGI x 3) / running 15 (AGI x 5).
- Sprint bonus also increases: dwarfs/trolls get +2 meters per hit; elves/humans/orks get +3 meters per hit.

### Diseases (HMHVV Mechanics)

Diseases work like toxins (p.408, SR5) with the following key differences:

- **Speed:** Incubation period between initial exposure and first Disease Resistance Test. Also represents interval between subsequent tests. Number in parentheses = minimum number of Disease Resistance Tests required.
- **Penetration:** Modifier on systems that boost disease resistance (e.g., pathogenic defense).
- **Power:** Functions like toxin Power. Disease is only defeated when minimum number of tests are made **and** Power is reduced to 0.
- **Power Accumulation:** If a Disease Resistance Test doesn't reduce Power to 0, remaining Power carries over and adds to the Power of the next test, until minimum tests are reached (at which point it peaks and no longer accumulates).
- **Nature:** Disease type (bacterial, fungal, parasitic, retroviral, toxin, viral). Determines treatment options.
- **Effect:** Two kinds -- Ongoing (experienced until disease eliminated) and Final (occurs if Power not reduced below a threshold in time). Other disease effects disappear once Final Effects kick in.
- **Edge Burn:** A character may permanently burn a point of Edge to guarantee pass or fail on a Disease Resistance Test.

## Negative Infected Qualities

### Carrier

- **Bonus:** 10 Karma
- The character survived a Strain II or Strain III attack but did not walk away unscathed -- the virus is in their body and they can spread it through unprotected contact with their bodily fluids.
- Can take this quality twice (once for Strain II, once for Strain III).
- Characters Infected with Strain II can still carry Strain III, and vice-versa. They cannot be infected by the strain of virus they carry.
- Exposure to their bodily fluids triggers Disease Resistance Tests (see Diseases, p.142).
- If the character carries two strains and both must be resisted, only one will express if the victim becomes Infected (they become a carrier of the other strain).
- **Social penalty:** -2 dice pool to all Social Tests against anyone who knows the character is HMHVV-positive.
- **Notoriety:** +1 per time this quality is taken (cumulative).

## HMHVV Disease Mechanics

### HMHVV Strain I

- **Vector:** Infection power (p.398, SR5)
- **Speed:** 1 minute (1)
- **Penetration:** -3
- **Power:** 13
- **Nature:** Retroviral
- **Effect (Final):** Coma, transformation
- Creates: banshees, dzoo-noo-qua, goblins, mutaqua, nosferatu, vampires, wendigos
- Spread only by the Infection power
- Very little chance of escape once Infected
- If Power is 6 or lower after the one required Disease Resistance Test: character has resisted turning but the disease still kills them (they die)
- If Power is 7 or higher after the test: character fails to resist transformation, enters coma for **(30 - Body) hours**, then awakens with the appropriate Infected quality, **Essence 1**, and **maximum Magic of 1 + initiate grade** (minimum Magic 1 if not already Awakened; if technomancer, loses all Resonance/technomancer abilities, Resonance-linked Active Skills become Knowledge Skills)
- **Edge Burn:** A character may permanently burn a point of Edge to guarantee pass or fail on the Disease Resistance Test

### HMHVV Strain II

- **Vector:** Injection
- **Speed:** 1 hour (3)
- **Penetration:** -3
- **Power:** 10
- **Nature:** Retroviral
- **Effect (Ongoing):** Nausea
- **Effect (Final):** Essence loss, transformation
- Creates: bandersnatches, fomoraig, gnawers, grendels, harvesters, loup-garou
- Typically spread by scratch, bite, or unprotected contact of Infected bodily fluids with an open wound
- Must reduce Power to 0 by end of the **three** required Disease Resistance Tests
- If successful: full recovery, though may end up as a carrier (see Carrier negative quality, p.141)
- If victim succumbs: loses 1 point of Essence. If Essence reaches 0 or below, the character dies. Otherwise, body begins transforming over **15 - Body days** (character is in extreme pain; treat as nausea, p.409, SR5)
- Upon completion: acquires appropriate Infected quality, begins with pre-Infection **Essence - 1**. If already Awakened, begins with **original Magic - 1** (see Infection, Magic, Resonance, and Essence, p.141). If not Awakened, begins with Magic 1. If technomancer, loses all Resonance; Resonance-linked Active Skills become Knowledge Skills.
- **Edge Burn:** May permanently burn Edge to guarantee pass/fail
- **Essence lost to HMHVV II infection cannot be recovered by any means**

### HMHVV Strain III

- **Vector:** Injection
- **Speed:** 12 hours (10)
- **Penetration:** -3
- **Power:** 6
- **Nature:** Retroviral
- **Effect (Ongoing):** Nausea
- **Effect (Final):** Essence loss, transformation
- Known as Krieger strain; creates ghouls
- Spread by scratches, bites, or unprotected contact with bodily fluids
- Longer incubation; accompanied by pain and fever
- Must reduce Power to 0 by end of the **ten** required Disease Resistance Tests
- If victim succumbs: loses 1 point of Essence. If Essence reaches 0 or below, character dies. Otherwise, metamorphosis takes another **10 - Body days** (character is awake and aware, in extreme pain; treat as nausea)
- Upon completion: acquires Ghoul Infected quality, begins with pre-Infection **Essence - 1**. If already Awakened, begins with **original Magic - 1**. If not Awakened, begins with Magic 1. If technomancer, loses all Resonance.
- **Edge Burn:** May permanently burn Edge to guarantee pass/fail. May end up as carrier (see Carrier, p.141).
- **Essence lost to HMHVV III infection cannot be recovered by any means**

## New Critter Powers

### Adaptive Coloration

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Simple | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Sustained
- Allows the critter to refract light around itself, becoming nearly invisible.
- **Immobile:** Dice pool modifier of **-(critter's Magic x 2)** to Perception Tests to detect by sight.
- **Moving:** Dice pool modifier drops to **-(critter's Magic)**.
- Also impairs depth perception: **additional -2 dice pool modifier** to any ranged attacks against the critter.
- Critters can roll **Agility + Intuition (2) Test**; net hits beyond threshold act as a positive dice pool modifier for Infiltration and Sneaking Tests.
- Works against normal vision and augmented thermographic vision.
- Does **not** work against non-visual senses (sonar, radar, scent tracking, astral perception).
- Some critters may have a version affecting a wider range of senses (noted in critter description).

### Corrosive Secretions

- **Type:** P | **Action:** Auto | **Range:** Self | **Duration:** Always
- The critter's skin secretes a highly caustic substance.
- Causes **Acid damage** (p.170, SR5) with:
  - **DV:** (Magic x 2)P
  - **AP:** -(critter's Magic)
- Damages anything or anyone that touches or is touched by the critter.
- The critter is immune to its own corrosive secretions.

## Implementation Notes

- **Infected quality as a quality type:** Each Infected type should be modeled as a positive quality with metatype restrictions, Karma cost, and embedded stat modifications (attribute min/max overrides, gained powers/weaknesses, optional powers pool).
- **Attribute table overrides:** When an Infected quality is applied, the character's metatype attribute table should be replaced with the Infected Metatype Attribute Table values. The +2 physical and +2 mental attribute points are applied on top of this.
- **HMHVV Strain as metadata:** Each Infected quality should reference its HMHVV strain (I, Ia, II, or III). This determines starting Essence/Magic and disease mechanics if the character was infected during play.
- **Strain Ia (Mutaqua, Nosferatu):** These are Strain I variants with special Essence rules: can increase Essence up to 3x natural maximum, and lose Essence at a slower rate (every 2 months for mutaqua, every 4 months for nosferatu).
- **Essence Loss tracking:** Strain I Infected need periodic Essence loss tracked (interval varies by type). This drives the need for Essence Drain and the Magic fluctuation system.
- **Optional Powers:** Infected characters choose one free Optional Power at creation from their type's list. Additional Optional Powers can be purchased with Karma (one every two in-game months), using the Infected Optional Powers table costs.
- **Ghoul metatype variants:** The Ghoul quality (29 Karma) applies to any metatype, but the attribute min/max values differ per base metatype. The JSON contains separate rows for Ghoul (Dwarf), Ghoul (Elf), Ghoul (Human), Ghoul (Ork), Ghoul (Sasquatch), Ghoul (Troll).
- **Carrier quality:** This is a negative quality that can be taken independently of being Infected. It represents surviving an attack without full transformation. It can stack (once per strain).
- **Disease resistance system:** If implementing HMHVV infection during gameplay, you need a multi-test disease resistance pipeline that tracks cumulative Power across multiple rolls at defined intervals.
