# Johnson Genesis -- Syndicate/Corp Johnson Generation

**Source:** Run Faster, p.202-211
**PDF Pages:** 204-213

---

## Overview

This section provides GM guidance for generating Mr. Johnson NPCs based on their organizational background. It covers megacorporate Johnsons (Big Ten), syndicate Johnsons (Mafia, Yakuza, Triads, Vory), extremist organization Johnsons (Humanis, Illuminates of the New Dawn, Neo-Anarchists), and amateur Johnsons. Each profile describes behavioral tendencies, meet preferences, payment styles, betrayal risk, and mechanical implications for gameplay.

> **Cross-reference:** Notoriety rules for Johnson interactions at SR5, p.372. Contact rules at Run Faster, p.172-180.

---

## Rules

### Mr. Johnson and Notoriety (Game Information)

Actions that earn runners Notoriety when dealing with Johnsons (p.202):

- Being noticed using Manipulation magic under normal circumstances
- Showing up at a meet heavily armed in an environment that doesn't warrant it
- Being caught hacking the Johnson's commlink under normal circumstances (unless the log is erased after the fact)
- Killing a Johnson who paid you

> **Cross-reference:** Notoriety rules, SR5, p.372. "Insulting or otherwise pissing off a Johnson" can earn Notoriety.

## Megacorporate Johnsons

Corporate Johnsons are professional specialists hired and trained specifically for the role. They are typically former lawyers, project managers, consultants, or military/security officers. They live for the job (11-12 hour days), have alpha personalities, use designer drugs, and have no moral compass. Megas pay better than other employers but expect more.

**General Megacorp Johnson Traits:**

- Professional full-time Johnsons (not incidental hires)
- Alpha personalities, exacting standards
- The run idea comes from the corporation, not the Johnson -- find the real stakeholder
- Johnsons obfuscate which corporation they work for
- Invading a Johnson's commlink to trace the source is risky but effective
- High risk of being offered a retainer (company suit) if you do well repeatedly
- Meet locations: fancy restaurants, VIP booths at exclusive clubs (corp is paying)

### Big Ten Johnson Profiles

#### Ares Macrotechnology

- **Recruitment pool:** Military and security officers (more than any other corp)
- **Personality:** Crafty out-of-the-box thinkers, not rigid drill sergeants
- **Payment style:** Generous; everything is "just a figure." Often offers hard-to-get milspec gear. Lifestyle perks from Ares Entertainment as bonuses
- **Reputation:** Mostly good among shadowrunners
- **Betrayal risk:** Moderate -- they know they have a good rep and will use betrayal to its fullest if the situation requires it
- **Special note:** Recent Excalibur fiasco has stirred distrust in the shadows

#### Aztechnology

- **Reputation:** Worst among all corps in the shadows (due to overall agenda, not individual dealings)
- **Payment style:** Fair to generous; provide lots of assistance to teams
- **Quirk:** Tendency to hide affiliation -- will mimic employment conditions of other corps. May try to pay in Aztechnology scrip (tell them no and continue negotiating)
- **Betrayal risk:** High -- overall corporate plan to "dominate or destroy the world" colors all dealings
- **GM guidance:** If you can look past the agenda, they are excellent employers

#### Evo Corporation

- **Personality:** Paranoid about security and privacy
- **Meet preferences:** Wide-open spaces outside the city; particular fondness for boats in the middle of the ocean. Every meet has top security regardless of job size
- **Hiring bias:** Usually metahumans; play up "friendliness" to metas. Will hire anyone including overt racists if you take nuyen and do the job
- **Payment style:** Cash as standard; known to offer cyber or bioware payments with access to medical facilities for installation
- **Special note:** Maintain high security at every meet, no matter how small the job

#### Horizon

- **Personality:** Amped-up, high on drugs (mostly novacoke), high cycling/burnout rate. Best and brightest of their peers despite the drug use
- **Approach:** Consider the whole op as a single team effort ("us" not "us-vs-you"). Will facilitate whatever runners need, take run feedback into account, alter plans based on runner suggestions
- **Role overlap:** Horizon Mr. Johnson pretty much doubles as a fixer
- **Payment style:** Good pay
- **Betrayal risk:** Associated with the Dawkins Group -- manipulative operatives who erase people and convince them to give up everything. Dawkins can destroy anyone with minimal effort. Nobody likes Dawkins
- **Special warning:** If Dawkins Group gets involved in your run, things get very dangerous

#### Mitsuhama Computer Technologies (MCT)

- **Personality:** Insanely precise and methodical
- **Approach:** Entire run pre-planned in detail -- maps, covers, weapon caches, vehicles, everything. Demand you follow the plan to the letter
- **Payment style:** All-or-nothing. If you fail, expect no payment AND refund any advance. Success pays exceedingly well
- **Betrayal risk:** Very high on failure -- may send kill squad if you fail and accepted payment
- **Special note:** Distinction between a direct MCT Johnson and MCT subcontracting through Yakuza. If MCT farms it to the Yakuza, use Yakuza profile instead (lower pay, different modus operandi)

#### NeoNET

- **Personality:** Highly variable -- NeoNET lacks a central corporate culture (effectively Novatech, Erika, Transys, plus ancient fuchi remnants)
- **Structure:** Form webs of secret agents; internal competition is fierce. Johnsons lend teams to colleagues to show off
- **Betrayal risk:** Very low -- building reliable teams is their priority. However, expect to be shipped around the globe
- **Special note:** International coterie of back-channel Johnsons set up by Miles Lanier (now CFD-afflicted). Lanier's network works better and smoother than official NeoNET channels

> **Ambiguity:** NeoNET's corporate status changed significantly after the CFD crisis in later publications. This profile reflects the Run Faster (2014) era.

#### Renraku Computer Systems

- **Personality:** Crave stability; prize company suits over freelancers
- **Approach:** Scout and test teams (first two or three runs are auditions). If you do well, offer retainer -- lock, stock, and barrel. They will _insist_ you accept
- **Payment style:** Predictable income, access to toys/gear if on retainer. Most company teams get attached to one particular Johnson
- **Betrayal risk:** Moderate -- internal politics mean Johnson's enemies become your enemies. Symbiotic relationship means more loyalty than expected
- **Special warning:** The Red Samurai _hate_ company suits. They are jealous guardians of the company. If doing joint ops with Red Samurai, watch your back

#### Saeder-Krupp

- **Personality:** Play the misdirection game; don't advertise as S-K
- **Disguise:** Will present as mob guys, other corps, or amateur scientists. Very good at disguises
- **Mission style:** Odd jobs; never the same kind of job you'd get from other corps. Don't go for repeatable patterns
- **Recruitment pool:** People with field experience who can take care of themselves -- adepts, mages, people who are discreet, lethal, and top-of-the-line in cyber
- **Betrayal risk:** High uncertainty -- S-K Johnsons get left alone a lot in terms of security. They quickly learn to stop figuring out the reasons behind missions
- **Special note:** Exception is Hans Brackhaus -- legendary shadow figure played by many actors. If Hans Brackhaus hires you, your life is about to get complicated

#### Shiawase Corporation

- **Division:** Almost all Johnsons come from the Marketing and Intelligence Forecast Division (MIFD) -- Big Data search algorithms and intelligence analysis
- **Personality:** Not subtle; expected to be corporate culture exemplars. Stiff backs, no sense of humor, Bushido all the way. Do not expect adaptability when problems arise
- **Hiring culture:** Expected to show loyalty and honor. Bushido does not permit surrender or defeat
- **Payment style:** Steady, well-thought-out work. Try asking for information as payment -- Johnsons don't do this at first, but if you prove your honor, they may feed you valuable intel
- **Betrayal risk:** Low if honor is maintained

#### Wuxing Incorporated

- **Unique trait:** Always work in pairs -- two Wuxing Johnsons at every meet, at least one Awakened
- **Magic policy:** They will use magic on _you_ (typically Analyze Truth). Never use magic (especially Manipulation spells) at a Wuxing meet
- **Meet preferences:** Triad-owned bars, dockyards, locations near power sites
- **Payment style:** Pays least among megas. Best non-cash payment: smuggling services (shipping/logistics). Association with Triads can help with items and travel
- **Betrayal risk:** Moderate -- be aware the magical member is always watching

## Syndicate Johnsons

Syndicate Johnsons operate closer to the source of the run than corporate Johnsons. You deal with the number two (Consigliere, Wakagashira, Lodge Master, etc.) rather than the big boss. Syndicate Johnsons are typically less anonymous than corporate ones -- get their rap sheet.

**General Syndicate Johnson Traits:**

- Strongly encourage prejudice based on traditions, cultures, and biases
- Almost all criminal syndicates are racist and prejudiced -- know their biases and use them to your advantage (or at minimum, don't create a disadvantage)
- Jobs tend to have a tricky political element -- internal power plays, strikes against rival syndicates
- Very high risk of post-run liquidation (second most likely group to kill you after extremists)
- Show respect; syndicate Johnsons understand violence and use intimidation (showing examples of what happens to failures)
- Syndicate Johnsons lure runners with vices after first meets -- girls/boys, drugs, liquor. Nothing is ever free
- Never trust a syndicate "friend" -- the guy you least expect is always the one coming for you

### Major Syndicates

#### Mafia (Cosa Nostra)

- **Diversity:** Very wide and diverse -- Jewish, Irish, Polish, Black, Italian, and more
- **Common thread:** Respect. Always show respect. Any disrespect _must_ be punished
- **Meet preferences:** Restaurants, bars, gambling clubs in their territory. Can extend to docks or alleys. Don't read too much into location choice
- **Payment style:** Mob typically pays what it promises. Not getting paid is rarely a concern -- they either pay you fully or kill you
- **Hiring bias:** Not much of a problem working with runners. Good alcohol usually offered
- **Betrayal risk:** Low for payment; high for violence if you cross them

#### Yakuza

- **Hiring bias:** Most trouble working with runners. Dislike dishonorable outsiders. Prejudiced against non-Japanese, women, and metahumans. All-Japanese-human-male teams have fewest issues
- **Personality:** Unpleasant. Don't like being near you. Get to business quickly and plainly. Assume you are an idiot. Keep it simple. Don't ask too many questions
- **Meet preferences:** Invite you somewhere nice, but you won't sample any delights -- those are for the Johnson
- **Payment style:** Pay well enough; prefer payment in kind (vices, cyberware, hacking gear, weapons)
- **Corporate ties:** Have claws in a lot of corporations through extortion and blackmail. Runs often have inside help from the corp being targeted
- **Betrayal risk:** High -- biggest hypocrites in the shadows. Break all rules they insist you follow

#### Triads

- **Personality:** Straightforward. No strong opinion about runners one way or another. Typically older gentlemen and ladies
- **Quirks:** Infuriatingly superstitious; talk in metaphors way too much. Often pair runners with their own muscle as backup or diversion
- **Loyalty:** Don't expect to become a regular. You are purely mercenaries and never will be favorites
- **Payment style:** Decent
- **Betrayal risk:** High if you cross them -- payback _will_ come using magic. Find a nice warded place and plan to stay there permanently if you cross them

#### Vory (Vory v Zakone)

- **Personality:** Tough, cold, pragmatic. Keep ticking after beatings. Feel superior to outsiders but respect delivery
- **Approach:** Epitome of violent men. Only know one way to solve problems: kill everyone you know, knew, or might possibly know in spectacularly bloody fashion
- **Hiring bias:** Rely on shadowrunners more than other syndicates. You're still an outsider, but delivering results earns respect
- **Payment style:** Good for a certain charge. Very pragmatic -- whatever works, whatever you need to do, is fine by them as long as it doesn't screw up their work
- **Betrayal risk:** Moderate -- good people to work for if you aren't looking to make friends

## Extremists

Extremist organizations include terrorist groups, eco-terrorists, insurgents, freedom fighters, rebels, secret societies, political organizations, and magical groups. Anyone who values a cause above nuyen.

**General Extremist Johnson Traits:**

- The Johnson is almost always the boss (cell structure -- nobody else can authorize hiring)
- Will promise anything and lie about everything to get you to do the job
- Very high post-run kill risk (highest of all employer types)
- Extremely paranoid -- government, corporate spy agencies, and law enforcement agencies watch them. If they smoke you for simply not being all in, imagine what they'll do if you oppose them
- Reputation and the covenant of the shadows don't matter to them
- Most fund quite a lot of shadow work -- they need your skillset

### Extremist Organizations

#### Humanis

- **Approach:** Subtle -- not lining up metas for execution. Datasteal, surveillance, planting evidence to convince people metahumans are bad
- **Johnson type:** Not a toothless hick -- probably a rich guy's assistant. Thinks highly of himself and even more highly of his boss
- **Hiring policy:** No qualms hiring metahumans. Getting caught proves their point ("metahumans are nasty little thugs and thieves")
- **Payment style:** Middle-ground. Decent pay, no real perks, average chance of betrayal
- **Betrayal risk:** Average
- **Job types:** Humanis Johnsons tend to be lazy; like hiring the same teams over and over as long as nothing goes dramatically bad
- **True agenda example:** Organization presents itself as "not anti-metahuman but pro-human" while hiring runners to burn down an orphanage for ork children

#### Illuminates of the New Dawn (IoND)

- **Johnson type:** Smug fascist hermetic mage. Hoarder of secrets, very self-important
- **Hiring needs:** Not just mage runners -- need street muscle, techies, riggers, hackers
- **Planning:** Very good plans, but rely too much on magic and human manipulation. Plans tend to be light on hacking and good old violence
- **Payment style:** Reputation for being rich is overstated -- don't pay that well. However, they have information in spades (blackmail dirt, intel on who is doing what to whom). Hermetics on your team may appreciate arcane items as payment
- **Betrayal risk:** Moderate
- **GM guidance:** Evaluate and inject your own expertise into their plan -- they'll be offended but eventually come around. They know deep down you're better at this than they are

#### Neo-Anarchists

- **Personality:** Natural affinity with shadowrunners. Get along well over soykaf and Styrofoam cups
- **Hiring pool:** Never a shortage of need for qualified shadowrunners (their own people are idealist dreamers who can't get anything done)
- **Payment style:** Poorest pay you've ever seen. Next to no chance of getting betrayed
- **Betrayal risk:** Very low
- **Contact quality:** They have contacts among the poorest people -- street docs, hacker software, hardware supplies, weapons, safehouses. Everything runners might need

## Amateurs

Amateurs are people who do not normally operate in the shadows. They don't know shadowrunners, don't know the rules, and just stepped in.

**Examples:** Scientist hiring you to sabotage a rival's project, rich mage guy wanting artifact retrieval, parents hiring you to find their missing daughter

**General Amateur Johnson Traits:**

- Not inherently dangerous per se -- may try to kill you after, but plans will be silly and over-elaborate
- Pay is bad -- few have thousands of nuyen. Typically cash plus something personally precious (family heirloom)
- Intel will be worthless, biased, or dangerously wrong (e.g., "Our good little girl has gone missing! She is helpless!" -- actually an insect shaman)
- No contacts in the shadows -- whatever you do is unlikely to affect your reputation
- Two types of runners take amateur jobs: those who refuse most payment, and those who milk Mr. Johnson dry
- Require patience but can be a nice change of scenery. Feel-good factor may come at a decrease in cash payment
- Amateurs are easy marks if you choose to exploit them

## Implementation Notes

- **No mechanical tables** in this section -- this is entirely narrative GM guidance. The JSON captures structured profiles for each organization.
- **Johnson background** is a GM tool for NPC generation, not a player-facing mechanic. It influences roleplay, meet scenarios, payment negotiation, and betrayal likelihood.
- **Betrayal risk** is qualitative (not a dice roll). GMs should use these profiles to inform narrative decisions about double-crosses.
- **Syndicate bias fields** (e.g., Yakuza anti-metahuman, anti-female, anti-gaijin) could inform Social skill modifiers at GM discretion but are not codified as such in the book.
- **Payment style** descriptions can inform the nuyen range and alternative payment options offered during job negotiation scenes.
- For a Johnson NPC generator tool, the key structured fields per organization are: `meetPreferences`, `paymentStyle`, `betrayalRisk`, `personality`, `hiringBias`, `specialWarnings`.
