# A Dump of One's Own — Lifestyles

**Source:** Run Faster, p.212-227
**PDF Pages:** 214-229

---

## Overview

Run Faster expands the SR5 core lifestyle system with a point-based customization layer. A lifestyle is divided into four categories: **Comforts & Necessities (C&N)**, **Security (S)**, **Neighborhood (N)**, and **Entertainment (E)**. Each lifestyle has preset levels for these categories plus a pool of points that can raise individual category levels or purchase entertainment options. Each point spent on C&N, Security, or Neighborhood increases the monthly cost by 10% of the base lifestyle cost. Entertainment points have variable costs per option.

> **Cross-reference:** Core lifestyle descriptions on p.373, SR5. Core lifestyle qualities (Cramped, Extra Secure, etc.) on p.370, SR5.

---

## Lifestyle Categories

### Comforts & Necessities

- An integral part of any lifestyle covering shelter quality, food, and basic living conditions.
- At the beginning of most game sessions, characters roll to resist fatigue damage simulating rest quality.
- **Base Fatigue damage:** 6S. Reduce DV by 2 for each level in Comforts & Necessities.
- **Allergy interactions (C&N level 3 or lower):**
  - Common allergies: increase Fatigue DV by 1 per level above mild.
  - Uncommon allergies: increase Fatigue DV by 1 if Severe, 2 if Extreme.
  - Common food allergies (soy, mycoprotein, etc.): increase Fatigue DV by 1 per level above mild if C&N is level 3 or lower.
  - Uncommon food allergies: increase DV by 1 if Severe, 2 if Extreme.

> **Cross-reference:** Fatigue damage rules, p.172, SR5.

### Security

- Represents deterrence to unwanted guests and criminal activity.
- Each point into Security reduces risk of theft of the character's possessions at home.
- **Security Test (burglary while away):** Roll dice pool of (Security level x 2) vs. Sneaking + Agility of burglars. If Security wins, the attempt is noticed (alarms triggered, officers aware, etc.).
- **Theft abstraction (alternative):** GM may use a test with dice pool equal to the difference between minimum Security required and actual Security purchased. Each hit = player pays 1% of monthly lifestyle cost to replace stolen/damaged goods.
- **Item targeting:** GM should compare Availability of rare items to the Security level. If item Availability exceeds Security, someone may notice it and target it. The greater the discrepancy, the more likely the item will be targeted.

> **Cross-reference:** Armed and dangerous security, p.355, SR5.

### Neighborhood

- Abstract category covering acceptable behavior, safety, and general atmosphere.
- Uses Neighborhood Zone classifications (Z through AAA) mapped to numeric levels 0-7.
- Between Neighborhood and Security, the GM and player develop the feel and look of the area.

### Entertainment

- Covers things that entertain, make life easier, and give characters things to do and people to see.
- Each perk has a **monthly nuyen cost** and a **point cost**.
- If the lifestyle that meets or exceeds the "no cost" lifestyle for an entertainment item, the element can be added without extra nuyen (financial costs are absorbed by the lifestyle expense), though points must still be spent.
- A minimum lifestyle of "None" means no lifestyle can cover the cost; the character must pay out of pocket.
- Each item can only be purchased once per domicile unless the description says otherwise.
- Advantages only apply when the character is actively using the domicile (not traveling).
- Three types: **Assets** (physical characteristics or nearby facilities), **Services** (services at home or in neighborhood), **Outings** (opportunities in the neighborhood).

### Bolt Hole

- A space away from everything; not pretty.
- Add **+4** to the threshold of any test involving someone trying to track a character living in a Bolt Hole.
- In the Entertainment category, assets can be bought for a Bolt Hole but **not** services or outings (see p.219).
- Bolt holes only have a monthly cost when used. The character pays for the whole month even if used for a single day.
- **Built-in option:** Not A Home.

### Street

- See category description on p.373, SR5.
- Increased levels in C&N, Security, and Neighborhood result in a flat increase of **50 nuyen per level** per month.
- Entertainments come with normal listed costs, but assets or services must be approved by the GM (must be usable/accessible by the general public).

### Traveler (1D6 + 2 Points)

- The traveler keeps mobile and packs light.
- Every month, the traveler stays someplace new, settling by chance.
- Player rolls **1D6** to determine the number of points allocated to the lifestyle that month.
- Points can be spent on Comforts, Security, or Necessities.
- The rest of the premise is designed by the GM, including what Entertainment is or is not available.
- In the Entertainment category, only assets and services are added by the GM; they do not add to the cost as they are provided by the establishment.

### Commercial

- A storefront, not a proper residence.
- Includes a location where the character can manufacture, fence, or repair goods.
- Includes permits to sell specific legal items.
- Because it is a business that can make money, the monthly cost can vary.
- **Rent reduction:** Charisma + Etiquette (Corporate) [Social] test at start of each month. Each net hit reduces monthly cost by 1,000 nuyen for that month.

## Point System Rules

- Not all points need to be spent. Each point spent on C&N, Security, or Neighborhood increases cost by **10% of base lifestyle cost**.
- Points in Entertainment vary in cost depending on the option (see Entertainment section).
- Lifestyle categories **cannot be lowered** below the base level (unless you switch to a lower lifestyle tier).
- The Entertainment category does not have a numeric level; points buy specific entertainment options.
- **Lifestyle options** (p.224) can be taken to get more points or reduce categories. Negative options give +1 point each. Positive options can increase the limit of a category.
- A lifestyle can gain up to **twice** the starting lifestyle points when taking lifestyle options. Some positive options can increase the limit further (noted per option).
- Some lifestyles come with lifestyle options already included (noted in lifestyle descriptions).

## Maintaining a Lifestyle

- If a complete lifestyle (with all additions) cannot be maintained, add-ons can be dropped (no monthly payment needed for them).
- Assets must be paid for in advance; if not paid at the beginning of the month, the character does not get to use it.
- Dropped asset points are not returned. Can resume payments in a future month to regain the option.
- To switch assets, buy a new lifestyle with the needed assets.

## Implementation Notes

- **Data model:** Each lifestyle should store category values (comfortsAndNecessities, security, neighborhood) as `{ value: number, limit: number }`. Entertainment has no numeric level; it is a collection of purchased options.
- **Point tracking:** Track `pointsTotal`, `pointsSpent`, and validate `pointsSpent <= pointsTotal`. For Traveler, `pointsTotal` should be recalculated each game month (1D6 + 2).
- **Cost calculation:** `monthlyCost = baseCost + (pointsSpentOnCategories * baseCost * 0.10) + sum(entertainmentNuyenCosts)`. For Street lifestyle, use flat 50 nuyen/level instead of percentage.
- **Minimum lifestyle validation:** Each entertainment option has a `minimumLifestyle` that must be met or exceeded by the character's lifestyle tier. Use a tier ordering: Street < Squatter < Low < Medium < High < Luxury. "None" means no minimum. "Commercial" means the lifestyle must be or include a Commercial lifestyle.
- **Lifestyle options:** Positive options may modify category values/limits. Negative options add +1 to the point pool. Enforce maximum: a lifestyle can gain up to 2x its starting points from options.
- **Bolt Hole special rules:** Entertainment restricted to assets only (no services or outings). Add +4 threshold to tracking tests.
- **Safehouse option:** Adds +4 to tracking dice pool. Restricts Entertainment to non-asset purchases only (inverted from Bolt Hole). Adds 500 nuyen/month.
- **Commercial rent reduction:** Implement as a monthly Charisma + Etiquette (Corporate) [Social] test. Each net hit = -1,000 nuyen (minimum 0).
- **Fatigue resistance:** At session start, characters resist 6S fatigue damage. DV reduced by 2 per C&N level. Factor in allergy modifiers when C&N is 3 or lower.
- **Neighborhood zones** map directly to neighborhood level values (0=Z, 1=E, 2=D, 3=C, 4=B, 5=A, 6=AA, 7=AAA). Response times are for emergency services.

> **Cross-reference:** Equipment Packs (p.228-252, Run Faster) include lifestyle-related gear packs that may reference these lifestyle tiers.
