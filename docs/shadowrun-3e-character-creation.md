# Shadowrun 3rd Edition Character Creation Guide

## Overview

Shadowrun 3rd Edition uses a **Priority System** for character creation. Players assign priorities (A through E, where A is highest) to five categories: Magic, Race, Attributes, Skills, and Resources. Each priority level provides different benefits in that category.

## Priority System

### General Rules
- Each priority (A-E) can only be assigned **once**
- Priority A = highest/best, Priority E = lowest/least
- Magic priority can be "N/A" (for non-magical characters) at Priority C, D, or E
- When assigning priorities, you select one row from the table above

### How It Works

**Example Priority Selection:**
- If you choose **Attributes Priority A**: You get 30 attribute points
- If you choose **Attributes Priority B**: You get 27 attribute points
- If you choose **Attributes Priority C**: You get 24 attribute points
- If you choose **Attributes Priority D**: You get 21 attribute points
- If you choose **Attributes Priority E**: You get 18 attribute points

**The same applies to Race, Magic, Skills, and Resources.**

### Priority Categories

#### 1. Race Priority
Determines which metatype (race) the character can be.

| Priority | Metatypes Available |
|----------|---------------------|
| A | All (Human, Elf, Dwarf, Ork, Troll) |
| B | All (Human, Elf, Dwarf, Ork, Troll) |
| C | Troll or Elf only |
| D | Dwarf or Ork only |
| E | Human only |

**Racial Modifications Table:**

| Race | Modifications |
|------|---------------|
| Human | No attribute modifiers (baseline) |
| Dwarf | +1 Body, +2 Strength, +1 Willpower<br>Thermographic Vision<br>Resistance (+2 Body to any disease or toxin) |
| Elf | +1 Quickness, +2 Charisma<br>Low-light Vision |

**Important Notes:**
- Attribute modifiers are applied **after** attribute point distribution
- No character may start with any attribute higher than **6** (before racial modifiers)
- After racial modifiers are applied, attributes may exceed 6 (subject to metatype maximums)
- Physical or Mental attributes cannot be less than 1 (after modifiers)
- Special abilities (vision, resistance, reach, armor) are in addition to attribute modifiers

#### 2. Magic Priority
Determines magical ability.

| Priority | Magic Type | Magic Rating | Points | Notes |
|----------|------------|--------------|--------|-------|
| A | Full Magician | 6 | 25 Spell Points | Can purchase additional points |
| B | Adept/Aspected Magician | 4 | 25 Power Points (Adept) or 35 Spell Points (Aspected) | Adept or Aspected choice |
| C | N/A | 0 | 0 | Mundane (no magic) |
| D | N/A | 0 | 0 | Mundane (no magic) |
| E | N/A | 0 | 0 | Mundane (no magic) |

**Full Magician (Priority A) Rules:**
- Receives 25 spell points at creation
- Spell points used to purchase spells at 1 point per spell Force
- Can bond foci during creation: 1 spell point per Force rating
- Can purchase additional spell points: 25,000¬• per point, up to maximum of 50 total spell points
- Can begin with conjured spirits: 1 spell point per Force, +2 spell points per service
- Limitations:
  - No spells, foci, or spirits higher than Force 6
  - Cannot begin with more than 6 spirits
  - Cannot begin with any spirit owning more than 6 services
- Must follow either Shamanic or Hermetic tradition

**Aspected Magician (Priority B) Rules:**
- Receives 35 spell points at creation
- Specialized in one aspect of their tradition with certain restrictions
- Can purchase additional spell points: 25,000¬• per point, max 50 total points

**Adept (Priority B) Rules:**
- Receives 25 Power Points at creation
- Uses Power Points to purchase adept powers (physical enhancements)
- Cannot cast spells but has physical magical enhancements
- **Power Cost Types:**
  - **Fixed Cost Powers:** Pay the listed Power Point cost (e.g., Astral Perception: 2 Power Points)
  - **Per-Level Powers:** Pay cost √ó level (e.g., Attribute Boost: 0.25 per level)
  - **Specification Required:** Some powers require additional specification (e.g., Attribute Boost requires choosing which attribute to boost)
- **Available Powers:**
  - Astral Perception (2 Power Points) - Can perceive the astral plane
  - Attribute Boost (0.25 Power Points per level) - Boosts a physical or mental attribute (must specify attribute)
  - More powers to be added as provided

**Mundane (Priority C, D, E):**
- No magical ability
- Magic Rating = 0
- Cannot cast spells or use magical abilities

#### 3. Attributes Priority

**Attributes:**
- Body (BOD)
- Quickness (QUI)
- Strength (STR)
- Charisma (CHA)
- Intelligence (INT)
- Willpower (WIL)

**Attribute Distribution:**
- Player distributes points among all 6 attributes (Body, Quickness, Strength, Charisma, Intelligence, Willpower)
- Points can be allocated unevenly (e.g., 8 points to Body, 4 to Quickness, etc.)
- **Minimum per attribute:** 1 point (Physical or Mental attributes cannot be less than 1)
- **Maximum per attribute:** 6 points before racial modifiers
- After racial modifiers applied, attributes may exceed 6 (subject to metatype maximums)
- No character may start with any attribute higher than 6 (before racial modifiers)

**Derived Attributes:**
- **Reaction** = (Quickness + Intelligence) √∑ 2, rounded down
- **Essence** = 6.0 (reduced by cyberware)
- **Magic** = Can be 0 (mundane) or 1-6 (magical characters)

#### 4. Skills Priority
Determines total skill points available.

| Priority | Skill Points | Notes |

**Skill Rules:**
- Skill points can be used for Active Skills, Knowledge Skills, and Language Skills
- **Skill Cost Calculation:**
  - Each skill has a **linked attribute** (e.g., Pistols links to Quickness, Computer links to Intelligence)
  - **If skill rating ‚â§ linked attribute rating:** Cost is **1 skill point per skill rating**
    - Example: Quickness 4, Pistols Rating 3 ‚Üí Cost = 3 points (3 √ó 1)
  - **If skill rating > linked attribute rating:** Cost is **2 skill points per skill rating**
    - Example: Quickness 4, Pistols Rating 6 ‚Üí Cost = 12 points (6 √ó 2)
- **Specializations:** Cost additional points (typically +1 point)
- **Skill Groups:** May have different costs (consult skill group rules)
- **Maximum starting skill rating:** Typically 6 (subject to linked attribute constraints)

## Character Creation Order

**Note:** Priorities (A-E) should be assigned to Magic, Race, Attributes, Skills, and Resources before beginning character creation. Each priority is used only once.

### Step 1: Choose Race (Metatype)
1. Based on Race priority, choose metatype:
   - Priority A or B: All races available (Human, Elf, Dwarf, Ork, Troll)
   - Priority C: Troll or Elf only
   - Priority D: Dwarf or Ork only
   - Priority E: Human only
2. Apply metatype attribute modifiers
3. Note attribute maximums for chosen metatype

### Step 2: Choose Magical Abilities
1. Based on Magic priority, choose magical type:
   - **Priority A**: Full Magician (Mage/Shaman)
   - **Priority B**: Adept or Aspected Magician (Mage/Shaman)
   - **Priority C, D, E**: Mundane (no magical ability)
2. If Full Magician or Aspected Magician:
   - Select Tradition: Shamanic or Hermetic
   - If Shamanic we need to choose a totem.  
   - Note spell points received (Full Magician: 25, Aspected: 35)
   - Note Magic Rating (Full Magician: 6, Aspected/Adept: 4)
3. If Adept:
   - Note Power Points received (25)
   - Note Magic Rating (4)
4. If Mundane:
   - Magic Rating = 0

### Step 3: Choose Attributes (Distribute Attribute Points)
1. Based on Attributes priority, receive attribute points (30, 27, 24, 21, or 18)
2. Distribute points among **six** of the **nine** attributes:
   - **Allocated attributes:** Body, Quickness, Strength, Intelligence, Willpower, Charisma
   - **Derived attributes:** Essence, Magic, Reaction (determined separately)
3. Each allocated attribute must have at least 1 point
4. No allocated attribute can have more than 6 points (before modifiers)
5. Apply metatype racial modifiers
6. Verify attributes don't exceed metatype maximums
7. Calculate Reaction: (Quickness + Intelligence) √∑ 2, rounded down
8. Set Essence to 6.0 (will decrease with cyberware)
9. Set Magic Rating based on Magic priority (0-6)

### Step 4: Assigning Skills
Based on Skills priority, receive skill points (50, 40, 34, 30, or 27).

1. **Purchase Active Skills**
   - Physical and magical actions (Pistols, Spellcasting, Stealth, etc.)
   - **Skill Point Cost Calculation:**
     - Determine the skill's linked attribute (e.g., Pistols ‚Üí Quickness, Computer ‚Üí Intelligence)
     - Compare skill rating to linked attribute rating:
       - **If skill rating ‚â§ linked attribute:** Cost = 1 point per rating
         - Example: Quickness 5, Pistols Rating 4 ‚Üí Cost = 4 points
       - **If skill rating > linked attribute:** Cost = 2 points per rating
         - Example: Quickness 3, Pistols Rating 5 ‚Üí Cost = 10 points (5 √ó 2)
   - **Note:** Skill costs vary based on attributes, so attribute distribution affects skill point efficiency

2. **Add Specializations**
   - Specializations cost +1 additional point per specialization
   - Can be added to active skills

3. **Purchase Knowledge Skills**
   - Academic, Street, Professional, Technical knowledge
   - Same cost calculation rules as active skills (based on linked attribute, typically Intelligence or Charisma)

4. **Purchase Language Skills**
   - Language skills represent the ability to understand and speak spoken and written language
   - **Free Native Language:** Characters receive one language at rating 6 for free (typically English)
   - **Language Skill Points:** Available language skill points = Intelligence √ó 1.5 (rounded down)
   - **Separate from Regular Skill Points:** Language skill points are separate from active/knowledge skill points
   - **Cost Calculation:** Same as other skills (1 point per rating if ‚â§ Intelligence, 2 points per rating if > Intelligence)
   - **Linked Attribute:** All language skills link to Intelligence
   - **Understanding Complexity:** When reading/listening, language skill rating determines what can be understood:
     - Rating 2: Universal concepts (hunger, fear, bodily functions)
     - Rating 4: Basic conversation (concerns of daily life)
     - Rating 6: Complex subjects (special/limited-interest topics)
     - Rating 9: Intricate subjects (almost any technical subject)
     - Rating 11: Obscure subjects (deeply technical/rare knowledge)
     - Speaking lingo/variation: +2 to target number

5. Record skill ratings and track remaining skill points

### Step 5: Assigning Resources
Based on Resources priority, receive starting nuyen (1,000,000¬•, 400,000¬•, 90,000¬•, 20,000¬•, or 5,000¬•).

Resources are spent in the following order:

1. **Magical Gear and Spells** (if applicable)
   - **For Full Magicians (Priority A):**
     - Spend spell points: 1 point per spell Force
     - Can bond foci: 1 spell point per Force rating (instead of nuyen)
     - Can purchase additional spell points: 25,000¬• per point (max 50 total spell points)
     - Can begin with conjured spirits: 1 spell point per Force + 2 spell points per service
     - Limitations:
       - No spells, foci, or spirits higher than Force 6
       - Cannot begin with more than 6 spirits
       - Cannot begin with any spirit owning more than 6 services
   - **For Aspected Magicians (Priority B):**
     - Spend spell points: 1 point per spell Force
     - Can purchase additional spell points: 25,000¬• per point (max 50 total spell points)
   - **For Adepts (Priority B):**
     - Receive 25 Power Points at creation
     - Spend Power Points on adept powers (not nuyen)
     - **Power Types:**
       - **Fixed Cost Powers:** Pay listed Power Point cost (e.g., Astral Perception: 2 Power Points)
       - **Per-Level Powers:** Pay cost √ó level (e.g., Attribute Boost: 0.25 per level)
     - **Available Powers:**
       - Astral Perception (2 Power Points) - Can perceive the astral plane
       - Attribute Boost (0.25 Power Points per level) - Boosts an attribute (must specify which attribute)
     - More adept powers can be added as they are provided
   - Purchase magical gear (foci, ritual materials, etc.) with nuyen

2. **Cyberware**
   - Purchase cyberware with nuyen
   - Each cyberware item has:
     - Cost (in nuyen)
     - Essence cost (reduces Essence from starting 6.0)
     - Availability rating (may need dice rolls to acquire)
   - When installing cyberware:
     - Deduct nuyen cost
     - Subtract Essence cost from current Essence
     - **Cannot go below 0.1 Essence** (essence of 0 = death)
   - Bioware costs nuyen but doesn't reduce Essence (much more expensive)

3. **Contacts**
   - **Free Contacts:** Characters begin with two free level 1 contacts
   - **Contact Levels:**
     - Level 1 (Contact): 5,000¬•
     - Level 2 (Buddy): 10,000¬•
     - Level 3 (Friend for life): 200,000¬•
   - **Loyalty Rating:** 1-3 (not 1-6)
   - **Contact Types:** Fixer, Dealer, Street Doc, Bartender, Information Broker, Corporate, Gang, Law Enforcement, Media, Smuggler, Talislegger, General
   - **No Limits:** No limits on starting contacts
   - Each contact has:
     - Name
     - Type (contact type)
     - Level (1-3)
     - Loyalty rating (1-3)

4. **Lifestyle**
   - Choose starting lifestyle (monthly cost):
     - Street: 0¬•
     - Squatter: 100¬•
     - Low: 1,000¬•
     - Middle: 5,000¬•
     - High: 10,000¬•
     - Luxury: 100,000¬•
   - Lifestyle is a monthly recurring cost
   - Lifestyle affects monthly expenses and quality of life

5. **Other Equipment**
   - Purchase weapons, armor, bioware, gear, vehicles
   - Each item has:
     - Cost (in nuyen)
     - Availability rating (may need dice rolls to acquire)
     - Essence cost (for cyberware/bioware)
   - Track remaining nuyen

### Step 6: Final Details
1. Calculate final Essence (6.0 minus all cyberware costs)
   - Essence cannot be lowered to 0 or less (essence of 0 = death)
   - Verify Essence is at least 0.1
2. Calculate final Reaction: (Quickness + Intelligence) √∑ 2, rounded down
3. Verify all priorities used correctly (A-E each used once)
4. Verify attribute minimums (no Physical or Mental attribute < 1)
5. Verify attribute maximums respected (no attribute > 6 before modifiers, then check metatype max)
6. Verify skill points spent correctly (consider linked attribute costs)
7. Verify spell points/Power Points spent correctly (if magical)
8. Verify nuyen spent (can't go negative, track remaining nuyen)
9. Set starting values:
   - **Karma**: 0 (starting karma)
   - **Total Karma**: 0 (lifetime karma earned)
   - **Reputation**: All categories start at 0
     - Street Cred: 0
     - Notoriety: 0
     - Public Awareness: 0
   - **Initiation Grade**: 0 (for magical characters)
10. Add character name, background, notes

## Character Creation Order Summary

**Prerequisites:** Assign priorities (A-E) to Magic, Race, Attributes, Skills, and Resources. Each priority used only once.

1. **Choose Race** (Metatype)
   - Based on Race priority (A/B: All, C: Troll/Elf, D: Dwarf/Ork, E: Human)
   - Apply racial attribute modifiers

2. **Choose Magical Abilities**
   - Priority A: Full Magician (Mage/Shaman)
   - Priority B: Adept or Aspected Magician (Mage/Shaman)
   - Priority C/D/E: Mundane

3. **Choose Attributes** (Distribute Attribute Points)
   - Based on Attributes priority (30, 27, 24, 21, or 18 points)
   - Distribute among Body, Quickness, Strength, Charisma, Intelligence, Willpower
   - Apply racial modifiers

4. **Assigning Skills**
   - Purchase active skills
   - Add specializations
   - Purchase knowledge skills
   - Purchase language skills

5. **Assigning Resources** (Spend Nuyen)
   - Magical Gear and Spells (if applicable)
   - Cyberware (and bioware)
   - Contacts
   - Lifestyle
   - Other equipment (weapons, armor, gear, vehicles)

## Special Cases

### Adepts
- Magical characters who enhance physical abilities
- Use Magic priority but spend Power Points instead of Spell Points
- Cannot cast spells but have physical enhancements

### Technomancers/Deckers
- Matrix-focused characters
- Cyberdeck attributes: Response, System, Firewall
- Matrix programs (Attack, Browse, Decrypt, etc.)
- Hacking skills are critical

### Magic Traditions
- **Hermetic**: Academic, structured magic
- **Shaman**: Spirit-based, nature-oriented magic
- Other traditions may exist (varies by sourcebooks)
