# Reference Persona: The Experienced Gamemaster

**Name:** Marcus "Fixer" Delacroix
**Role:** Gamemaster
**Experience:** 10+ years running Shadowrun 5e. Has run one-shots, short runs, and full campaigns.

## Background

Marcus knows the SR5 rulebook well enough to not need it open during play. He builds runs with layered complications and cares deeply that his tools don't break immersion or slow the table down. He will immediately notice if ShadowMaster misrepresents a rule, skips a validation, or produces a character that couldn't exist at a legitimate table.

## Primary Goals

- Build and manage campaigns without re-entering data that already exists in the system
- Create and manage NPCs quickly during session prep
- Configure which sourcebooks and optional rules are active per campaign
- Trust that player characters have been validated correctly against SR5 rules before play
- Run sessions without opening the physical rulebook
- Build one-shots fast using archetypes or quick-build options

## Key Pain Points

- Tools that allow invalid characters (wrong attribute caps, availability violations, miscalculated Essence)
- No control over which sourcebooks are active at his table
- No GM-side view showing all player characters at a glance
- Character sheets that don't surface initiative, condition monitors, and dice pools during play
- Slow NPC creation that makes prep feel like a second job

## Most Frequent Workflows

1. Pre-session: Review and approve player characters before the run
2. Pre-session: Create 2-5 NPCs (contacts, enemies, opposition)
3. Session: Track initiative, condition monitors, and ammo across the table
4. Session: Quick-reference a rule or modifier without leaving the current view
5. Post-session: Award Karma and nuyen, note gear and lifestyle changes
6. Campaign setup: Configure sourcebook access and optional rules

## Definition of Success

Marcus runs a full session without opening the physical book. Every player character was valid before play started. NPCs took under 10 minutes to build. Combat moved fast because condition monitors and dice pools were always visible.

## SR5-Specific Expectations

- Physical and Stun damage tracks are separate and tracked independently
- Availability limits are enforced at character creation and flagged at GM approval
- Essence loss reduces Magic and Resonance maximums, including fractional edge cases
- Action types (Simple, Complex, Free) and initiative passes are represented correctly
- Physical, Mental, and Social limits are applied to dice pool results, not ignored
